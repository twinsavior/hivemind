# Managed Payments — Complete Seller Guide

## Overview

eBay Managed Payments is eBay's integrated payment processing system. All sellers on eBay are required to use Managed Payments. eBay handles the full transaction — collecting payment from the buyer, deducting fees, and disbursing the remainder to the seller's bank account or Payoneer account.

---

## How Managed Payments Works

### Transaction Flow

1. **Buyer pays** using any supported method (credit card, debit card, Apple Pay, Google Pay, PayPal, eBay gift card).
2. **eBay collects the payment** and holds it temporarily.
3. **eBay deducts fees** (final value fee, promoted listings fees, shipping label costs, etc.).
4. **Net proceeds are added** to your Available balance.
5. **Payout is initiated** according to your payout schedule.
6. **Funds arrive** in your bank account (typically 1–3 business days after payout initiation).

### Buyer Payment Methods

Sellers do not need to configure anything — eBay handles all payment methods automatically:

| Payment Method | Available | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Credit card (Visa, MC, Amex, Discover) | Yes | Most common |
| Debit card | Yes | Processed same as credit |
| Apple Pay | Yes | Mobile purchases |
| Google Pay | Yes | Mobile purchases |
| PayPal | Yes | Buyer chooses PayPal at checkout |
| eBay gift cards | Yes | Applied at checkout |
| eBay Bucks | Yes | Loyalty program credits |
| Afterpay / eBay installments | Yes | Buy-now-pay-later option |

> **Key point:** Even though buyers can pay with PayPal, you do NOT need a PayPal account. eBay collects PayPal payments and sends your proceeds to your bank account.

## Payout Schedule

### Payout Frequency Options

**Navigation:** Seller Hub > Payments > Payment settings (or My eBay > Account > Payment settings)

| Schedule | How It Works | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Daily | Payout initiated every day you have available funds | High-volume sellers who want cash flow |
| Weekly | Payout initiated once per week on your chosen day | Moderate-volume sellers |
| Biweekly | Payout initiated every two weeks | Low-volume sellers |

### Payout Timing

| Event | Timeline |
|---|---|
| Order paid by buyer | Funds enter "Processing" status |
| Processing to Available | 1–2 business days after buyer pays (longer for new sellers) |
| Available to Payout initiated | Next scheduled payout day |
| Payout to bank deposit | 1–3 business days (varies by bank) |

**Total time from sale to bank deposit:** Typically 2–5 business days for established sellers.

### Payout Minimum

- Minimum payout amount: $0.01 (no minimum).
- If your available balance is $0 or negative (due to refunds exceeding sales), no payout is issued.

### Negative Balance

If refunds, chargebacks, or fees exceed your sales, your balance goes negative. eBay will:
1. Deduct from future payouts until the balance is recovered.
2. If the balance remains negative for an extended period, eBay may charge your on-file payment method.

## Payout Methods

### Bank Account (US Sellers)

- **Type:** Checking or savings account.
- **Setup:** Provide routing number and account number.
- **Processing time:** 1–3 business days after payout initiation.
- **Verification:** eBay may make small test deposits to verify your account.

### Payoneer (International Sellers)

- **For:** Sellers who do not have a bank account in their eBay marketplace's country.
- **Setup:** Create a Payoneer account and link it to your eBay account.
- **Fees:** Payoneer may charge currency conversion and withdrawal fees.
- **Processing time:** 1–3 business days to Payoneer; additional time for withdrawal from Payoneer to your local bank.

### Changing Your Payout Method

**Navigation:** Seller Hub > Payments > Payment settings > Payout method > Change

- You can switch between bank accounts.
- There may be a brief hold period (up to 3 days) when changing payout accounts for security.
- You can have only one active payout destination at a time.

## Payment Holds

### Why Funds Are Held

eBay places temporary holds on funds for several reasons:

| Hold Reason | Duration | How to Release |
|---|---|---|
| New seller (less than 90 days or under 25 transactions) | Up to 21 days | Build selling history; upload tracking |
| Tracking not uploaded | Until tracking shows delivered | Upload valid tracking with carrier scan |
| Buyer opens a case or return | Until case/return is resolved | Resolve the case or return |
| High-risk item category | Up to 7 days after delivery confirmed | Upload tracking; wait for delivery confirmation |
| Unusual account activity | Until eBay review completes | Contact seller support if hold persists |
| Payment dispute (chargeback) | Until dispute is resolved (up to 90 days) | Provide evidence; await resolution |
| eBay policy violation | Until review completes | Address the violation; contact support |

### New Seller Holds

New sellers (under 90 days of selling or fewer than 25 transactions) typically experience:
- Funds held until tracking shows delivery.
- Maximum hold: 21 days from the payment date if no tracking is uploaded.
- Holds are released faster when you:
  - Upload tracking promptly.
  - Use eBay shipping labels (tracking auto-uploads).
  - Build positive feedback.
  - Maintain good seller metrics.

### How to Minimize Holds

1. **Always upload tracking within your handling time.**
2. **Use tracked shipping methods** for every shipment.
3. **Ship promptly** — faster delivery = faster fund release.
4. **Maintain good metrics** — sellers with consistent performance get fewer holds.
5. **Buy eBay shipping labels** — tracking is linked to the order automatically.

## Payment Disputes (Chargebacks)

### What Is a Payment Dispute

A payment dispute (chargeback) occurs when a buyer contacts their bank or credit card company to reverse a charge, bypassing eBay's return/refund system entirely.

### Common Dispute Reasons

| Reason | Description |
|---|---|
| Unauthorized transaction | Buyer claims they did not make the purchase |
| Item not received | Buyer claims they never received the item |
| Item not as described | Buyer claims the item was significantly different from the listing |
| Duplicate charge | Buyer was charged twice |

### Dispute Process

**Step 1: Notification.**
- eBay notifies you of the dispute via email and in Seller Hub > Payments > Payment disputes.
- The disputed amount is put on hold.

**Step 2: Respond within the deadline.**
- You have **5 calendar days** to respond with evidence.
- If you do not respond, the dispute is decided in the buyer's favor.

**Step 3: Provide evidence.**

| Evidence Type | When to Include |
|---|---|
| Tracking with delivery confirmation | Always (most important evidence) |
| Signature confirmation | Orders over $750 |
| Screenshots of buyer communication | When buyer acknowledged receipt or asked for cancellation |
| Proof of item description accuracy | "Not as described" disputes |
| Photos of item condition and packaging | "Not as described" disputes |
| Invoice or receipt | Showing item authenticity |

**Step 4: eBay submits evidence to the buyer's bank.**

**Step 5: Bank makes a decision.**
- Timeline: 30–75 days from the dispute filing.
- If decided in your favor: funds are released.
- If decided against you: funds are taken from your account.

### Dispute Fees

- eBay charges a **$20 dispute fee** for each payment dispute filed.
- The fee is waived if:
  - The dispute is decided in your favor.
  - You are a Top Rated Seller.
  - eBay determines the dispute was due to eBay's error.

### Seller Protection for Payment Disputes

You are protected from payment disputes when ALL of the following are true:
- Tracking shows delivery to the address on file.
- Signature confirmation is provided for items over $750.
- The item was shipped to the address provided in the eBay order (not an alternate address).
- The item matches the listing description.

## Reserve Amounts

### What Is a Reserve

eBay may hold a percentage of your funds as a reserve if your account is considered higher risk.

### When Reserves Are Applied

| Trigger | Typical Reserve |
|---|---|
| New seller with rapid sales volume growth | 5%–25% of sales |
| High return or refund rate | 10%–30% of sales |
| Below Standard seller level | 5%–15% of sales |
| Active payment disputes | Variable |
| Account restriction or appeal in progress | Up to 100% |

### How to Reduce or Remove Reserves

- Maintain Above Standard or Top Rated seller level.
- Keep return and refund rates low.
- Build consistent selling history.
- Resolve any open cases or disputes quickly.
- Reserves are reviewed periodically and may be lowered or removed automatically.

## Tax Reporting (1099-K)

### Who Receives a 1099-K

Under current US tax law (as of 2026):
- eBay issues a 1099-K form if your gross sales exceed **$600** in a calendar year.
- The 1099-K reports **gross sales** (total amount buyers paid), NOT your profit.
- The form is sent to both you and the IRS.

### What the 1099-K Includes

| Included | Not Included |
|---|---|
| Item sale prices | Your cost of goods sold |
| Shipping charges collected from buyers | Shipping you paid |
| Sales tax collected (reported separately) | eBay fees |
| Returns that were later refunded (as adjustments) | Your actual profit |

### Accessing Your 1099-K

**Navigation:** Seller Hub > Payments > Reporting > Tax documents

- Available by January 31 for the previous tax year.
- Downloadable as PDF.
- Also accessible through your tax transcript from the IRS.

### Tax Considerations

- The 1099-K amount will be higher than your actual income because it includes COGS, fees, shipping costs, and refunds.
- Keep records of all expenses to offset the gross amount on your tax return.
- Consult a tax professional for your specific situation — this guide does not constitute tax advice.

## Fees Deducted from Payouts

### Fee Types

| Fee Type | Description | When Charged |
|---|---|---|
| Final value fee (FVF) | Percentage of total sale amount (item + shipping) | On each sale |
| Per-order fee | Fixed fee per order (currently $0.30) | On each sale |
| International fee | Additional percentage for cross-border sales | On international sales |
| Below Standard surcharge | +4% for Below Standard sellers | If Below Standard |
| Promoted Listings Standard fee | Ad rate percentage of sale price | When promoted item sells |
| Promoted Listings Advanced fee | Cost-per-click charges | When buyer clicks ad |
| Shipping label cost | Postage purchased through eBay | When label is purchased |
| Dispute fee | $20 per payment dispute | When chargeback is filed |
| Refund credit | Portion of FVF refunded when you refund a buyer | On refunds (credit to you) |

### Understanding Fee Deductions

Fees are deducted from your available balance in real-time, not charged separately. For each sale:

```
Sale price + shipping collected by buyer = Gross amount
- Final value fee (% of gross)
- Per-order fee ($0.30)
- International fee (if applicable)
- Promoted Listings fee (if applicable)
= Net to your Available balance
```

### Checking Fee Breakdowns

**Navigation:** Seller Hub > Payments > All transactions > Click any sale

Each transaction shows an itemized fee breakdown with the exact dollar amount for each fee type.

### Monthly Fee Summary

**Navigation:** Seller Hub > Payments > Reporting > Monthly statement

Shows total fees paid by category for the month, useful for bookkeeping and tax preparation.

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Cause | Resolution |
|---|---|---|
| Payout not received | Bank processing delay | Allow 3 business days; check bank account info is correct |
| Funds showing "On hold" | Missing tracking or new seller hold | Upload tracking; contact support if hold is unexpected |
| Negative balance | Refunds exceed recent sales | Will be recovered from future sales |
| Payout amount seems low | Fees, refunds, or holds deducted | Check All Transactions for detailed breakdown |
| Cannot change bank account | Security hold after recent change | Wait 72 hours; verify identity if prompted |
| 1099-K amount too high | Includes gross sales, not net profit | Track expenses for tax deduction purposes |
| Dispute fee charged despite winning | Fee was charged at filing; credit should follow | Check if credit was applied; contact support if not |
