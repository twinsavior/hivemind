# Amazon Seller Policies & General Information — Overview

## Overview

This guide covers the foundational information every Amazon seller needs: account types, fee structures, getting started process, and general selling policies.

---

## Selling Plans

Amazon offers two selling plans:

| Feature | Individual Plan | Professional Plan |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Monthly fee** | None | $39.99/month |
| **Per-item fee** | $0.99 per unit sold | None |
| **Best for** | Sellers moving fewer than 40 items/month | Serious sellers, all advanced features |
| **Advertising access** | No | Yes |
| **Brand Registry** | No | Yes |
| **Reports & analytics** | Limited | Full access |
| **API access** | No | Yes |
| **Eligible for Buy Box** | Limited | Yes |
| **Bulk listing tools** | No | Yes |

**Recommendation:** Most sellers should start with the Professional plan. At 40+ units per month, the Professional plan is cheaper than paying $0.99 per item.

## Fee Structure

### Selling Fees

| Fee Type | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Subscription fee** | $39.99/month (Professional) or $0.99/item (Individual) |
| **Referral fee** | Percentage of total price per item sold; varies by category (typically 6-45%, most commonly 15%). Minimum referral fee of $0.30 in some categories. |
| **Closing fee** | Applies to media categories (books, DVDs, etc.) — $1.80 per item |

### FBA Fees (if using Fulfillment by Amazon)

| Fee Type | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Fulfillment fee** | Per-unit fee based on size and weight |
| **Monthly storage fee** | Per-cubic-foot charge; higher during Q4 (Oct-Dec) |
| **Long-term storage fee** | Additional charge for inventory stored 181+ days |
| **Removal/disposal fee** | Per-unit charge to return or dispose of FBA inventory |
| **Returns processing fee** | Applies to certain categories (e.g., apparel) |

### Additional Potential Fees

- **High-volume listing fee** — For sellers with very large catalogs.
- **Rental book service fee** — For textbook rentals.
- **Advertising costs** — CPC-based; separate from selling fees.

Use Amazon's **Revenue Calculator** to estimate fees for specific products before listing.

## Registration Requirements

To create a selling account, you need:

- **Government-issued ID** (passport, driver's license).
- **Phone number and email address.**
- **Internationally chargeable credit card.**
- **Bank account and routing number** (for receiving payments).
- **Business license or registration** (if applicable).
- **Proof of residential address** (document dated within last 180 days).
- **Tax information** (SSN or EIN for US sellers).

### Registration Steps

1. Provide business information (business type, name, address).
2. Provide seller personal details.
3. Enter billing information.
4. Set up store and product information.
5. Complete identity verification (may require photo or video call).

## How Selling on Amazon Works

### The Basics

- More than **60% of sales** in the Amazon store come from independent (third-party) sellers.
- Most sellers are small to medium-sized businesses.
- Independent sellers in the US averaged more than **$290,000 in annual sales** in 2024.

### Core Process

1. **Create your account** — Choose Individual or Professional plan.
2. **List your products** — Match existing product pages or create new catalog entries. Can be done individually or in bulk.
3. **Set your pricing** — Competitive pricing is essential for the Featured Offer (Buy Box).
4. **Choose fulfillment method** — FBM (you ship) or FBA (Amazon ships).
5. **Manage orders** — Process and confirm orders through Seller Central.
6. **Get paid** — Amazon deposits funds via ACH transfer to your registered bank account.

### Payment Timeline

- Amazon initiates payments on a regular settlement cycle (typically every 14 days).
- It can take up to **5 business days** for money to appear in your bank account after Amazon initiates payment.
- Only bank accounts can receive deposits — credit cards and online payment systems are not accepted.

## Fulfillment Options

### Fulfilled by Merchant (FBM)

You handle storage, packing, shipping, and customer service.

**Pros:** More control, lower fees, better for slow-moving or oversized items.
**Cons:** More work, must meet Amazon's performance standards.

### Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA)

Amazon stores, picks, packs, and ships your products. Amazon also handles returns and customer service for FBA orders.

**Pros:** Prime eligibility, hands-off fulfillment, better Buy Box chances.
**Cons:** FBA fees, less control over packaging and branding, inventory storage costs.

Both methods can be used simultaneously — many sellers use a hybrid approach.

## Listing Products

### Two Approaches

1. **Match an existing product** — If your product already exists on Amazon, you add your offer to the existing product page. No new listing creation needed.
2. **Create a new product listing** — If your product is new to Amazon's catalog, you create a new product detail page.

### Required Listing Information

- Product title.
- Brand name.
- Product description and bullet points.
- Product images (main image + supporting images).
- UPC/EAN/ISBN (product identifier).
- Search terms (backend keywords).
- Category selection.
- Price and condition.
- Quantity available.

## Seller Performance Standards

Amazon monitors several performance metrics to maintain marketplace quality:

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| **Order Defect Rate (ODR)** | Below 1% |
| **Late Shipment Rate** | Below 4% |
| **Pre-Fulfillment Cancel Rate** | Below 2.5% |
| **Valid Tracking Rate** | Above 95% |
| **On-Time Delivery Rate** | Above 97% |

Falling below these thresholds can result in warnings, loss of Buy Box eligibility, or account suspension.

## New Seller Incentives

Amazon offers incentives for new sellers during their first 90 days:

- **10% back** on first $50,000 in branded sales (requires Brand Registry).
- **5% back** through year one up to $1,000,000 in branded sales.
- **$200 Vine credit** for product reviews.
- **Up to $1,000** in advertising credits for Sponsored Products.
- **$50 coupon credit.**
- New sellers using the New Seller Guide generate approximately **6x more first-year sales** on average.

## Self-Publishing (Books)

Amazon offers **Kindle Direct Publishing (KDP)** for authors wanting to self-publish:
- Publish titles in **24-48 hours**.
- Available as ebooks and print-on-demand paperbacks.
- Separate from the standard seller platform.
