# eBay Seller Account Performance Guide

> Complete reference for seller levels, service metrics, evaluation cycles, and how to maintain or regain good standing.

---

## Seller Level Overview

eBay evaluates every seller on a quarterly basis and assigns one of four seller levels. Your level directly affects your visibility in search results, fee rates, and access to seller protections.

| Seller Level | Requirements | Key Benefits |
|---|---|---|
| **Below Standard** | Fails one or more minimum performance standards | Restricted selling privileges, 6% fee surcharge, reduced search visibility |
| **Above Standard** | Meets all minimum performance standards | Normal selling privileges, standard fees |
| **Top Rated Seller (TRS)** | Exceeds performance standards + 100 transactions + $1,000 sales in 12 months | 10% final value fee discount (with TRS Plus), priority search placement, faster payouts |
| **Top Rated Plus** | Top Rated Seller + 1-day handling + 30-day free returns on listing | Per-listing badge, 10% FVF discount on that listing, highest search boost |

---

## Evaluation Schedule

eBay evaluates seller performance on the **20th of every month**, but official level changes happen on a **quarterly cycle**.

| Evaluation Period | Level Effective Date |
|---|---|
| March 20 evaluation | April 1 |
| June 20 evaluation | July 1 |
| September 20 evaluation | October 1 |
| December 20 evaluation | January 1 |

### How the Lookback Period Works

- **Transaction defect rate**: Evaluated over the most recent **12-month period**
- **Late shipment rate**: Evaluated over the most recent **3-month period** (the "recent" evaluation) OR the 12-month period — whichever is worse for the seller is used
- **Cases closed without resolution**: 12-month period
- **Tracking upload rate**: Most recent 3-month period

> **Important**: eBay uses the evaluation date, not the sale date. A defect from a case opened 11 months ago still counts in the 12-month window until it ages out.

## Service Metrics: Detailed Breakdown

### 1. Transaction Defect Rate (TDR)

The transaction defect rate is the percentage of transactions that received one or more "defects." This is the single most impactful metric for your seller level.

**What counts as a defect:**
- Seller-cancelled transaction (reason: out of stock or unable to fulfill)
- Case closed without seller resolution (eBay or PayPal Money Back Guarantee case)
- Late shipment with valid tracking confirming late delivery (less common)

**What does NOT count as a defect:**
- Buyer-requested cancellation
- Return request handled within policy
- Case closed in seller's favor
- Neutral or negative feedback (feedback does not create defects)
- Late shipment alone without a case being opened

| Seller Level | Transaction Defect Rate Requirement |
|---|---|
| Top Rated | Less than or equal to 0.5% |
| Above Standard | Less than or equal to 2.0% |
| Below Standard | Greater than 2.0% |

**Example calculation**: If you had 500 transactions in 12 months and 8 defects, your TDR = 8/500 = 1.6%. You would be Above Standard but not qualify for Top Rated.

### 2. Cases Closed Without Seller Resolution

This measures how often a buyer escalates a case to eBay (Money Back Guarantee, PayPal claim) and eBay closes it without you resolving it first.

| Seller Level | Rate Requirement | Count Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Top Rated | ≤ 0.3% AND | ≤ 2 cases |
| Above Standard | ≤ 0.3% OR | ≤ 2 cases |
| Below Standard | > 0.3% AND | > 2 cases |

> **Key distinction**: Top Rated requires you to meet BOTH the rate AND the count. Above Standard requires you to meet EITHER the rate OR the count.

### 3. Late Shipment Rate

Measures the percentage of transactions where tracking shows the item was shipped after the stated handling time.

| Seller Level | Late Shipment Rate |
|---|---|
| Top Rated | ≤ 3.0% |
| Above Standard | ≤ 7.0% |
| Below Standard | > 7.0% |

**How eBay measures "late":**
1. Your handling time starts when the buyer pays
2. eBay checks the first tracking scan date against your stated handling time
3. If the first scan is after the handling deadline, it counts as late
4. Business days only — weekends and eBay-recognized holidays are excluded

### 4. Tracking Upload Rate

Percentage of transactions where valid tracking was uploaded within the handling time window.

| Seller Level | Tracking Upload Rate |
|---|---|
| Top Rated | ≥ 95% |
| Above Standard | No minimum (but affects search) |
| Below Standard | Not a factor for Below Standard determination |

> **Note**: This metric is required specifically for Top Rated status. However, even Above Standard sellers benefit from high tracking upload rates through improved search visibility.

## Additional Requirements for Top Rated Seller

Beyond the service metrics above, Top Rated Seller status requires:

| Requirement | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Active eBay account | At least 90 days |
| US-based transactions in 12 months | At least 100 transactions |
| US-based sales volume in 12 months | At least $1,000 |
| Compliance with eBay policies | No serious or repeated policy violations |

## Top Rated Plus: Per-Listing Badge

Top Rated Plus is not an account-level status — it is a **per-listing designation** applied to individual listings that meet additional criteria:

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Account must be Top Rated Seller | All TRS requirements met |
| Handling time | Same-day or 1 business day |
| Return policy | 30-day (or longer) free returns accepted |
| Listing must have tracking uploaded | On all recent transactions for that listing |

**Benefits of TRS Plus (per qualifying listing):**
- 10% discount on the final value fee for that listing
- TRS Plus badge displayed on the listing
- Highest priority in Cassini search results
- Increased buyer confidence and conversion rate

## Below Standard: Consequences and Recovery

### What Happens When You Drop to Below Standard

| Consequence | Details |
|---|---|
| **Fee surcharge** | Additional 6% added to final value fees on all sales |
| **Search suppression** | Listings are demoted in search results significantly |
| **Selling restrictions** | May lose access to certain seller features |
| **Promoted Listings** | May be restricted from Promoted Listings Standard |
| **Payment holds** | Funds may be held for longer periods |
| **Listing limits** | May have listing limits reduced |
| **Risk of suspension** | Continued Below Standard performance may lead to account restrictions or suspension |

### How to Recover from Below Standard

1. **Check your current metrics**: Go to Seller Hub > Performance > Service Metrics
2. **Identify which metric(s) failed**: Focus on the metric that pushed you Below Standard
3. **Address root causes**:
   - High TDR: Improve inventory accuracy, stop cancelling orders
   - High case rate: Respond to all cases within 3 business days, offer full refunds proactively
   - High late shipment rate: Reduce handling time or ship faster
   - Low tracking upload: Upload tracking same day you ship
4. **Wait for re-evaluation**: Your level updates on the next quarterly evaluation date
5. **Volume strategy**: Increase your successful transaction volume to dilute the defect percentage

> **Recovery timeline**: Even if you fix all issues immediately, you must wait until the next quarterly evaluation. The 12-month lookback means old defects take time to age out.

## How to Check Your Performance

### Navigation Path
```
Seller Hub > Performance > Service Metrics
```

### What You'll See
- Current seller level and next evaluation date
- Transaction defect rate (with breakdown of defect types)
- Cases closed without resolution (count and rate)
- Late shipment rate
- Tracking upload rate
- Projected status for next evaluation (if available)

### Detailed Defect Review
```
Seller Hub > Performance > Service Metrics > [Click any metric] > View Details
```

This shows individual transactions that contributed to each metric, allowing you to identify patterns and dispute incorrect defects.

## Disputing Defects

You can request removal of defects in certain situations:

| Situation | How to Dispute |
|---|---|
| Buyer admitted fault in messages | File a defect removal request through Seller Hub |
| Tracking proves on-time delivery but defect recorded | Contact eBay seller support with tracking evidence |
| Case opened after seller already issued refund | File a defect removal request |
| Buyer committed feedback extortion | Report to eBay — defect is automatically removed if confirmed |
| eBay system error caused the defect | Contact seller support with documentation |

### Dispute Path
```
Seller Hub > Performance > Service Metrics > [Click metric] > View Details > Request Removal (on specific transaction)
```

## Seasonal Considerations

- **Q4 (October-December)**: Holiday volume can dilute defect rates if you perform well, but increased volume also means more potential issues
- **January evaluation**: Covers the holiday period — late shipments from holiday rush can hurt
- **New Year recovery**: If you dropped to Below Standard in January, you have until the March 20 evaluation to improve
- **Summer slowdown**: Lower volume means each defect has a larger percentage impact

## Best Practices for Maintaining Top Rated Status

1. **Ship within 1 business day** — meets TRS Plus requirement and minimizes late shipment risk
2. **Upload tracking immediately** — do not wait until end of day
3. **Offer 30-day free returns** — required for TRS Plus and reduces INAD cases
4. **Respond to all cases within 24 hours** — even if you cannot resolve immediately, acknowledge it
5. **Never cancel transactions** — if you must, have the buyer request the cancellation
6. **Monitor metrics weekly** — do not wait for quarterly evaluations to discover problems
7. **Keep inventory accurate** — out-of-stock cancellations are the most common defect source
8. **Use eBay's shipping labels** — tracking is automatically uploaded, eliminating tracking upload issues
9. **Build volume buffer** — more transactions means each defect has less percentage impact
10. **Document everything** — save messages, photos, and tracking info for potential disputes
