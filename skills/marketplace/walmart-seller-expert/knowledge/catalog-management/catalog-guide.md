# Catalog Management on Walmart Marketplace: Complete Guide

## Overview

Once your items are live on Walmart Marketplace, ongoing catalog management is essential for maintaining competitive listings, accurate inventory, and optimal pricing. Walmart Seller Center provides tools for updating content, managing inventory, setting pricing rules, and leveraging automated pricing strategies.

---

## Updating Content

### Individual Item Updates
- Navigate to **Catalog > Items** in Seller Center.
- Select the item you want to update and edit fields directly.
- Changes can include title, description, key features, images, and attributes.
- Items can be saved as drafts before publishing.
- Note: You may not always be able to define which content is displayed if multiple sellers offer the same product -- Walmart determines "best available content."

### Bulk Content Updates
- Use Walmart's Excel templates to update multiple items at once.
- Navigate to **Catalog > Add items > Upload in bulk**.
- Download the appropriate template, make changes, and re-upload.
- Templates support updating product attributes, descriptions, images, and other content fields.
- Updates can appear in as quickly as 15 minutes or may take up to 4 hours.
- Spreadsheet files must not exceed 5MB.

### Managing Drafts
- Items saved as drafts appear in the Draft status in your catalog.
- Drafts are items that have incomplete information or have not yet been published.
- Complete all required fields to move items from Draft to Published status.
- Items remain as Draft until account setup completes, inventory is added, and pricing is set.

---

## Deleting Items

- Bulk item deletion is available through templates in Seller Center.
- Use the deletion template to remove multiple items simultaneously.
- Deleted items are removed from your catalog and will no longer appear on Walmart.com.
- Exercise caution -- deleted items may need to be re-created from scratch.

---

## Updating SKUs

### Individual SKU Updates
- Navigate to the item in Seller Center and update the SKU field.
- Ensure the new SKU is unique within your catalog.

### Bulk SKU Updates
- Download the bulk upload template from Seller Center.
- In the template, fill in the updated SKU and all other required fields and item attributes.
- Locate the column labeled **SKU Update** in the Optional section of the template.
- You must choose **Yes** from the dropdown menu in the SKU Update column for the SKUs to successfully update.
- Upload the completed template.
- Updates can appear in as quickly as 15 minutes or take up to 4 hours.

---

## Updating Product IDs

- Product Identifiers (product IDs) are universally accepted codes unique to every item sold on Walmart.com.
- All items sold on Walmart Marketplace must have a product ID (GTIN/UPC).
- To update a product ID, navigate to the item in Seller Center and edit the product ID field.
- Ensure the new product ID is GS1-verified.
- Bulk product ID updates are available through templates.

## Cross-Listing Internationally

- Walmart supports cross-listing items across international markets.
- Sellers can add additional markets to existing accounts through the Global Onboarding process.
- Cross-listing allows you to reach customers in multiple Walmart markets from a single account.

## Managing Duplicates

- Duplicate listings can occur when multiple sellers list the same product.
- Walmart's system attempts to match duplicate items to a single product page.
- Sellers can manage duplicate items through the Items dashboard in Seller Center.
- The Repricer tool's "Items in Other Strategies" section helps identify duplicates.

## Retiring and Reactivating Items

### Retiring Items
- Items can be retired (unpublished) when you no longer want to sell them.
- Retired items remain in your catalog but are not visible to customers on Walmart.com.
- Useful for seasonal products or items temporarily out of stock.

### Reactivating Items
- Previously retired items can be reactivated by updating their status in Seller Center.
- Ensure inventory and pricing are current before reactivating.
- Reactivated items go through Walmart's review process before becoming visible again.

---

## Inventory Management

### Individual Inventory Updates
- Update inventory quantities for single items directly in Seller Center.
- Navigate to **Catalog > Inventory** and adjust quantities.

### Bulk Inventory Updates
- Use Walmart's inventory template to update multiple items simultaneously.
- Download the template, fill in updated quantities, and upload.
- Supports inventory tracking by fulfillment center location.

### Inventory Tracking by Location
- If you operate multiple fulfillment centers, track inventory by location.
- Set inventory levels for each fulfillment center individually.
- Walmart uses location-based inventory to determine shipping options and delivery dates.

### Best Practices
- Maintain accurate inventory to prevent overselling and cancellations.
- Set inventory buffers to reduce overselling risk.
- Synchronize inventory across all sales channels to avoid stockouts.
- Monitor inventory levels regularly -- a high order cancellation rate (over 2%) can affect account health.

- Price changes are reflected on Walmart.com once processed.

### Bulk Price Updates
- Use Walmart's pricing template to update multiple items simultaneously.
- Download the template, input new prices, and upload.

## Pricing Rules and Policies

### Core Pricing Rule
- Walmart automatically unpublishes offers that are priced significantly higher than prices currently or recently offered for the same item on Walmart.com or competing websites.
- The system reviews both listing price and shipping fees when determining compliance.
- This also applies to potential price gouging situations.

### Automatic Unpublishing
- Offers violating pricing rules receive the reason code "Reasonable Price Not Satisfied."
- Sellers are notified via email and Seller Center.
- Details are visible in the **Unpublished Items** dashboard.

### Republishing
- Once prices are adjusted to comply with rules, Walmart typically republishes offers within **48 hours** automatically.

### Reference Pricing
- The Unpublished Items dashboard provides a reference price calculated using multiple external signals: match price from competitor websites, historical selling price, Buy Box winner price, and average offer price.

### Egregious Shipping Cost Rule
- Extremely high shipping fees can trigger unpublishing or Buy Box ineligibility.
- Shipping costs are measured relative to item price.
- Shipping template uploads exceeding allowed limits are rejected.
- Sellers are limited to **10 shipping cost edits per Shipping Template daily**; exceeding this locks the template for 24 hours.
- Accounts may face suspension for repeated shipping cost violations.

### Buy Box Ineligibility
- Items priced substantially higher than Walmart.com or competitor offerings lose the Add to Cart button and price visibility.
- They remain purchasable only through the "More Seller Options" flow.
- Resold items (pre-owned, restored, open box) become ineligible when priced above corresponding new items.

### Pricing Error Protection
- Walmart may unpublish unintentionally low-priced offers to protect sellers from losses.
- Sellers can submit documentation to demonstrate intentional pricing.

### Enforcement
- Violation of pricing rules or marketplace policies may result in account suspension or termination.

## Buy Box

### What is the Buy Box?
- The Buy Box is the prominent "Add to Cart" button on a product page on Walmart.com.
- When multiple sellers offer the same product, Walmart's algorithm determines which seller wins the Buy Box.
- Winning the Buy Box significantly increases sales, as most customers purchase through it.

### Factors Affecting Buy Box
- **Price**: Lower total price (item + shipping) gives an advantage.
- **Fulfillment method**: WFS items typically have an advantage.
- **Shipping speed**: Faster delivery options improve Buy Box eligibility.
- **Seller performance**: On-time delivery, valid tracking, and low cancellation rates.
- **Stock availability**: In-stock items are preferred.
- **Customer feedback**: Positive reviews and ratings help.

### Buy Box Algorithm (2026)
- The algorithm is increasingly sensitive to pricing and fulfillment.
- Small price changes, shipping speed, and stock availability can shift ownership within minutes.
- Automated pricing tools help maintain competitiveness.

## Repricer Tool

### Overview
- The Repricer is Walmart's automated pricing tool enabling automatic price adjustments for items in your catalog.
- Create custom strategies, assign eligible items, monitor performance, and qualify for Referral Fee discounts.
- The Repricer only adjusts the item price and does not change shipping price.
- Price adjustments typically take effect within **15 minutes to 4 hours**.

### Getting Started
1. Navigate to **Pricing** section in Seller Center.
2. Choose **Automate Pricing**.
3. Click **Create Strategy** at the top right.
4. Choose **Repricer strategy**.
5. Accept the Automate Pricing Terms & Conditions (which authorizes Walmart to adjust your item prices on your behalf).

### Requirements
- Items must have a **minimum and maximum price** entered to be assigned to a strategy.
- Items cannot simultaneously belong to multiple strategies.

### Available Pricing Strategies

1. **Match Competitive Price**: The system matches lower competitive prices after applying seller adjustments.
2. **Adjust Against Competitive Price**: Applies adjustments to whichever is lower -- Buy Box or competitive price.
3. **Don't Compete with Competitive Price**: Only applies adjustments to Buy Box price, ignoring competitive pricing.
4. **Compare with Competitive Price Only**: Applies adjustments exclusively to competitive prices.

### Strategy Management
- **Strategy Preview**: Simulate pricing logic using sample data showing current price, Buy Box price, competitive price, and projected prices.
- **Price History**: Review via the Current Price column.
- **Pause/Delete**: Use the Action column to pause or delete strategies.
- **Handle Missing Data**: Select "Use your last submitted price if there's no comparable price found" to maintain pricing when competitor data is unavailable.

### When Multiple Sellers Use Repricer
- When multiple sellers use Repricer on identical items, the tool auto-adjusts the price for each offer based on each seller's individual strategy settings.

### Item Assignment Tips
- Prioritize high-traffic items and competitively positioned products.
- Items with warning symbols require review -- check for minimum allowed price limitations, missing comparable prices, or promotional ineligibility.
- Use the "Manage Items" function to address issues.

### Referral Fee Benefits
- Sellers using the Repricer can qualify for Referral Fee discounts, incentivizing adoption.

### Available Reports
