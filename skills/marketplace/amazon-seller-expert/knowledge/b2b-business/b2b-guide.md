# Amazon Business (B2B Selling) — Comprehensive Guide

## Overview

Amazon Business is a B2B marketplace that allows sellers to reach business, institutional, and government customers. It provides specialized tools for business pricing, quantity discounts, tax exemptions, and certification-based selling. Business customers often buy in larger quantities and return items less frequently than retail consumers.

**Key stats:**
- **$35 billion** in annualized sales driven by Amazon Business.
- **8 million** organizations served globally.
- **97** Fortune 100 companies shop on the platform.

---

## How Amazon Business Works

Amazon Business is not a separate marketplace — it is a layer on top of the standard Amazon marketplace. Business customers see all the same products as retail consumers, plus they can see additional business-specific pricing and features you set up.

### Key B2B Features

#### 1. Business Pricing & Quantity Discounts

Set special lower prices visible only to verified business customers. Create tiered quantity discounts to incentivize bulk purchases.

**How to set up:**
- Go to Manage Inventory in Seller Central.
- Use the "Business Price" column to set B2B-specific prices.
- Create quantity discount tiers (e.g., 5+ units at 10% off, 20+ units at 20% off).
- Use the Automate Pricing tool for automated price adjustments.

#### 2. Bulk Selection & Package Hierarchies

Create package hierarchies for different quantities — single units, case packs, and pallets — to encourage larger orders. Amazon's tools help identify which products are good candidates for bulk selling.

#### 3. Certifications & Business Profiles

Showcase diversity and quality certifications on your seller profile:
- Small Business.
- Minority-Owned Business.
- Women-Owned Business.
- Veteran-Owned Business.
- ISO certifications.
- Other industry-specific certifications.

Certifications appear throughout the Amazon Business shopping experience and on your seller profile page. Amazon does not assist in obtaining certifications — you must determine eligibility and obtain documentation independently.

#### 4. Tax Exemption Program

Automate tax exemptions on qualified purchases through the Amazon Tax-Exemption Program (ATEP). Business customers with valid tax-exempt status can purchase without sales tax being charged.

#### 5. Quote Management

Review and respond to quote requests from business customers using the Manage Quotes tool. Business buyers can request custom pricing for large orders.

#### 6. Lower Referral Fees

Sellers receive reduced referral fees on larger B2B purchases compared to standard retail referral fees.

## Getting Started with Amazon Business

### Requirements

- **Professional selling account** ($39.99/month plus selling fees).
- Individual account holders can upgrade to Professional anytime.

### Five-Step Setup Process

**Step 1:** Sign up for a Professional account or log into your existing Seller Central account. Access **B2B Central** from the main menu.

**Step 2:** Add B2B products to your catalog using standard listing processes. Use the **B2B Product Opportunities tool** for recommendations on which products to prioritize.

**Step 3:** Set business prices and quantity discounts via the Manage Inventory page's "Business Price" column, or create automated pricing rules.

**Step 4:** Add certifications and complete your business profile with company information.

**Step 5:** Explore additional B2B Central tools including quote management, case pack opportunities, and discount insights.

## Fulfillment for B2B Orders

Business orders can be fulfilled using the same methods as retail orders:

| Method | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Fulfilled by Merchant (FBM)** | Ship directly from your warehouse |
| **Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA)** | Uses the same inventory pool as retail orders — no separate inventory needed |
| **Hybrid** | Mix FBA and FBM on a per-product basis |

## B2B Automation

Use **Amazon Business APIs** (part of the Selling Partner API) to automate:
- Business pricing and quantity discount updates.
- Real-time inventory synchronization across locations.
- Package hierarchy creation.
- Business hour delivery optimization.
