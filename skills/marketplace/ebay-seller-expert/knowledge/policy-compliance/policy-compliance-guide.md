# Policy Compliance — Complete eBay Seller Guide

## Overview

eBay enforces policies to maintain marketplace trust and legal compliance. Violations can result in listing removals, account restrictions, fee surcharges, or permanent suspension. Understanding these policies is essential to protecting your business.

---

## VeRO — Verified Rights Owner Program

### What VeRO Is

VeRO is eBay's intellectual property enforcement program. Brand owners and rights holders register with eBay and can request removal of listings they believe infringe their trademarks, copyrights, or patents.

### How VeRO Works

1. A rights holder files a complaint with eBay claiming your listing infringes their IP.
2. eBay removes the listing immediately (within hours).
3. You receive an email notification with the rights holder's name and the reason for removal.
4. A VeRO strike is added to your account.

### Common VeRO Triggers

| Trigger | Example | How to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Trademark infringement | Using "Louis Vuitton" in a listing for a non-LV product | Only use brand names for authentic products you are selling |
| Unauthorized use of images | Using a brand's official product photos | Take your own photos of the actual item |
| Counterfeit goods | Selling replica/fake branded items | Never sell counterfeit products |
| Unauthorized distribution | Selling products a brand restricts from resale on eBay | Research brand restrictions before sourcing |
| Patent claims | Selling a product a patent holder claims infringes | Less common; usually affects manufacturers |

### Brands Known for Aggressive VeRO Enforcement

Many major brands actively police eBay. Commonly enforced brands include (but are not limited to): luxury fashion houses, major electronics brands, sports leagues, entertainment franchises, and some consumer goods brands. Research before listing any branded items from unfamiliar sources.

### How to Handle a VeRO Takedown

**Step 1: Read the removal notice carefully.**
- Identify the specific reason (trademark, copyright, patent).
- Note the rights holder's name and contact information.

**Step 2: Evaluate whether the claim is valid.**
- If you were selling counterfeit goods: accept the removal, do not relist.
- If you were using brand images: accept the removal, relist with your own photos.
- If you believe the claim is invalid (you are selling an authentic item): proceed to Step 3.

**Step 3: Contact the rights holder directly.**
- Use the contact information in the removal notice.
- Explain that you are selling an authentic product.
- Provide proof of authenticity (purchase receipts, supplier invoices, photos).
- Many rights holders will withdraw their claim if satisfied the item is genuine.

**Step 4: File a counter-notice with eBay (if needed).**

### Filing a VeRO Counter-Notice

**Navigation:** The removal email includes a link to the counter-notice form, or go to `pages.ebay.com/seller-center/listing-and-marketing/verified-rights-owner-program.html`

Required information:
- Your eBay user ID.
- The item number(s) that were removed.
- A statement under penalty of perjury that you believe the removal was a mistake.
- Your physical address and phone number.
- Your signature (electronic signature accepted).

After filing:
- eBay forwards your counter-notice to the rights holder.
- The rights holder has 10 business days to file a lawsuit or withdraw.
- If they do not respond, eBay may reinstate your listing.

> **Warning:** Filing a false counter-notice can expose you to legal liability. Only file if you genuinely believe the item is authentic and the claim is invalid.

### VeRO Strike Impact

| Number of Strikes | Consequence |
|---|---|
| 1 | Warning; listing removed |
| 2–3 | Listings removed; potential temporary selling restriction |
| 4+ | Account suspension possible; selling restrictions likely |
| Pattern of violations | Permanent account suspension |

## Prohibited and Restricted Items

### Completely Prohibited on eBay

- Counterfeit goods, replicas, and knockoffs.
- Firearms and certain weapons (varies by jurisdiction).
- Illegal drugs and controlled substances.
- Stolen property.
- Human remains and body parts.
- Live animals (with limited exceptions).
- Hazardous materials (with exceptions for consumer products).
- Surveillance equipment designed for illegal use.
- Lottery tickets and gambling items.
- Embargoed goods.

### Restricted (Allowed with Conditions)

| Category | Restriction |
|---|---|
| Alcohol | Allowed in some categories (collectible containers, etc.) with restrictions |
| Tobacco | Collectible items only; no consumable tobacco |
| Knives and blades | Must comply with local laws; no switchblades |
| Pesticides and chemicals | Must be EPA-registered; proper labeling required |
| Medical devices | Must be FDA-cleared; prescription devices prohibited |
| Recalled products | Cannot be listed if under active recall |
| Adult items | Must be listed in the Adult Only category with appropriate settings |
| Food | Must be commercially packaged; expiration dates must be visible |
| Batteries | Shipping restrictions apply; must comply with hazmat rules |
| Gift cards | Restrictions on denomination and seller requirements |

### How to Check If an Item Is Allowed

1. Search eBay's prohibited and restricted items policy page.
2. Check the specific category rules for the item type.
3. Look for any active product recalls on CPSC.gov.
4. When in doubt, contact eBay seller support before listing.

## Listing Violations

### Keyword Spamming

**Definition:** Adding irrelevant keywords to a listing title or description to attract search traffic.

**Examples of violations:**
- "Nike shoes NOT Adidas NOT Puma NOT Reebok" (brand name stuffing).
- "Like Gucci" or "Gucci style" for non-Gucci products.
- Stuffing item specifics fields with irrelevant terms.
- Adding unrelated category keywords in the description.

**Consequences:** Listing removal, search penalty, account warning.

**Best practice:** Only include keywords that directly and accurately describe your item.

### Miscategorization

**Definition:** Listing an item in the wrong category to gain visibility or avoid fees.

**Examples:**
- Listing a phone case in the "Cell Phones" category instead of "Cases, Covers & Skins."
- Listing an accessory in the main product category.

**Consequences:** Listing removal, search demotion.

### Stock Photo Misuse

**Definition:** Using manufacturer or third-party stock photos when your actual item differs.

**eBay's rules:**
- Stock photos are allowed for new, sealed, manufacturer-packaged items.
- Used or open-box items MUST use your own photos of the actual item.
- You cannot use another seller's photos without permission.
- You cannot use photos from websites you do not own.

**Consequences:** Listing removal, VeRO claim from image owner, increased return rate.

### Other Common Violations

| Violation | Description | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Fee avoidance | Asking buyers to pay outside eBay | Account suspension |
| Contact info in listings | Including phone number, email, or external website | Listing removal |
| Duplicate listings | Multiple identical listings for the same item | Listings removed; possible restriction |
| Misleading titles | Title does not match the item (e.g., "READ DESCRIPTION") | Listing removal; search demotion |
| Shill bidding | Bidding on your own auctions or having associates bid | Account suspension |
| Feedback manipulation | Offering incentives for positive feedback | Account restriction |

## Account Restrictions and Suspensions

### Types of Account Actions

| Action | Severity | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| Warning | Low | Email notification; no immediate impact |
| Listing removal | Low–Medium | Specific listing(s) taken down |
| Selling restriction | Medium | Limited selling capacity (reduced limits) |
| Temporary suspension | High | Cannot sell or buy for a set period (3–30 days typical) |
| Indefinite suspension | Highest | Account locked; must appeal to regain access |
| Permanent suspension | Final | Account terminated; no reinstatement |

### MC011 Notice

**What it is:** A notice that your selling privileges have been restricted or suspended.

**Common reasons:**
- High defect rate (exceeded Below Standard thresholds).
- Policy violations (multiple listing violations).
- Buyer complaints pattern.
- Suspected fraudulent activity.

**What to do:**
1. Read the notice carefully to understand the specific reason.
2. Do NOT create a new account (this will result in permanent suspension of both accounts).
3. Prepare your appeal (see below).

### MC113 Notice

**What it is:** A notice asking you to verify your identity to continue selling.

**Common triggers:**
- New account with rapid scaling.
- Account information changes.
- Suspicious login activity.
- High-value transactions from a new account.

**Required documents (typically):**
- Government-issued photo ID.
- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement).
- Supplier invoices for items you are selling.
- Business license (if applicable).

**How to respond:**
1. Gather requested documents.
2. Reply to the email or use the link provided in the notice.
3. Upload clear, legible copies.
4. Response time: typically 24–72 hours for review.

## How to Appeal Account Restrictions

### Step-by-Step Appeal Process

**Step 1: Understand the reason.**
- Read the restriction notice completely.
- Check Seller Hub > Performance > Service metrics for specific defect data.
- Review recent buyer communications and cases.

**Step 2: Create a Plan of Action (POA).**

Your POA should address three things:

1. **Root cause:** What specifically caused the issue?
   - "I had shipping delays due to [specific reason]."
   - "Several listings had incorrect item specifics."
2. **Corrective action:** What have you already done to fix it?
   - "I have updated my handling time to 2 days to account for processing."
   - "I have revised all listings with accurate item specifics."
3. **Preventive measures:** How will you prevent this from happening again?
   - "I now use automated shipping rules and purchase labels immediately upon sale."
   - "I have implemented a listing review checklist before publishing."

**Step 3: Submit your appeal.**
- **Online:** Seller Hub > Performance > Seller level > Appeal (if link is available).
- **Phone:** Call eBay seller support at the number provided in your notice.
- **Email:** Reply to the restriction notice email with your POA.

**Step 4: Follow up.**
- Allow 48–72 hours for review.
- If denied, you can submit a revised appeal with additional evidence.
- Escalate to a supervisor if initial support is unhelpful.

### Appeal Tips

- Be factual and professional; do not be emotional or accusatory.
- Include specific data (order numbers, tracking numbers, dates).
- Show concrete changes, not just promises.
- Acknowledge mistakes if they occurred.
- Keep the appeal concise (1 page maximum).

## Intellectual Property Policy

### What Constitutes IP Infringement on eBay

| Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Trademark | Using a brand name/logo without authorization | Listing a generic item as "Gucci style" |
| Copyright | Reproducing protected creative works | Using a photographer's images without license |
| Patent | Selling a product that infringes a patent | Manufacturing and selling a patented design |
| Trade dress | Imitating a product's distinctive appearance | Copying unique packaging design |

### Safe Selling Practices

- **First Sale Doctrine:** In the US, you can generally resell authentic, legally obtained branded products. This is your strongest defense for VeRO claims.
- **Use your own photos** for all listings, especially used items.
- **Be accurate** in your titles and descriptions. Do not imply endorsement or authorization.
- **Keep receipts** for all inventory purchases as proof of authenticity.
- **Describe modifications** clearly if you have altered a branded product.

## Product Recall Compliance

### Your Obligations

- You may not list products under an active safety recall.
- eBay automatically blocks some recalled items but not all.
- Monitor CPSC.gov (US), ec.europa.eu/safety-gate (EU) for active recalls.
- If a product you sold is recalled, contact the buyer proactively.

### Checking for Recalls

1. Search CPSC.gov with the product name and model number.
2. Check the manufacturer's website for safety notices.
3. Review eBay's recalled items policy page for eBay-specific blocked items.

## eBay Seller Code of Conduct

### Core Requirements

1. **Honest and accurate listings.** Describe items truthfully; disclose all defects.
2. **Timely shipping.** Ship within your stated handling time.
3. **Professional communication.** Respond to buyers within 24 hours.
4. **Fair returns.** Honor your return policy; do not make returns difficult.
5. **No marketplace manipulation.** No shill bidding, fee avoidance, or feedback manipulation.
6. **Compliance with laws.** Follow all applicable local, state, and federal laws.
7. **Respect for IP.** Only sell authentic items; do not infringe on others' intellectual property.

### Consequences of Code Violations

| Violation Severity | First Offense | Repeat Offense |
|---|---|---|
| Minor (listing error) | Warning + listing removal | Temporary restriction |
| Moderate (policy violation) | Temporary restriction | Suspension |
| Severe (fraud, counterfeit) | Immediate suspension | Permanent ban |
