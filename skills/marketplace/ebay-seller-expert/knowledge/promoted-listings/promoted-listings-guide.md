# Promoted Listings — Complete Seller Guide

## Overview

Promoted Listings is eBay's advertising platform that increases item visibility in search results and browse pages. There are three distinct products: **Standard** (cost-per-sale), **Advanced** (cost-per-click), and **Express** (simplified CPC for auction/BIN listings). Each serves different seller needs and budget models.

---

## Promoted Listings Standard (PLS)

### How It Works

- **Model:** Cost-per-sale (CPS). You only pay when a buyer clicks your promoted listing AND purchases within 30 days.
- **Ad Fee:** A percentage of the final sale price (excluding shipping and tax).
- **Placement:** Promoted listings appear in search results, category browse pages, product detail pages, and the "Similar items" carousel.
- **Badge:** Listings display a small "Sponsored" label but otherwise look like normal results.

### Ad Rate Percentages

| Category | Typical Trending Rate | Competitive Range |
|---|---|---|
| Electronics | 2%–4% | 3%–8% |
| Clothing & Shoes | 5%–10% | 8%–15% |
| Collectibles | 3%–6% | 5%–10% |
| Home & Garden | 4%–7% | 6%–12% |
| Auto Parts | 2%–5% | 4%–8% |
| Sporting Goods | 3%–6% | 5%–10% |
| Toys & Hobbies | 4%–8% | 6%–12% |
| Health & Beauty | 5%–9% | 8%–14% |

> **Trending Rate:** The ad rate eBay suggests based on what other sellers in that category are bidding. Shown during campaign setup and in the campaign management dashboard.

### Setting Up a Standard Campaign

**Navigation:** Seller Hub > Marketing > Promoted Listings > Create campaign

1. **Select campaign type:** Choose "Standard — Cost per sale."
2. **Name your campaign:** Use descriptive names (e.g., "Q1-2026-Electronics-High-Margin").
3. **Select listings:** Options include:
   - **Manual selection** — Pick specific listings.
   - **Rule-based** — Automatically include listings matching criteria (category, price range, quantity).
   - **Bulk upload** — Upload CSV with item IDs and ad rates.
4. **Set ad rates:**
   - **Single rate** — One percentage for all listings in the campaign.
   - **Individual rates** — Different percentages per listing (recommended for diverse inventory).
   - **Suggested rate** — Apply eBay's trending rate to each listing automatically.
5. **Launch the campaign.**

### Ad Rate Strategy

| Strategy | When to Use | Typical Rate |
|---|---|---|
| At trending rate | Default starting point for most items | 100% of suggested |
| Below trending rate | High-demand items that sell anyway | 50%–75% of suggested |
| Above trending rate | Slow-moving inventory or competitive niches | 125%–200% of suggested |
| Minimum viable rate | Testing whether promotion helps at all | 1%–2% flat |

### When the Ad Fee Is Charged

1. Buyer clicks your promoted listing.
2. A 30-day attribution window opens.
3. If the buyer purchases that item (or any item from your store) within 30 days, the ad fee applies.
4. The fee is calculated on the final sale price of the item the buyer clicked on.
5. The fee is deducted from your payout automatically.

> **Important:** The 30-day click-through attribution means you may pay ad fees on sales that appear "organic" in your orders. Check the Promoted Listings report to see attributed sales.

## Promoted Listings Advanced (PLA)

### How It Works

- **Model:** Cost-per-click (CPC). You pay when a buyer clicks your ad, regardless of whether they purchase.
- **Auction system:** You bid on keywords. Higher bids get better placement.
- **Placement:** Top slots in search results (positions 1–4), marked "Sponsored."
- **Budget control:** Daily budget cap prevents overspending.
- **Eligibility:** Requires eBay Store subscription (Basic or higher).

### Setting Up an Advanced Campaign

**Navigation:** Seller Hub > Marketing > Promoted Listings > Create campaign > Advanced

1. **Select listings:** Choose items to advertise (works best with 5–20 high-value listings per campaign).
2. **Choose keywords:**
   - **Suggested keywords** — eBay recommends keywords based on your listing title and category.
   - **Manual keywords** — Add your own keyword targets.
   - **Match types:**
     - **Broad match** — Ad shows for searches containing your keywords in any order, plus related terms.
     - **Exact match** — Ad shows only for searches matching your keywords precisely.
     - **Phrase match** — Ad shows for searches containing your keywords in order.
3. **Set bids:**
   - **Per-keyword bids** — Set a max CPC for each keyword.
   - **Suggested bid range** — eBay shows low/medium/high bid suggestions.
   - Minimum bid: $0.02.
   - Typical winning bids: $0.10–$2.00 depending on category competition.
4. **Set daily budget:**
   - Minimum: $1/day.
   - Recommended starting budget: $10–$25/day for testing.
5. **Add negative keywords:** Exclude irrelevant search terms to avoid wasted clicks.
6. **Launch campaign.**

### Keyword Strategy

| Keyword Type | Example (for "Nike Air Max 90") | CPC Range | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brand + model | "nike air max 90" | $0.30–$0.80 | High-intent buyers |
| Generic category | "men's running shoes" | $0.15–$0.50 | Volume/awareness |
| Long-tail | "nike air max 90 black size 11" | $0.10–$0.30 | Very high intent, low volume |
| Competitor brand | "adidas ultraboost alternative" | $0.20–$0.60 | Steal competitor traffic |

### Budget Management

- **Daily budget** resets at midnight Pacific Time.
- **Campaign budget** is optional and caps total campaign spend.
- If your daily budget runs out, your ads stop showing until the next day.
- Underspend does NOT roll over to the next day.
- Monitor spend-to-budget ratio: if you consistently spend less than 70% of budget, your bids may be too low.

## Promoted Listings Express

### How It Works

- **Model:** Cost-per-click (CPC), simplified.
- **Target:** Auction-style and Buy It Now listings without a Store subscription.
- **Setup:** One-click promotion from the listing page or active listings view.
- **Budget:** eBay sets the bid automatically; you set a daily budget.
- **Eligibility:** Available to all sellers, including those without a Store.

### When to Use Express

- You sell primarily through auctions.
- You have a small number of high-value listings.
- You do not have a Store subscription and cannot access Advanced.
- You want a quick promotion without campaign management overhead.

## Reporting and Analytics

### Accessing Reports

**Navigation:** Seller Hub > Marketing > Promoted Listings > select campaign > Performance tab

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Good Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Impressions | Times your promoted listing was shown | Varies by category |
| Clicks | Times buyers clicked your promoted listing | — |
| Click-through rate (CTR) | Clicks / Impressions | >0.5% |
| Sales | Number of items sold attributed to promotion | — |
| Conversion rate | Sales / Clicks | >5% for Standard |
| Ad spend | Total fees paid for the period | — |
| ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) | Revenue / Ad Spend | >10x for Standard |
| Ad fee % of revenue | Ad Spend / Total Revenue | <10% |
| Organic uplift | Sales increase compared to non-promoted period | Track month-over-month |

### Report Downloads

- **Campaign-level report:** Summary performance by campaign.
- **Listing-level report:** Performance for each individual listing.
- **Keyword report (Advanced only):** Performance by keyword, including impressions, clicks, CPC, and conversions.
- Download as CSV from: Marketing > Promoted Listings > Reports > Download.

## ROI Calculation

### Standard (CPS) ROI Formula

```
ROI = ((Revenue from Promoted Sales - Cost of Goods - eBay Fees - Ad Fees) / Ad Fees) x 100
```

**Example:**
- Item sells for $50
- Cost of goods: $20
- eBay final value fee (13.25%): $6.63
- Ad rate: 5% = $2.50
- Shipping cost: $5
- **Profit = $50 - $20 - $6.63 - $2.50 - $5 = $15.87**
- **ROI = ($15.87 / $2.50) x 100 = 635%**

### Advanced (CPC) ROI Formula

```
ROI = ((Revenue from Ad Clicks - COGS - eBay Fees - Total CPC Spend) / Total CPC Spend) x 100
```

**Example:**
- 100 clicks at $0.25 CPC = $25 ad spend
- 8 sales at $50 each = $400 revenue
- COGS: $160 (8 x $20)
- eBay fees: $53 (8 x $6.63)
- **Profit = $400 - $160 - $53 - $25 = $162**
- **ROI = ($162 / $25) x 100 = 648%**

### Break-Even Ad Rate (Standard)

```
Max Ad Rate = (Sale Price - COGS - eBay Fees - Shipping) / Sale Price x 100
```

This tells you the highest ad rate you can set without losing money on the sale.

## Best Practices for Ad Rate Optimization

### 1. Start at the Trending Rate, Then Adjust

- Launch at 100% of the trending rate for 7–14 days.
- If impressions and clicks are strong, reduce the rate by 1–2% increments weekly.
- If impressions are low, increase by 1–2% increments.
- Goal: find the lowest rate that maintains consistent impression volume.

### 2. Use Tiered Rates by Margin

| Margin Tier | Ad Rate Strategy |
|---|---|
| High margin (>50%) | Can afford 8%–15% ad rates |
| Medium margin (25%–50%) | Target 3%–8% ad rates |
| Low margin (<25%) | Keep rates at 1%–3% or skip promotion |

### 3. Promote Strategically, Not Everything

- Promote your top 20% of listings that drive 80% of revenue.
- Do NOT promote items with very low margins unless you need sell-through velocity.
- Promote new listings aggressively for the first 7–14 days to gain initial traction.
- Pause promotions on items that sell quickly without them.

### 4. Seasonal Strategy

| Season | Strategy |
|---|---|
| January | Post-holiday clearance — increase ad rates on excess inventory |
| February–March | Pre-spring — promote garden, outdoor, sports |
| April–May | Graduation and wedding season — formal wear, gifts |
| June–August | Summer items, back-to-school ramp-up in late July |
| September–October | Halloween, fall fashion — increase budgets |
| November | Black Friday/Cyber Monday — max ad rates, max budget |
| December 1–15 | Holiday peak — highest competition, highest rates needed |
| December 16–25 | Ship-by cutoff approaching — reduce spend on items with long handling |

### 5. Monitor and Prune

- Review campaign performance weekly.
- Remove listings with high impressions but zero sales (poor conversion).
- Remove listings with very low impressions (ad rate too low or listing quality issue).
- Add high-performing organic listings to campaigns.

## Which Items to Promote

### Best Candidates

- Items with strong margins that can absorb ad fees.
- Newly listed items (first 14 days) that need initial search visibility.
- Competitive categories where organic ranking is difficult.
- Items with excellent listing quality (good photos, complete item specifics).
- Seasonal items approaching peak demand.

### Poor Candidates

- Items with margins under 10% (ad fees will eliminate profit).
- Items that already sell quickly and consistently organically.
- Items with poor listing quality (low-quality photos, missing item specifics) — fix the listing first.
- One-of-a-kind items with very niche appeal (low search volume means few impressions anyway).
- Items with very low sell-through rates (promotion cannot fix a pricing or demand problem).

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| No impressions | Ad rate too low or listing has quality issues | Increase ad rate; complete all item specifics; improve title keywords |
| High impressions, no clicks | Poor listing photo or unappealing price | Improve main photo; check competitive pricing |
| High clicks, no sales | Listing content or price not converting | Improve description; check return policy; review price vs. competitors |
| Ad spend too high relative to revenue | Ad rates set too high or low-margin items promoted | Lower rates; remove low-margin items from campaign |
| Attributed sales seem inflated | 30-day attribution window captures unrelated purchases | This is normal behavior — focus on incremental ROAS |

## Quick Reference: Standard vs. Advanced vs. Express

| Feature | Standard | Advanced | Express |
|---|---|---|---|
| Payment model | Cost-per-sale | Cost-per-click | Cost-per-click |
| When you pay | Only on completed sale | On every click | On every click |
| Keyword targeting | No | Yes | No |
| Budget control | No cap (pay per sale) | Daily/campaign budget | Daily budget |
| Store required | No | Yes (Basic+) | No |
| Minimum ad rate/bid | 1% | $0.02/click | Set by eBay |
| Best for | Broad visibility, risk-free | High-value keyword targeting | Quick single-listing boost |
| Setup complexity | Low | Medium–High | Very low |
| Reporting detail | Campaign + listing level | Campaign + keyword level | Basic |
