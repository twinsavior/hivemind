## Shipping Carriers Available Through eBay

eBay offers discounted shipping labels for the following carriers when purchased through Seller Hub or the eBay app.

| Carrier | Services Available via eBay Labels | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **USPS** | First Class Mail, Priority Mail, Priority Mail Express, Ground Advantage, Media Mail, Parcel Select | Lightweight items (under 1 lb), media, most small packages |
| **UPS** | Ground, 3 Day Select, 2nd Day Air, Next Day Air, Next Day Air Saver | Heavier packages (over 1 lb), business addresses |
| **FedEx** | Ground, Home Delivery, Express Saver, 2Day, Standard Overnight, Priority Overnight | Heavy items, time-sensitive deliveries |

### eBay Label Discounts (Approximate Savings)

| Carrier | Approximate Discount vs. Retail Rates |
|---|---|
| USPS via eBay | Up to 50% off retail USPS rates |
| UPS via eBay | Up to 55% off standard UPS rates |
| FedEx via eBay | Up to 67% off standard FedEx rates |

> **Note**: Exact discount percentages vary by service, package size, weight, and destination. eBay negotiates commercial rates that are typically better than what individual sellers can get directly from carriers.

### How to Purchase eBay Shipping Labels

```
Seller Hub > Orders > Awaiting Shipment > [Select order] > Print Shipping Label
```

Or from the eBay Seller mobile app:
```
Orders > [Select order] > Ship > Buy Label
```

**Benefits of eBay labels:**
- Tracking is automatically uploaded to the order
- Discounted rates (significant savings over retail)
- Buyer sees tracking immediately
- Shipment confirmation counts toward your handling time metric
- Claims are easier to file if package is lost/damaged

## Shipping Strategies: Free vs. Calculated vs. Flat Rate

### Free Shipping

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| **How it works** | You build shipping cost into the item price, buyer pays $0 for shipping |
| **Search boost** | Listings with free shipping rank higher in Cassini search |
| **Buyer psychology** | Buyers strongly prefer free shipping; reduces cart abandonment |
| **Risk** | You absorb shipping cost even for distant/expensive zones |
| **Best for** | Lightweight items, items with predictable shipping costs, competitive categories |

**Free Shipping Strategy:**
1. Calculate your average shipping cost across all zones
2. Add that cost to your item price
3. Offer free shipping via the cheapest viable service (USPS Ground Advantage for most items)
4. Factor in that some buyers will be close (cheap to ship) and some far (expensive) — it averages out

### Calculated Shipping

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| **How it works** | Buyer enters their ZIP code, eBay calculates exact shipping cost based on package dimensions, weight, and distance |
| **Search impact** | Slight negative impact compared to free shipping |
| **Buyer experience** | Total price varies by buyer location; can discourage distant buyers |
| **Risk** | Minimal — buyer pays actual shipping cost |
| **Best for** | Heavy items, large items, items where shipping cost varies widely by destination |

**Setting up calculated shipping:**
1. In listing form, select "Calculated: Cost varies by buyer location"
2. Enter package weight (with packaging)
3. Enter package dimensions (length x width x height)
4. Select carrier and service
5. Optionally add handling fee (fixed amount added to calculated cost)

### Flat Rate Shipping

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| **How it works** | You set a fixed shipping cost regardless of buyer location |
| **Search impact** | Neutral (between free and calculated) |
| **Risk** | Moderate — you may lose money on distant shipments or profit on nearby ones |
| **Best for** | Items with consistent package size, mid-weight items, when you want simplicity |

## Handling Time

Handling time is the number of business days between when the buyer pays and when you must ship (upload tracking with a carrier scan).

### Handling Time Options

| Handling Time | Description | TRS Plus Eligible? |
|---|---|---|
| Same business day | Must ship by end of the business day payment is received | Yes |
| 1 business day | Must ship by end of next business day | Yes |
| 2 business days | Must ship within 2 business days | No |
| 3 business days | Must ship within 3 business days | No |
| 4+ business days | Extended handling (up to 30 days for custom items) | No |

### How Business Days Are Calculated

- **Business days**: Monday through Friday, excluding eBay-recognized US holidays
- **Cutoff time**: If a buyer pays after a certain time (varies, typically after your local carrier pickup time), the handling clock starts the next business day
- **Weekends**: Saturday and Sunday are not business days
- **Holidays**: New Year's Day, MLK Day, Presidents' Day, Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Columbus Day, Veterans Day, Thanksgiving, Christmas

### What Counts as "Shipped"

eBay considers an item "shipped" when:
1. Tracking number is uploaded to the order, AND
2. The carrier has performed a **first scan** (acceptance scan, pickup scan, or in-transit scan)

> **Critical**: Simply uploading a tracking number is NOT sufficient. The carrier must scan the package. If you print a label but don't drop off the package, the tracking will not show a scan, and the shipment will count as late.

### Late Shipment Consequences

| Consequence | Details |
|---|---|
| Late shipment recorded | Counts against your late shipment rate metric |
| Seller level impact | Can cause drop to Below Standard if rate exceeds 7% |
| Buyer trust | Buyers may leave negative feedback or open cases |
| Estimated delivery pushed | eBay updates estimated delivery date, potentially triggering INR cases |

## Tracking Requirements

### General Tracking Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Tracking required | On all US domestic transactions to maintain seller protections |
| Upload deadline | Must be uploaded within your stated handling time |
| Automatic upload | eBay labels auto-upload tracking; third-party labels must be entered manually |
| Valid carriers | Must be a carrier eBay recognizes (USPS, UPS, FedEx, DHL, and others) |

### Tracking Upload Rate for Top Rated Status

To qualify for Top Rated Seller, you must upload valid tracking on **95% or more** of transactions within the stated handling time (measured over the most recent 3-month period).

### How to Upload Tracking Manually

```
Seller Hub > Orders > Awaiting Shipment > [Select order] > Add Tracking Number
```

Enter:
- Carrier name (select from dropdown)
- Tracking number
- Click "Save" or "Mark as Shipped"

### Signature Confirmation Requirements

| Sale Price | Tracking Requirement |
|---|---|
| Under $750 | Standard tracking (delivery confirmation) is sufficient |
| $750 and above | **Signature confirmation required** for seller protection |

> **Critical**: For items sold at $750 or more, if a buyer claims Item Not Received (INR), you MUST have signature confirmation to win the case. Standard delivery confirmation is NOT sufficient. Always purchase signature confirmation for high-value items.

### Signature Confirmation by Carrier

| Carrier | Signature Service Name | Approximate Cost |
|---|---|---|
| USPS | Signature Confirmation | ~$3.45 |
| USPS | Adult Signature Required | ~$8.50 |
| UPS | Signature Required | Included with some services, ~$6 add-on |
| UPS | Adult Signature Required | ~$7.00 |
| FedEx | Direct Signature Required | ~$6.75 |
| FedEx | Adult Signature Required | ~$7.50 |

## Shipping Profiles

Create reusable shipping configurations so you don't set up shipping details for every listing.

### Creating a Shipping Profile

```
Seller Hub > Listings > Business Policies > Shipping > Create Policy
```

### What to Include in a Shipping Profile

| Setting | Options |
|---|---|
| **Domestic shipping** | Free, flat rate, or calculated |
| **Domestic services** | USPS Ground Advantage, Priority Mail, UPS Ground, etc. |
| **Handling time** | Same day, 1 day, 2 days, 3 days |
| **International shipping** | None, flat rate, calculated, or eBay International Shipping |
| **Package type** | Letter, package, large package, freight |
| **Package dimensions** | Length, width, height, weight |
| **Combined shipping** | Discount rules for multiple items |
| **Excluded locations** | States, territories, PO Boxes, APO/FPO |

### Recommended Shipping Profiles

| Profile Name | Configuration | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Free Standard | Free shipping, USPS Ground Advantage, 1-day handling | Most items under 1 lb |
| Free Priority | Free shipping, USPS Priority Mail, 1-day handling | Items needing faster delivery |
| Calculated by Weight | Calculated shipping, USPS Ground Advantage, 1-day handling | Items varying in weight |
| Heavy Item | Calculated shipping, UPS Ground, 2-day handling | Items over 5 lbs |
| Fragile/High Value | Calculated shipping, UPS Ground or FedEx, signature required, 1-day handling | Electronics, jewelry, $750+ items |

## Combined Shipping

When a buyer purchases multiple items from you, combined shipping saves both of you money.

### Setting Up Combined Shipping Rules

```
Seller Hub > Listings > Business Policies > Shipping > [Select Policy] > Combined Shipping
```

| Rule Type | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **Flat amount** | Each additional item adds a fixed amount (e.g., first item $5, each additional $1) |
| **Percentage discount** | Each additional item gets a % off its shipping cost |
| **Free for additional items** | Buyer pays shipping on first item, additional items ship free |
| **Calculated** | eBay recalculates based on combined package weight |

### Combined Shipping Best Practices

- Enable combined shipping to encourage multi-item purchases
- Set reasonable additional item rates (cost of added weight/space)
- Mention combined shipping in your listings to attract buyers browsing your store
- Process combined orders as one shipment to save on shipping costs

## International Shipping

### Option 1: eBay International Shipping (Recommended)

eBay International Shipping is eBay's managed international shipping program. You ship domestically to an eBay hub, and eBay handles international logistics.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **How it works** | Ship to eBay's domestic hub (usually in Kentucky or Illinois). eBay forwards internationally. |
| **Customs forms** | Handled by eBay |
| **Duties and taxes** | Collected from buyer at checkout by eBay |
| **Returns** | Return to eBay's domestic facility (not from international buyer to you) |
| **Tracking** | End-to-end tracking provided |
| **Seller protection** | Full eBay seller protection applies |
| **Fee** | 1.65% international fee on total sale amount |
| **Eligible items** | Most items. Some restrictions (hazmat, oversized, food, etc.) |

**How to enable:**
```
Seller Hub > Listings > Business Policies > Shipping > International Shipping > eBay International Shipping
```

### Option 2: Global Shipping Program (GSP) — Legacy

The original eBay international program. Similar to eBay International Shipping but being phased out for new enrollments.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **How it works** | Ship to eBay's GSP hub in Erlanger, KY. eBay forwards internationally. |
| **Customs** | Handled by Pitney Bowes (eBay's partner) |
| **Availability** | Being replaced by eBay International Shipping for new enrollments |

### Option 3: Direct International Shipping

You ship directly to the international buyer and handle customs forms yourself.

| Pros | Cons |
|---|---|
| More control over shipping service | You must complete customs forms |
| Potentially faster delivery | You handle duties/taxes disputes |
| Can use any carrier | Higher risk of lost packages |
| | No eBay international shipping protection |
| | Returns come back internationally (slow, expensive) |

### International Shipping Exclusions

You can exclude certain destinations:

```
Seller Hub > Listings > Business Policies > Shipping > Exclude Locations
```

Common exclusions:
- APO/FPO/DPO (military addresses)
- US Protectorates (Guam, Puerto Rico, Virgin Islands — note: these are domestic addresses)
- Specific countries or regions
- PO Boxes

## Package Dimensions and Weight Guidelines

### USPS Size Limits

| Service | Max Weight | Max Length | Max L+Girth |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ground Advantage | 70 lbs | 130" combined (L+W+H) | N/A |
| Priority Mail | 70 lbs | 108" length | 130" combined |
| Priority Mail Flat Rate (Small) | 70 lbs | 8-5/8" x 5-3/8" x 1-5/8" | N/A |
| Priority Mail Flat Rate (Medium) | 70 lbs | 11" x 8-1/2" x 5-1/2" (or 13-5/8" x 11-5/8" x 3-3/8") | N/A |
| Priority Mail Flat Rate (Large) | 70 lbs | 12" x 12" x 5-1/2" | N/A |
| First Class Mail (letters/flats) | 13 oz | Varies | N/A |
| Media Mail | 70 lbs | 108" length | 130" combined |

### UPS Size Limits

| Metric | Limit |
|---|---|
| Max weight | 150 lbs |
| Max length | 108" |
| Max length + girth | 165" |
| Oversize surcharge | Applies if L+Girth > 130" or any dimension > 96" |

### FedEx Size Limits

| Metric | Limit |
|---|---|
| Max weight | 150 lbs |
| Max length | 119" (ground), 108" (express) |
| Max length + girth | 165" |
| Oversize surcharge | Similar thresholds to UPS |

### Dimensional Weight (DIM Weight)

Carriers charge based on actual weight OR dimensional weight, whichever is greater.

**DIM Weight Formula:**
```
DIM Weight (lbs) = (Length x Width x Height in inches) / DIM Factor
```

| Carrier | DIM Factor |
|---|---|
| USPS (Priority Mail) | 166 |
| UPS | 139 |
| FedEx | 139 |

**Example**: A box measuring 18" x 14" x 10" weighing 5 lbs:
- UPS DIM weight: (18 x 14 x 10) / 139 = 18.1 lbs
- UPS charges for 18.1 lbs (DIM weight) because it exceeds actual weight of 5 lbs

> **Tip**: Use the smallest box possible. Oversized packaging dramatically increases shipping costs due to DIM weight pricing.

## Packaging Best Practices

### Packaging Materials

| Material | Use Case | Where to Get |
|---|---|---|
| Corrugated boxes | Most items | Free USPS Priority boxes at usps.com; buy from Uline, Amazon |
| Poly mailers | Soft goods (clothing, fabric items) | Amazon, Uline, eBay shipping supplies |
| Padded envelopes | Small, lightweight items | Amazon, Uline, eBay shipping supplies |
| Bubble wrap | Fragile items, gap fill | Buy in bulk rolls |
| Packing peanuts | Gap fill for large boxes | Use sparingly — messy for buyers |
| Air pillows | Gap fill | Inflator machines or pre-filled bags |
| Kraft paper | Wrapping, gap fill | Eco-friendly option |

### Free USPS Supplies

USPS offers free Priority Mail boxes and envelopes — order from `usps.com/freeboxes`:
- Priority Mail Flat Rate boxes (small, medium, large)
- Priority Mail envelopes
- Priority Mail Regional Rate boxes

> **Important**: Free USPS boxes can ONLY be used with the corresponding USPS service. You cannot ship a Priority Mail box via UPS or non-Priority USPS services.

## Shipping Cost Optimization

### Rules of Thumb

| Item Weight | Recommended Service | Typical Cost Range |
|---|---|---|
| Under 4 oz | USPS Ground Advantage (letter) | $3-$5 |
| 4-15.9 oz | USPS Ground Advantage (package) | $4-$8 |
| 1-3 lbs | USPS Ground Advantage or Priority Mail | $7-$15 |
| 3-10 lbs | UPS Ground or USPS Priority Mail | $10-$25 |
| 10-25 lbs | UPS Ground | $15-$40 |
| 25-70 lbs | UPS Ground or FedEx Ground | $25-$80+ |
| Over 70 lbs | Freight (special handling) | Varies widely |

### Cost Reduction Strategies

1. **Use eBay labels** — always cheaper than retail carrier rates
2. **USPS Flat Rate** when heavy items fit in flat rate boxes
3. **Right-size packaging** — avoid DIM weight penalties
4. **Regional Rate boxes** (USPS) — cheaper than standard Priority for short-zone shipments
5. **Carrier comparison** — always compare USPS, UPS, and FedEx before purchasing a label
6. **Cubic pricing** (USPS) — for small, heavy items, cubic pricing can be significantly cheaper
7. **Buy packaging in bulk** — mailers, tape, bubble wrap from wholesale suppliers

## Common Shipping Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Not shipping on time | Late shipment defect | Ship same day or next business day |
| Wrong package dimensions on label | Carrier adjustment surcharge ($15-$25+) | Measure package accurately after packing |
| No signature on $750+ items | Lose INR case automatically | Always add signature confirmation |
| Using wrong USPS box type | Media Mail in Priority box = surcharge | Match box to service |
| Not buying insurance for high-value items | You eat the loss if package is lost/damaged | eBay labels include some coverage; add more for high-value items |
| Shipping without sufficient padding | Damage claims, negative feedback | Pack as if the box will be thrown 10 feet |
| Giving tracking to wrong order | Delivery confirmation fails, lose INR case | Double-check tracking assignment before marking shipped |
