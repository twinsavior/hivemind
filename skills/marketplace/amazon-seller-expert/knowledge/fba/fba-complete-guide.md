# Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA) Complete Guide

## Overview

Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA) is a service where sellers ship their products to Amazon's fulfillment centers, and Amazon handles storage, picking, packing, shipping, customer service, and returns. FBA products are eligible for Prime two-day shipping, which significantly increases visibility and conversion rates.

---

## How FBA Works (6-Step Process)

1. **Register for FBA**: Set up through Seller Central account settings
2. **List and assign products**: Confirm eligibility, review policies, convert listings to FBA
3. **Send shipments**: Ship products to Amazon fulfillment centers using the Send to Amazon workflow
4. **Amazon handles fulfillment**: Amazon receives, stores, picks, packs, and ships orders
5. **Manage inventory**: Use the FBA Dashboard and restock tools to monitor stock levels
6. **Optimize operations**: Leverage FBA services, global expansion programs, and analytics

---

## Key Benefits

### Cost Savings
- FBA shipping costs are approximately **70% less per unit** compared to premium options from major US carriers
- Pay-as-you-go model with no minimum inventory requirements
- No need for your own warehouse, packing supplies, or shipping staff

### Sales Growth
- **Prime badge**: Products become Prime-eligible with two-day shipping
- Higher Buy Box win rate (FBA is a major factor in Buy Box eligibility)
- Increased customer trust and conversion rates
- Access to Prime Day and other Prime-exclusive deals

### Operational Relief
- Amazon handles all customer service inquiries for FBA orders
- Amazon manages returns processing
- Sellers focus on product development, sourcing, and marketing

### Additional Capabilities
- Multi-Channel Fulfillment (MCF): Use FBA for non-Amazon sales channels
- Subscribe & Save eligibility
- Buy with Prime: Offer Prime shipping on your own website
- Global selling through Amazon's international fulfillment network

## Eligibility and Enrollment

### Requirements
- Active Amazon seller account (Professional plan recommended)
- Products must comply with Amazon's FBA product restrictions
- Products must meet packaging and labeling requirements
- Seller must agree to FBA terms of service

### How to Enroll
1. Log into Seller Central
2. Go to **Settings > Account Info > Fulfillment by Amazon**
3. Accept FBA terms
4. Begin converting listings to FBA fulfillment

### Converting Existing Listings to FBA
- In Seller Central, select listings and change fulfillment channel from "Merchant" to "Amazon"
- Can be done individually or in bulk
- The FBA Enrollment Opportunities tool uses machine learning to identify your best products for FBA

## Product Preparation Requirements

### Labeling

#### Barcode Requirements
- Every product unit must have a unique **FNSKU** (Fulfillment Network Stock Keeping Unit) barcode
- Each unit must have an accessible, scannable barcode on its exterior
- Label size: Between **1" x 2" and 2" x 3"**
- Labels must be on the exterior with at least **1/4 inch** clearance from container edges
- Readable numbers must match the barcode

#### Covering Existing Barcodes
- Render any existing barcodes on shipping box exteriors unscannable
- Use opaque tape or felt-tip markers to obscure them
- This prevents incorrect barcodes from being scanned during receiving

#### Barcode Options
- **Amazon Barcode (FNSKU)**: Unique to your seller account, recommended for commingling avoidance
- **Manufacturer Barcode (UPC/EAN)**: Can be used but products may be commingled with other sellers' inventory
- **Amazon Label Service**: Amazon can apply FNSKU labels for a per-unit fee

### Packaging Requirements

#### General Rules
- All products must arrive at fulfillment centers ready to ship to the end customer
- Packaging must protect the product during storage and shipping
- Products must be securely enclosed so they do not come loose

#### Box Requirements
- Boxes must not exceed **25 inches on any side**
- Maximum weight: **50 lbs per box**
- If weight exceeds 50 lbs: Apply a **"Team Lift"** label
- If weight exceeds 100 lbs: Apply a **"Mechanical Lift"** label

#### Poly Bag Requirements
- Units not in secure packaging must be shrink-wrapped or placed in a clear poly bag
- Poly bags must have a minimum thickness of **1.5 mil** per side
- Poly bags must include a **suffocation warning** printed on the bag
- Suffocation warning must be readable and in the primary language of the marketplace

#### Special Product Types
- **Apparel**: All hangers must be removed (exception: mini hangers for baby clothes)
- **Expiration-dated products**: Date must be labeled on the case in **36-point font or larger** in MM-DD-YYYY format, and on each container inside
- **Fragile items**: Must be packaged with protective materials (bubble wrap, air pillows)
- **Liquids**: Must be sealed and double-bagged to prevent leaks
- **Sharp items**: Must be packaged so edges cannot cut through packaging
- **Small items**: Items smaller than 2-1/8" x 1-3/4" x 5/8" must be placed in a poly bag
- **Sets/bundles**: Must be packaged together and labeled as a set ("Sold as set," "Ready to ship")

### Hazardous Materials (Hazmat)
- Products containing hazardous materials require special handling
- Must be labeled with appropriate hazard warnings ("flammable," "corrosive," etc.)
- May require Safety Data Sheets (SDS) uploaded to Seller Central
- Some hazmat products are eligible for FBA with proper documentation; others are restricted
- Higher storage fees apply for dangerous goods

## Shipping to Amazon (Send to Amazon Workflow)

### Overview
Send to Amazon is the streamlined shipment creation workflow for getting inventory to fulfillment centers. Access it through **Seller Central > Inventory > Shipments > Send to Amazon**.

### The Five-Step Workflow

#### Step 1: Choose Inventory to Send
- Select your ship-from address and marketplace destination
- Select SKUs already converted to FBA
- Complete packing details for each SKU
- Create **case pack templates** (reusable templates storing box content, weight, dimensions, and prep details) for regular replenishment

#### Step 1b: Pack Individual Units
- Applies when shipping individual units or mixed-SKU boxes
- System indicates which SKUs can be packed together
- Choose between single or multiple boxes

#### Step 2: Confirm Shipping
- Provide estimated ship date (helps Amazon plan receiving)
- Choose shipping mode:
  - **Small Parcel Delivery (SPD)**: Individual boxes shipped via carrier
  - **Less-Than-Truckload (LTL)**: Palletized shipments
  - **Full Truckload (FTL)**: Full truck shipments
- Select carrier (Amazon partnered carriers offer discounted rates)
- Review estimated shipping costs

#### Step 3: Print Box Labels
- Review box details and print labels
- Required for all small parcel deliveries
- Each box gets a unique label tied to the shipment

#### Step 4: Confirm Carrier and Pallet Information (LTL/FTL Only)
- Provide pallet count and carrier information
- Print pallet labels for non-partnered carriers

#### Step 5: Print Pallet Labels (Partnered Carriers Only)
- For pallet shipments using Amazon partnered carriers

### Shipping Tips
- **Amazon Partnered Carriers** offer discounted rates, automated tracking, and integrated label printing
- Always use box content information to speed up receiving
- Accurate box weights and dimensions prevent receiving delays and potential fees
- Track shipments and resolve discrepancies through the Shipping Queue

### Inbound Placement Service
- Amazon may split your shipment across multiple fulfillment centers
- **Inbound Placement Service fee**: Charged for sending all inventory to a single warehouse (Amazon redistributes it)
- You can reduce this fee by shipping to multiple fulfillment centers yourself

## FBA Fees

### Fulfillment Fees (Per Unit Shipped)

Charged when Amazon ships an order to the customer. Based on product size tier and weight.

#### Standard FBA Rates (Non-Apparel)

| Size Tier | Weight Range | Fee per Unit |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Small Standard | 2 oz to 16 oz | $3.06 - $3.65 |
| Large Standard | 4 oz to 3 lb | $3.68 - $5.68 |
| Large Standard | 3 lb to 20+ lb | $5.68 - $6.92 + $0.08 per 4 oz above 3 lb |
| Large Bulky | Up to 50 lb | $9.61 + $0.38 per lb above 1st lb |
| Extra-Large (0-50 lb) | Varies | $26.33 - $29.33 |
| Extra-Large (50-70 lb) | Varies | $40.12 |
| Extra-Large (70-150 lb) | Varies | $54.90 |
| Extra-Large (150+ lb) | Over 150 lb | $194.95 |

#### Low-Price FBA Rates (Non-Apparel)
Reduced rates for lower-priced items (approximately 25% less than standard):

| Size Tier | Weight Range | Fee per Unit |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Small Standard | 2 oz to 16 oz | $2.29 - $2.88 |
| Large Standard | 4 oz to 3 lb | $2.91 - $4.91 |
| Large Standard | 3 lb+ | $4.91 - $6.15 + increments |

#### Apparel Rates
Slightly higher than non-apparel due to handling complexity:

| Size Tier | Weight Range | Fee per Unit |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Small Standard | 2 oz to 16 oz | $3.27 - $3.98 |
| Large Standard | 4 oz to 3 lb | $4.25 - $5.68 |
| Large Standard | 3 lb+ | $5.68 - $6.92 + increments |

### Monthly Storage Fees

Charged based on the daily average volume (in cubic feet) of inventory stored in fulfillment centers.

| Period | Standard Size | Oversize |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| January - September | $0.78 / cubic foot | $0.56 / cubic foot |
| October - December | $2.40 / cubic foot | $1.40 / cubic foot |

- **Dangerous goods**: Higher rates apply
- **Peak season (Oct-Dec)**: Storage fees increase significantly -- plan inventory accordingly

### Aged Inventory Surcharge
- Applied to items stored **181 days or more**
- Charges increase the longer items sit in the warehouse
- Designed to incentivize efficient inventory turnover
- Check your Inventory Age report regularly

### Long-Term Storage Fees
- Applied to inventory stored for **365+ days**
- Significantly higher per-cubic-foot charges
- Can quickly erode profits on slow-moving inventory
- Options: Remove inventory, liquidate, or create a removal order

### Removal and Disposal Fees
- **Removal order**: Amazon returns inventory to you (per-unit fee)
- **Disposal order**: Amazon destroys inventory (per-unit fee, lower than removal)
- Use for slow-moving or end-of-life inventory to avoid escalating storage fees

### Other Potential Fees
- **Unplanned service fees**: Charged when products arrive without proper prep or labeling
- **FBA Prep Service fee**: If Amazon prepares your products (labeling, poly-bagging, etc.)
- **FBA Label Service fee**: If Amazon applies FNSKU labels
- **Returns processing fee**: For categories with free customer returns (apparel, shoes, etc.)
- **Inbound placement service fee**: For consolidated shipments to a single fulfillment center

## FBA New Selection Program

### What It Is
A program designed to help sellers launch new products on FBA by offering significant fee discounts and credits.

### Benefits for New FBA ASINs
- **Free monthly storage** for eligible new-to-FBA ASINs
- **Fulfillment fee rebates** on initial units sold
- **Free removals** for unsold inventory
- **Free liquidation** services
- Credits toward inbound shipping

### New Seller Incentives (separate but related)
- **$100** in shipping credits
- **$200** in fulfillment credits
- **$400** in inbound placement credits
- Free storage with auto-enrollment in the New Selection program

### Eligibility
- Product must be new to FBA (not previously fulfilled by Amazon)
- Seller must have a Professional account
- Product must meet standard FBA requirements
- Program details and specific rebate amounts vary; check the FBA New Selection Dashboard in Seller Central

## Restricted Products

### Products NOT Eligible for FBA
- Alcoholic beverages
- Tires
- Most products requiring temperature control
- Certain hazardous materials
- Products banned by Amazon's general selling policies

### Products Requiring Approval
- Some categories require pre-approval before using FBA
- Hazmat products may be eligible with proper documentation (Safety Data Sheets)
- Check the FBA product restrictions page in Seller Central before sending inventory

### Products with Special Requirements
- **Expiration-dated items**: Must have at least 90 days of shelf life remaining upon arrival at the fulfillment center
- **Meltable products**: Restricted seasonally (typically cannot be sent April through October)
- **Glass/fragile items**: Must meet enhanced packaging requirements
- **Batteries**: Require special hazmat classification and documentation

## Inventory Management

### FBA Dashboard
- Central hub for all FBA inventory management
- Forecasting demand and restock recommendations
- Tracking storage utilization and fees
- Monitoring shipment status
- Identifying slow-moving and excess inventory

### Inventory Performance Index (IPI)
- Score from 0-1,000 measuring how efficiently you manage FBA inventory
- Factors: Excess inventory, sell-through rate, stranded inventory, in-stock rate
- **Low IPI scores** can result in storage limits being applied to your account
- **High IPI scores** earn higher storage limits and better account standing
- Checked quarterly

### Restock Recommendations
- Amazon provides automated restock suggestions based on sales velocity and lead times
- Available in Seller Central and the Amazon Seller App
- Consider seasonal demand patterns when evaluating recommendations

### Stranded Inventory
- Inventory in fulfillment centers without an active listing
- Does not generate sales but still incurs storage fees
- Fix by relisting the product or creating a removal order
- Monitor the Stranded Inventory report regularly

### Multi-Channel Fulfillment (MCF)
- Use FBA inventory to fulfill orders from other sales channels (your website, eBay, etc.)
- Higher per-unit fees than standard FBA fulfillment
- No Prime branding on non-Amazon orders

## FBA Reimbursements

### When Amazon Owes You
Amazon may reimburse sellers when:
- Inventory is lost or damaged at a fulfillment center
- Customer returns are not returned to your inventory
- Incorrect fees are charged
- Shipment discrepancies (received quantity differs from sent quantity)

### How to Claim
- File claims through Seller Central under **Reports > Fulfillment > Inventory Adjustments**
- Amazon may auto-reimburse for some situations
- Track your inventory closely; discrepancies do occur
- Time limits apply for filing claims (typically 18 months)
- Third-party tools exist to help identify and file reimbursement claims

## FBA vs. Merchant Fulfillment: When to Choose FBA

### Choose FBA When:
- Your products are small-to-medium sized with healthy margins
- You want Prime eligibility and Buy Box advantage
- You do not have efficient fulfillment capabilities
- You sell in multiple categories and want simplified operations
- Customer service and returns handling is a burden

### Consider Merchant Fulfillment When:
- Your products are very large/heavy (FBA fees may be prohibitive)
- Your margins are thin and FBA fees would eliminate profit
- You have an efficient warehouse and shipping operation
- You need to include custom inserts or packaging
- You sell very low-velocity items (storage fees would accumulate)

### Revenue Calculator
- Amazon provides a Revenue Calculator to compare FBA vs. merchant fulfillment costs
- Enter your product details to see side-by-side fee comparisons
- Available at sellercentral.amazon.com (search "Revenue Calculator")

## Key Takeaways

1. FBA gives you Prime eligibility, Buy Box advantages, and Amazon handles customer service/returns.
2. Shipping costs are approximately 70% less than premium carriers.
3. Every unit needs an FNSKU barcode, proper packaging, and must meet Amazon's prep requirements.
4. Use the Send to Amazon workflow for streamlined shipment creation.
5. Monitor storage fees closely -- aged inventory surcharges start at 181 days and increase at 365 days.
6. The FBA New Selection program offers significant discounts for launching new products.
7. Track your Inventory Performance Index (IPI) to maintain storage limits.
8. File reimbursement claims for lost/damaged inventory -- Amazon does make mistakes.
9. Use the Revenue Calculator to compare FBA vs. self-fulfillment before committing.
