# Pillar 1: Reactive Sourcing Guide

## Buy the Dip — Real-Time Amazon Price Drop Flips

Reactive sourcing is the foundation of A2A arbitrage. You monitor Amazon for sudden, significant price drops and act immediately to purchase inventory before the price recovers. This is a speed-dependent strategy where preparation and fast execution determine profitability.

---

## Core Concept

Amazon prices fluctuate constantly due to algorithmic repricing, inventory management, competitor actions, promotions, and errors. When a product's price drops significantly below its historical average, a window opens to buy low and resell at the recovered price. Your job is to detect these drops, evaluate them in seconds, and execute purchases before the opportunity disappears.

The typical lifecycle of a reactive flip:

1. Price drops (minutes to hours)
2. You detect and evaluate the drop (seconds to minutes)
3. You purchase inventory (immediate)
4. Price recovers to historical average (hours to days)
5. You list and sell at recovered price (days to weeks)
6. Profit realized after Amazon fees

---

## Decision Criteria Framework

Before purchasing any reactive deal, evaluate every item against these criteria in order. A failure at any step means you skip the deal.

### Evaluation Checklist

| # | Criterion | Threshold | Priority |
|---|-----------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | **Eligibility / Gating** | You can sell the item (no brand gating, category restrictions, or IP blocks) | Mandatory |
| 2 | **Sales Rank** | Under 50,000 in its parent category | Mandatory |
| 3 | **Price Recovery Potential** | Keepa 3-6 month chart confirms price was consistently higher | Mandatory |
| 4 | **ROI After All Fees** | Aim for 50%+ on quick flips; absolute minimum 10% or $5-10 profit | Mandatory |
| 5 | **Reason for the Drop** | Identifiable cause (clearance, algorithmic error, coupon stack, promotion) | Important |
| 6 | **Quantity Available** | Enough to buy meaningful quantity, but not so much supply that recovery stalls | Important |
| 7 | **Competition at Recovered Price** | Fewer than 5-8 FBA sellers at target sell price | Important |
| 8 | **FBA Fee Impact** | Fees don't consume more than 30-35% of sell price | Important |
| 9 | **Size / Weight** | Not oversized (higher FBA fees erode margins) | Consider |
| 10 | **Historical Volatility** | Price doesn't swing wildly (indicates unstable market) | Consider |

### Sales Rank Guidance by Category

| Category | Good Rank | Acceptable Rank | Avoid |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| Toys & Games | Under 10,000 | 10,000-30,000 | Over 50,000 |
| Kitchen & Dining | Under 15,000 | 15,000-40,000 | Over 50,000 |
| Health & Household | Under 20,000 | 20,000-50,000 | Over 75,000 |
| Beauty | Under 15,000 | 15,000-40,000 | Over 60,000 |
| Office Products | Under 10,000 | 10,000-30,000 | Over 40,000 |
| Electronics | Under 20,000 | 20,000-50,000 | Over 75,000 |
| Home & Kitchen | Under 25,000 | 25,000-50,000 | Over 75,000 |

### ROI Calculation Formula

```
ROI = ((Sell Price - FBA Fees - Purchase Price - Shipping to FBA) / Total Investment) x 100

Total Investment = Purchase Price + Tax + Shipping to FBA
Net Profit = Sell Price - FBA Fees - Total Investment
```

**Quick ROI targets:**

| Flip Speed | Minimum ROI | Ideal ROI |
|------------|-------------|-----------|
| Fast (under 7 days) | 10% or $5 minimum | 30-50% |
| Medium (7-30 days) | 25% | 50-75% |
| Slow (30+ days) | 40% | 75-100%+ |

## Keepa Deals Filter Settings

Configure Keepa Deals page with these filters for reactive sourcing:

| Setting | Value | Rationale |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| **Price Drop %** | 30% or greater | Filters out minor fluctuations |
| **Time Window** | Within last 24 hours | Fresh deals only |
| **Sales Rank** | 200,000 or less | Ensures reasonable demand |
| **Seller** | Amazon (as a seller) | Confirms legitimate Amazon inventory |
| **Condition** | New | A2A requires new condition |
| **Minimum ROI** | 10% or higher | Basic profitability floor |
| **Category** | Select 3-5 categories you know well | Focus beats breadth |

### Advanced Filter Additions

- Exclude products with fewer than 10 reviews (too risky, unknown demand)
- Exclude products with rating below 3.5 stars (return risk)
- Set minimum price at $15 (below this, fees eat margins)
- Set maximum price based on your capital (start with $75 cap)

## Standard Operating Procedure: 22-Step Reactive Sourcing Workflow

### Phase 1: Setup and Scanning (Steps 1-5)

| Step | Action | Time |
|------|--------|------|
| 1 | Open Keepa Deals page with saved filters | 30 sec |
| 2 | Open FBA Revenue Calculator in separate tab | 15 sec |
| 3 | Open your tracking spreadsheet | 15 sec |
| 4 | Open your Amazon buying account (non-Prime, logged in) | 15 sec |
| 5 | Scan Keepa Deals results, sort by drop percentage | 2-5 min |

### Phase 2: Evaluation (Steps 6-12)

| Step | Action | Time |
|------|--------|------|
| 6 | Click on a promising deal to open the Keepa product page | 10 sec |
| 7 | Check the 3-month and 6-month Keepa price chart: Is the current price significantly below the average? | 30 sec |
| 8 | Verify the price was stable at the higher level (not a one-time spike) | 15 sec |
| 9 | Check sales rank history: Is demand consistent? Any seasonal patterns? | 30 sec |
| 10 | Check for gating or brand restrictions on the ASIN in Seller Central | 30 sec |
| 11 | Run the numbers in FBA Calculator: enter buy price, expected sell price, calculate net profit and ROI | 1 min |
| 12 | If ROI meets threshold, proceed. If not, return to Step 5 for next deal. | 10 sec |

### Phase 3: Purchase Execution (Steps 13-17)

| Step | Action | Time |
|------|--------|------|
| 13 | Navigate to the Amazon product page on your buying account | 15 sec |
| 14 | Confirm the price matches what Keepa showed (prices can change fast) | 10 sec |
| 15 | Select quantity (start conservative: 2-5 units for first purchase) | 10 sec |
| 16 | Complete purchase using NON-PRIME account (standard shipping) | 1 min |
| 17 | Save the order confirmation, screenshot the current price | 30 sec |

### Phase 4: Documentation and Tracking (Steps 18-22)

| Step | Action | Time |
|------|--------|------|
| 18 | Log the purchase in your tracking spreadsheet (ASIN, product name, buy price, quantity, order ID, expected sell price) | 2 min |
| 19 | Set a Keepa price tracking alert on the ASIN for price recovery notification | 30 sec |
| 20 | When items arrive, inspect condition and prep for FBA shipment | Varies |
| 21 | Create FBA shipment, apply labels, ship to Amazon warehouse | Varies |
| 22 | After sale completes, log final sell price, actual profit, and conduct post-mortem analysis | 5 min |

## Good vs. Bad Reactive Flips

### Good Flip Example

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Product** | Kitchen stand mixer |
| **Normal Price** | $120 (consistent for 4+ months on Keepa) |
| **Drop Price** | $60 (50% drop) |
| **Sales Rank** | 5,000 in Kitchen & Dining |
| **FBA Fees** | ~$22 at $120 sell price |
| **Total Investment** | $60 + $5 shipping = $65 |
| **Net Profit** | $120 - $22 - $65 = $33 |
| **ROI** | 50.7% |
| **Why it works** | Strong rank, clear price history, significant margin, known brand |

### Good Flip Example 2

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Product** | Premium vitamin supplement (90-count) |
| **Normal Price** | $38 (stable 6 months) |
| **Drop Price** | $19 (50% coupon stack) |
| **Sales Rank** | 2,200 in Health & Household |
| **FBA Fees** | ~$9 at $38 sell price |
| **Total Investment** | $19 + $3 shipping = $22 |
| **Net Profit** | $38 - $9 - $22 = $7 |
| **ROI** | 31.8% |
| **Why it works** | Excellent rank, coupon-driven (temporary), small/light item, fast recovery expected |

### Bad Flip Example 1: Chasing Everything

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Product** | Obscure phone case |
| **Normal Price** | $15 |
| **Drop Price** | $8 |
| **Sales Rank** | 180,000 in Cell Phones & Accessories |
| **Problem** | Rank is too high (slow sales), margin after FBA fees is under $2, hundreds of competing sellers |
| **Outcome** | Item sits in FBA for months, storage fees eat any profit |

### Bad Flip Example 2: Over-Buying

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Product** | Trending toy |
| **Drop Price** | Great deal, 40% off |
| **Quantity Bought** | 50 units |
| **Problem** | Bought too many, price recovers but 15 other sellers also found the deal, race to the bottom |
| **Outcome** | Forced to lower price, ROI drops from projected 60% to 8% |

### Bad Flip Example 3: Prime Abuse

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Product** | Any product |
| **Purchase Method** | Used Prime account for free 2-day shipping |
| **Problem** | Amazon receipts show Prime shipping, violates TOS |
| **Outcome** | Potential suspension of both buyer and seller accounts |

### Bad Flip Example 4: Poor Record-Keeping

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Product** | Brand-name health product |
| **Problem** | Didn't save invoice, got inauthentic complaint |
| **Outcome** | Cannot provide supplier documentation, listing deactivated, potential account suspension |

## Amazon TOS Compliance for Reactive Sourcing

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **No Prime for Sourcing** | Purchase using a non-Prime account only |
| **No Drop Shipping** | You must receive and physically handle the product before reselling |
| **Accurate Condition** | List item in its actual condition (New means factory sealed) |
| **Fair Pricing** | Price at or near historical market value; do not price gouge |
| **Order Volume** | Keep buying patterns reasonable; 50+ units of one item in one order raises flags |
| **Documentation** | Save every invoice and order confirmation |
| **Returns** | Do not excessively return items on your buying account |

## Timing and Frequency

### Best Times to Scan for Deals

| Time Window | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| 6:00-8:00 AM ET | Overnight algorithmic repricing creates fresh drops |
| 11:00 AM-1:00 PM ET | Lightning deals and daily deals launch |
| 9:00-11:00 PM ET | End-of-day clearance pricing, less competition from other flippers |
| Weekday mornings | Generally more algorithmic drops than weekends |

### Scanning Frequency

- **Minimum**: 2 sessions per day (morning + evening), 30 minutes each
- **Optimal**: 3-4 sessions per day, 20-30 minutes each
- **During major sales events** (Prime Day, Black Friday): continuous monitoring

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Buying before checking Keepa history** — A "low" price might actually be the new normal
2. **Ignoring FBA fees on heavy/oversized items** — Fees on large items can exceed 40% of sale price
3. **Not checking for gating first** — Wasted time analyzing a product you cannot sell
4. **Buying maximum quantity on first flip of a product** — Start with 2-5 units to validate
5. **Emotional purchasing** — The deal feels urgent; your analysis should not feel rushed
6. **Underpricing to sell fast** — Patience usually recovers more profit than panic-selling
7. **Not having a buying account ready** — Setting up during a deal wastes critical minutes
8. **Skipping the post-mortem** — Every flip teaches you something; log the outcome
