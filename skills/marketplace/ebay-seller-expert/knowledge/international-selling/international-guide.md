# International Selling — Complete eBay Guide

## Overview

Selling internationally on eBay significantly expands your buyer pool. eBay provides two primary programs to simplify cross-border sales: the **Global Shipping Program (GSP)** and **eBay International Shipping (eIS)**. You can also ship directly to international buyers. Each approach has different tradeoffs in cost, control, and buyer experience.

---

## Global Shipping Program (GSP)

### How GSP Works

1. You list an item on eBay US and opt into GSP.
2. An international buyer purchases your item.
3. You ship the item to **eBay's domestic shipping center** (located in Erlanger, Kentucky, US).
4. eBay's partner (Pitney Bowes) handles international shipping, customs declaration, duties, and taxes.
5. The buyer receives the item internationally.

**Key principle:** You ship domestically; eBay handles everything international.

### Setting Up GSP

**Navigation:** My eBay > Account > Site preferences > Shipping preferences > Offer Global Shipping Program

Or on a per-listing basis:
- When creating/editing a listing > Shipping > International shipping > Select "Global Shipping Program."

### GSP Fees and Costs

| Cost Component | Paid By | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic shipping (to KY center) | Seller | Your normal domestic shipping cost |
| International shipping | Buyer | Calculated at checkout based on destination |
| Import charges (duties, taxes) | Buyer | Calculated at checkout based on destination |
| eBay international fee | Seller | 1.65% of total sale amount (deducted from payout) |

### GSP Buyer Experience

The buyer sees at checkout:
- Item price.
- Shipping cost (international leg).
- Import charges (duties and taxes).
- Total cost — no surprises at delivery.

This upfront transparency is a major advantage. Buyers in non-GSP transactions may face unexpected customs charges at delivery, leading to refusals and returns.

### GSP Seller Protections

| Protection | Detail |
|---|---|
| Shipping responsibility | Your responsibility ends when the item is delivered to the GSP center in Kentucky |
| Tracking | eBay provides end-to-end tracking |
| Customs issues | eBay handles all customs declarations and issues |
| Damage in international transit | Buyer's claim is handled by the GSP, not charged to your account |
| Returns | International returns go through the GSP return process |
| Defects | Late shipments are only measured on the domestic leg (to the GSP center) |

### GSP Eligible Items

Most items are eligible. Items NOT eligible for GSP include:
- Items over 150 lbs or over 108 inches (longest side) or 165 inches (length + girth).
- Hazardous materials.
- Items prohibited for export (weapons, certain chemicals, etc.).
- Live plants and animals.
- Perishable food items.
- Vehicles and large machinery.
- Items on the US export control list.

### GSP Eligible Destinations

GSP supports shipping to approximately 100+ countries. Major markets include:
- Canada, United Kingdom, Germany, France, Australia, Japan, Italy, Spain, Brazil, Mexico, and many others.
- The full list is available during listing setup when selecting GSP.

## eBay International Shipping (eIS)

### How eIS Works

eBay International Shipping is eBay's newer international shipping program that is replacing GSP for many sellers.

1. You list items on eBay US with standard US shipping.
2. eBay automatically makes your listings visible to international buyers.
3. International buyer purchases — you ship to an eBay domestic hub.
4. eBay handles international transit, customs, and delivery.

### Key Differences: eIS vs. GSP

| Feature | GSP | eBay International Shipping |
|---|---|---|
| Seller opt-in | Per-listing or account-wide | Account-wide (eBay may auto-enroll sellers) |
| Domestic hub location | Erlanger, KY | Multiple US locations |
| Buyer cost transparency | Duties/taxes shown at checkout | Duties/taxes shown at checkout |
| Seller international fee | 1.65% | 1.65% |
| Return handling | Through GSP process | Through eBay's return process |
| Coverage | ~100 countries | ~200+ countries |
| Item eligibility | Manual opt-in | Automatic for eligible items |
| Seller protections | End at domestic hub | End at domestic hub |

### Setting Up eIS

**Navigation:** Seller Hub > Growth > International shipping, or My eBay > Account > Shipping preferences

eBay may automatically enroll eligible sellers. You can opt out if desired, but opting in is generally recommended.

## Listing Internationally (Direct Shipping)

### When to Ship Directly

- You have experience with international shipping.
- You want full control over shipping costs and carrier selection.
- The destination country is not covered by GSP/eIS.
- You can offer more competitive shipping rates than GSP/eIS.

### Setting Up International Shipping on a Listing

**Navigation:** Create listing > Shipping > International shipping

1. **Select shipping method:** Choose a carrier and service (USPS First Class International, Priority Mail International, UPS Worldwide, FedEx International, etc.).
2. **Set shipping cost:** Flat rate or calculated.
3. **Select countries:** Ship to specific countries, regions, or worldwide.
4. **Exclude countries:** Block specific countries you do not want to ship to.

### International Shipping Methods (US Origin)

| Service | Weight Limit | Typical Transit | Tracking | Insurance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| USPS First Class International | 4 lbs | 7–21 days | Limited (some countries) | Not included |
| USPS Priority Mail International | 70 lbs | 6–10 days | Full tracking | Included (up to $200) |
| USPS Priority Mail Express International | 70 lbs | 3–5 days | Full tracking | Included (up to $200) |
| UPS Worldwide Expedited | 150 lbs | 3–5 days | Full tracking | Optional |
| UPS Worldwide Express | 150 lbs | 1–3 days | Full tracking | Optional |
| FedEx International Economy | 150 lbs | 4–6 days | Full tracking | Optional |
| FedEx International Priority | 150 lbs | 1–3 days | Full tracking | Optional |

### Customs Forms

When shipping directly:
- **Customs declaration form** is required for all international shipments.
- eBay pre-fills customs forms when you buy eBay shipping labels.
- Include: item description, quantity, value, country of origin, HS tariff code (if known).
- Mark as "Merchandise" for commercial sales.

## International Return Policies

### Options for International Returns

| Option | Description | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| No international returns | Buyer cannot return; may increase cases | Not recommended |
| 30-day returns (buyer pays return shipping) | Standard policy; buyer bears return cost | Recommended minimum |
| 60-day returns (buyer pays return shipping) | Extended window; more buyer-friendly | Good for higher-value items |
| Free returns (you pay return shipping) | Maximum buyer confidence; highest cost to seller | Only if margins support it |

### GSP/eIS Returns

- Buyer initiates return through eBay.
- Return shipping label is generated through the program.
- Item is returned through the international shipping hub.
- You receive the return domestically.
- Return shipping costs depend on your return policy.

### Direct International Returns

- You are responsible for providing a return shipping label or address.
- International return shipping can be expensive ($15–$50+).
- Consider offering partial refunds instead of full returns for low-value items.

## Currency Conversion

### How It Works

- eBay listings are priced in the listing site's currency (e.g., USD for eBay.com).
- International buyers see an estimated price in their local currency at checkout.
- eBay handles currency conversion for Managed Payments transactions.
- Conversion rates are updated regularly and include a small spread.

### Multi-Currency Listings

- If you list on international eBay sites (eBay.co.uk, eBay.de, etc.), you list in the local currency.
- Payouts are converted to your bank account's currency.
- eBay's conversion fee is included in the exchange rate spread.

## VAT / GST Implications

### Selling to the European Union

| Threshold | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Under EU thresholds | eBay collects and remits VAT on your behalf for items under certain values |
| Over EU thresholds | You may need to register for VAT in EU countries |
| Items shipped from within EU | VAT registration likely required |

eBay now collects and remits VAT for:
- B2C sales of goods valued at EUR 150 or less imported into the EU.
- Sales facilitated through the eBay platform to EU consumers.

### Selling to the United Kingdom

- eBay collects and remits UK VAT on goods valued at GBP 135 or less.
- For goods over GBP 135, the buyer is responsible for import VAT and duties.

### Selling to Australia

- eBay collects and remits GST (10%) on all goods sold to Australian consumers valued at AUD 1,000 or less.

### Selling to Canada

- GST/HST may apply. eBay collects sales tax on behalf of sellers for Canadian sales as required by law.

### General VAT/GST Rules for US Sellers

- For most US-based sellers, eBay handles VAT/GST collection and remittance automatically.
- You generally do NOT need to register for VAT/GST in foreign countries if you are only shipping from the US through eBay.
- Consult a tax professional if you have inventory stored or operations in foreign countries.

## Excluded Countries

### How to Exclude Countries

**Navigation:** Create/edit listing > Shipping > International shipping > Exclude shipping locations

### Common Countries to Exclude

| Country/Region | Common Reason for Exclusion |
|---|---|
| Russia | Sanctions and trade restrictions |
| Countries under US sanctions | Legal compliance (Iran, North Korea, Syria, Cuba, etc.) |
| Certain South American countries | High shipping costs, customs delays, high fraud risk |
| Certain African countries | Delivery reliability concerns, limited tracking |
| PO Boxes in some countries | Carrier limitations |

### Account-Level Exclusions

**Navigation:** My eBay > Account > Site preferences > Shipping preferences > Exclude shipping locations

Set exclusions once and they apply to all new listings automatically.

## Shipping to APO/FPO/DPO Addresses

### What They Are

- **APO:** Army Post Office
- **FPO:** Fleet Post Office
- **DPO:** Diplomatic Post Office

These are US military and diplomatic addresses that use US ZIP codes but are located overseas.

### How to Ship to APO/FPO

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| Carrier | USPS only (UPS/FedEx cannot deliver to APO/FPO) |
| Shipping rate | Domestic USPS rates apply |
| Customs form | Required for all APO/FPO shipments |
| Transit time | 7–21 days (can be longer during deployments) |
| Tracking | Tracking available but may have gaps after leaving US |
| Size/weight limits | Standard USPS limits apply |

### APO/FPO Tips

- Ship via USPS Priority Mail for better tracking.
- Allow extra handling time — delivery can be unpredictable.
- Items may go through military screening, causing delays.
- Buyers at APO/FPO addresses are generally understanding of longer transit times.

## International Payment Processing

### Managed Payments and International Sales

- eBay processes all international payments through Managed Payments.
- Buyers pay in their local currency; eBay converts and deposits in your local currency.
- No additional payment processing setup is needed for international sales.

### International Fee

| Fee | Rate |
|---|---|
| International fee (GSP/eIS) | 1.65% of total sale amount |
| International fee (direct shipping) | 1.65% of total sale amount |

This fee is in addition to the standard final value fee.

### Payment Holds on International Orders

International orders may have longer payment hold periods due to:
- Longer transit times (funds held until delivery confirmed).
- Higher dispute rates on cross-border transactions.
- Customs delays affecting delivery confirmation.

## Best Practices for International Selling

### Listing Optimization

1. **Use clear, internationally understandable item specifics.** Include size conversions (US 10 = EU 44).
2. **Provide dimensions in both inches/cm and lbs/kg** where relevant.
3. **Avoid colloquialisms** in descriptions that may not translate well.
4. **Include voltage/plug type** for electronics.

### Shipping Best Practices

1. **Use GSP or eIS** unless you have a specific reason to ship directly.
2. **Always use tracked shipping** for international orders.
3. **Package items securely** — international transit is rougher on packages.
4. **Accurately declare customs values** — underdeclaring is illegal and can result in fines.
5. **Include packing slip** inside the package with order details.

### Risk Management

1. **Require signature confirmation** for items over $750.
2. **Take photos of the item and packaging** before shipping.
3. **Keep all shipping receipts and customs documentation.**
4. **Consider excluding high-fraud-risk destinations** if you sell high-value items.
5. **Monitor international return rates** — if a specific country has high returns, consider excluding it.

### Growing International Sales

1. **Start with GSP/eIS** to test international demand with minimal risk.
2. **Analyze Seller Hub traffic reports** — filter by buyer country to see where demand comes from.
3. **Research Terapeak** for international demand on your product categories.
4. **Consider listing on international eBay sites** (UK, DE, AU) for maximum visibility.
5. **Time listings for international buying hours** — eBay's Best Match considers recency.
