# Walmart Marketplace Taxes & Payments Guide

## Overview

Walmart Marketplace handles payment processing, tax collection, and financial reporting for sellers. Understanding payout cycles, payment holds, tax obligations, and reporting tools is essential for managing cash flow and staying compliant.

---

## Payout Processing

### Payment Cycle
- **Frequency**: Payouts occur biweekly (every 14 days) by default
- **Settlement cutoff**: The payment period closes at 11:59 PM on alternating Fridays (Pacific time)
- **Payment timeline**: Payments are made to sellers no later than the 7th day after the end of each period
- **Order-to-payout timing**: In most cases, payments for orders are received in the payout cycle following the date they shipped (approximately 28 days after order ships for new sellers)
- **Alternate arrangements**: Custom payment frequencies may be available in some cases

### What Is Included in Payouts
Each payout calculation includes:
- Revenue from shipped order notices during the payment cycle
- Deductions for refunded orders (seller or Walmart-processed)
- Order adjustments and credits/debits
- Outstanding negative balances carried over from previous cycles
- Walmart referral fees and affiliate charges
- WFS fulfillment and storage fees (if applicable)

### Payment Methods

#### Marketplace Wallet (Recommended)
- Embedded deposit account powered by JPMorgan Chase Bank
- Payouts deposited directly into Seller Center
- Link an approved U.S. business bank account registered under the same business name
- Transfer funds via ACH with no hidden fees
- FDIC insurance on deposited funds up to $250,000.00 per depositor at JPMorgan Chase Bank

#### Third-Party Providers
Depending on country of incorporation, sellers can choose from:
- Hyperwallet
- Payoneer
- PingPong
- LianLian
- Airwallex
- WorldFirst
- NetEase

Note: Third-party providers may charge additional fees that Walmart does not cover.

### WFS Billing
- WFS charges (fulfillment fees, storage fees, customer refunds) can be paid via:
  - Business credit card
  - U.S. bank account through ACH
- Charges are automatically deducted at the end of the Marketplace settlement cycle

## Payment Hold Policy (New Sellers)

### Overview
All new Marketplace sellers are subject to a payment hold, technically called a "rolling delay of payments." A portion of payments is delayed or held back during the initial period.

### Hold Duration by Region

| Seller Location | Rolling Delay Period |
|----------------|---------------------|
| United States | Up to 14 days |
| Non-U.S. sellers | Up to 21 days |

### Conditions for Hold Release
The payment hold ends when BOTH of these conditions are met:
1. **90 days** have passed since the first order shipped
2. The seller has received **$7,500** in total payments from Walmart Marketplace

Both conditions must be satisfied simultaneously. Even if 90 days have elapsed, the hold remains until the $7,500 threshold is also reached, and vice versa.

### How It Works
- The rolling delay starts on the day of your first shipped order
- During the hold period, you receive partial payments
- The remaining held amount is released once both conditions are satisfied
- This policy is designed to establish sales history and credibility

## Payment Reports and Statements

### Accessing Payment Information
- Log into Seller Center and navigate to Reports under the Payments and Fees section
- Payment statements are available on the Statements page
- Settlement cutoff dates are listed under Payment Information on the Statements page

### Settlement Report Contents
- **Row 3 (Summary)**: Payment period dates, total amount paid, and the account the payment was deposited to
- **Subsequent rows**: Line-item records of every payment and deduction within the selected payment period
- Reports detail all shipped orders, refunds, adjustments, fees, and credits

### WFS Payment Reports
- Separate reporting available for WFS-specific charges
- Includes fulfillment fees, storage fees, and return-related charges
- Accessible through the WFS section of Seller Center

### Payout Frequency Verification
- Check the "Walmart Marketplace Retailer Agreement" for your payment frequency terms
- Review dates on previous payment reports to confirm your payout cycle

## Tax Information

### Marketplace Facilitator Tax
- Walmart acts as a marketplace facilitator in states where applicable
- As a marketplace facilitator, Walmart collects and remits sales tax on behalf of sellers in applicable jurisdictions
- Sellers should verify their obligations in states where marketplace facilitator laws do not apply

### Tax Classifications
- Sellers must provide accurate tax classification during onboarding
- Tax profile information must be kept current in Seller Center
- Tax ID updates must occur before or during the tax year being reported (updates submitted after the tax year are not accepted)

### Updating Tax and Business Information
- Update tax details through Seller Center account settings
- Ensure business name, address, EIN/SSN, and entity type are accurate
- Changes to tax information may require verification

## Form 1099-K

### Eligibility Requirements
You will receive Form 1099-K if ALL of the following are true during the calendar year:
- You are registered with Walmart Marketplace as a W-9 entity
- You shipped more than **200 orders**
- Your gross (unadjusted) sales exceeded **$20,000**
- You provided complete tax profile information in Seller Center

Note: Filing requirements for some states may differ from federal requirements.

### What the Form Reports
- Summary of payments received from Walmart Marketplace orders in the calendar year
- Reports sales only -- does not include adjustments for credits, cash equivalents, discount amounts, fees, or refunded amounts
- Sellers must reconcile 1099-K figures with their own records, accounting for fees and refunds

### When It Is Issued
- Form 1099-K is issued by January 31 of the following year
- Issued through Walmart's payment processor

### How to Access Form 1099-K

#### Payoneer Users
- Access forms via tax1099.com
- Password format: Eight characters combining your legal/first name (4 characters, lowercase) plus your EIN's last four digits

#### Hyperwallet Users
- Log into Pay Portal
- Navigate to Resources then Tax Documents
- Download from Available Year-End Tax Forms

### Self-Reporting Obligation
- Sellers who do not meet the 1099-K threshold must self-report their sales figures directly to the IRS
- All income from Marketplace sales is reportable regardless of whether a 1099-K is issued

## Fee Structure

### Referral Fees
- Walmart charges category-specific referral fees ranging from 6% to 15% of the sale price (excluding shipping)
- No monthly subscription fees or setup fees
- Sellers only pay when a sale is made

#### Category Examples
| Category | Referral Fee |
|----------|-------------|
| Apparel & Accessories | 15% |
| Electronics (under $100) | 10% |
| Home & Garden | 12% |
| Jewelry (over $250) | 6% |
| Grocery | 8% |
| Most categories | ~15% |

### WFS Fees (If Using Walmart Fulfillment Services)
- **Fulfillment fees**: Based on item weight and dimensions, starting at $3.45 for items up to 1 lb
- **Storage fees**: Calculated based on unit volume (length x width x height / 1,728 = cubic feet) and storage duration

### New-Seller Savings Program (2026)
- 25% off fulfillment fees
- 50% off storage fees
- Up to $2,000 in total savings

### Pro Seller Fee Discounts
- Advanced Sellers: 5% referral fee discount on Pro Listings
- Pro Sellers: 10% referral fee discount on Pro Listings
- Applies to listings meeting fast-and-free shipping and competitive price requirements

## Final Settlement Payout Policy

- When a seller's account is closed (voluntarily or through termination), a final settlement payout is processed
- This covers any remaining balance including shipped orders, pending refunds, and fee adjustments
- The final payout timeline may be extended to account for potential returns and chargebacks

---

## Key Reminders

1. Payouts are biweekly by default, with payments made within 7 days after each period closes
2. New sellers face a payment hold until both 90 days have passed and $7,500 in payments have been received
3. The Marketplace Wallet (powered by JPMorgan Chase) offers fee-free ACH transfers
4. Form 1099-K is issued if you shipped more than 200 orders AND had more than $20,000 in gross sales
5. Referral fees range from 6-15% with no monthly subscription
6. Keep tax profile information current; updates cannot be applied retroactively to prior tax years
