# Amazon Product Listings & Catalog Guide

## Overview

Your product listing is the single most important asset on Amazon. It determines whether customers find your product (search ranking), click on it (impressions to clicks), and buy it (conversion rate). This guide covers listing creation, optimization, A+ Content, image requirements, backend keywords, variation relationships, and experimentation tools.

---

## Creating Product Listings

### Two Methods for Listing Products

#### 1. Individual Listing (Add a Product)
- Best for adding a few products at a time
- Done through Seller Central: **Catalog > Add Products**
- Search Amazon's catalog to match an existing product or create a new listing
- Fill in all required fields (title, brand, category, price, condition, etc.)

#### 2. Bulk Upload (Inventory File Upload)
- Best for adding many products at once
- Download the category-specific flat file template from Seller Central
- Fill in all required columns (and recommended columns for optimization)
- Upload via **Catalog > Add Products via Upload**
- Useful for large catalog management and bulk updates

### Matching an Existing Product vs. Creating New
- If the product already exists in Amazon's catalog, you match to the existing ASIN
- If the product is new to Amazon, you create a new ASIN
- You cannot create a duplicate ASIN for a product that already exists
- Brand Registry enrolled sellers have more control over their product detail pages

## Product Detail Page Elements

### Product Title
**The most important element for search visibility and click-through rate.**

#### Requirements (as of January 2025):
- Maximum **200 characters** including spaces for most categories (some categories have lower limits)
- Special characters like !, $, ?, _, {, }, ^, are **not allowed** unless part of the brand name
- The same word may not appear more than **twice** in the title
- No promotional language (e.g., "sale," "free shipping," "best seller")
- No ALL CAPS (capitalize the first letter of each word)

#### Best Practices:
- **Front-load** the most important keywords and attributes
- Include: Brand Name, Product Type/Name, Key Feature, Size/Quantity, Color/Variant
- Put the most search-relevant terms first (Amazon's algorithm weights the beginning of the title more heavily)
- Write for humans first, algorithm second -- it needs to be readable
- Target the primary keyword as close to the front as possible

#### Example Formula:
```
[Brand] [Product Type] [Key Feature] [Size/Quantity] [Color/Material] - [Differentiator]
```

### Bullet Points (Key Product Features)
**Amazon allows up to 5 bullet points** for most categories.

#### Best Practices:
- Use ALL 5 bullets -- each is an opportunity for keywords and persuasion
- Start each bullet with a **capitalized benefit keyword** (e.g., "DURABLE CONSTRUCTION:")
- Address a different benefit/feature in each bullet
- Answer the buyer's "so what?" -- explain outcomes, not just features
- Include relevant keywords naturally
- Keep each bullet to 200-250 characters for optimal readability
- Address common customer questions and objections
- Include credible proof points (materials, certifications, dimensions)

#### Order of Bullets (Recommended):
1. Primary benefit / unique selling proposition
2. Key feature with specifications
3. Quality/durability/materials
4. Size/compatibility/what's included
5. Guarantee/warranty/customer satisfaction

### Product Description
- Up to **2,000 characters** for most categories
- Supports basic HTML formatting (bold, line breaks, lists)
- **Replaced by A+ Content** for Brand Registry sellers (A+ Content takes priority)
- Include keywords not covered in title or bullets
- Tell the product's story and address remaining buyer concerns
- Use short paragraphs and formatting for readability

### Backend Keywords (Search Terms)
**Invisible to customers but indexed by Amazon's search algorithm.**

#### Requirements:
- Maximum **249 bytes** (essentially 249 characters for English)
- Entered in Seller Central under the "Keywords" tab when editing a listing

#### Best Practices:
- Do NOT repeat words already in your title, bullets, or description -- Amazon indexes those automatically
- Include synonyms, alternative names, abbreviations, and common misspellings
- Use lowercase only (Amazon is not case-sensitive)
- Separate words with spaces (no commas, semicolons, or special separators needed)
- Do not include brand names of competitors (policy violation)
- Do not include ASINs
- Do not include subjective claims ("best," "cheapest")
- Focus on expanding recall: alternate terms buyers might search for

#### What to Include:
- Synonyms (couch = sofa, bag = purse = handbag)
- Abbreviations (USB-C, LED, BPA)
- Alternate spellings (grey/gray, colour/color)
- Related use cases (camping, office, gift)
- Common misspellings of your product type (if applicable)
- Spanish or other language terms if relevant to your market

## Image Requirements

### Main Image (MAIN)
- **Pure white background** (RGB 255, 255, 255)
- Product must fill **85% or more** of the image frame
- **No text, logos, watermarks, or graphics** on the main image
- No props, accessories, or packaging unless they are part of the product
- **Professional quality** -- sharp, well-lit, high resolution
- Minimum 1,000 pixels on the longest side (recommended 2,000+ pixels for zoom functionality)
- Accepted formats: JPEG (.jpg), TIFF (.tif), PNG (.png), GIF (.gif)
- The product must be the actual item (no illustrations or drawings for the main image)

### Secondary Images (Additional Images)
- You can upload up to **8 additional images** (plus 1 video), for a total of 9 media slots
- Only the first **7 images** (or 6 images + 1 video) display on the listing
- Secondary images allow text overlays, infographics, lifestyle shots, and comparison charts
- Minimum 1,000 pixels on the longest side

#### Recommended Secondary Image Strategy:
1. **Infographic image**: Key features with callout text
2. **Lifestyle image**: Product in use by a person in a real setting
3. **Scale/size reference**: Product next to a common object for scale
4. **Detail/close-up**: Texture, material quality, craftsmanship
5. **What's in the box**: All included items laid out
6. **Comparison chart**: Your product vs. alternatives (without naming competitors)
7. **Back/alternate angle**: Different perspective of the product

### Product Video
- Highly recommended -- significantly improves conversion
- Must be high quality (at least 720p, ideally 1080p)
- No external website URLs or calls to action outside Amazon
- Show the product in use, demonstrate features, address common questions
- Keep to 30-90 seconds for optimal engagement

## A+ Content (Enhanced Brand Content)

### What Is A+ Content?
A+ Content replaces the standard product description with rich multimedia content including enhanced images, customized text layouts, comparison charts, and videos. Available to Brand Registry enrolled sellers.

### Types of A+ Content

#### Basic A+ Content
- Enhanced product descriptions with formatted text and images
- Customized image and text placement modules
- Shoppable product-comparison charts
- **Cost**: Free for eligible sellers
- **Impact**: Can increase sales by up to 8% according to Amazon data

#### Premium A+ Content
- Larger images and embedded videos
- Interactive hotspots for feature exploration
- Clickable image or video carousels
- Q&A modules
- Shoppable content elements
- **Impact**: Can increase sales by up to 20% according to Amazon data

#### Brand Story
- A dedicated section to communicate brand values and mission
- Appears across all product listings for the brand
- Drives traffic to your Amazon Brand Store

### Eligibility
- Professional selling account ($39.99/month)
- Brand enrolled in Amazon Brand Registry with Brand Representative or Reseller role, OR
- Generic (unbranded) products in your catalog

### How to Create A+ Content
1. Go to **Seller Central > Advertising > A+ Content Manager**
2. Select content type (Basic, Premium, or Brand Story)
3. Choose and configure modules
4. Use AI-ready modules for content generation assistance
5. Apply content to brand-owned ASINs
6. Submit for review before publication

### A+ Content Best Practices
- Use high-quality images that complement (not duplicate) your main listing images
- Tell a story about the product and brand
- Address common customer objections and questions
- Include comparison charts if you have multiple products
- Optimize for mobile viewing (most Amazon shoppers are on mobile)
- Use clear, readable fonts and avoid cluttered layouts
- Test different A+ Content versions using Manage Your Experiments

## Variation Relationships (Parent-Child Listings)

### What Are Variations?
Variations allow you to group related products (different sizes, colors, flavors, etc.) under a single product detail page. The buyer sees a single listing and can select their preferred option.

### Structure
- **Parent ASIN**: The "shell" listing that groups variations together (not buyable on its own)
- **Child ASINs**: The actual purchasable product variants
- **Variation Theme**: The attribute that differentiates the children (e.g., Size, Color, SizeName-ColorName)

### Common Variation Themes
- Size
- Color
- Size-Color (combination)
- Flavor
- Scent
- Pattern
- Style
- Count/Pack Size

### Benefits of Variations
- All reviews are consolidated on one listing (massive advantage)
- Higher combined sales velocity improves search ranking
- Better customer experience -- easy to compare options
- One listing to manage instead of many separate listings

### Requirements
- All child products must be fundamentally the same product (just different in the variation attribute)
- Must use a variation theme supported by your product category
- Cannot combine unrelated products as variations (policy violation)
- Parent listing cannot be purchased directly

### How to Create Variations
1. **Individual**: When creating a listing, select the variation theme and add child options
2. **Flat file upload**: Use the variation-specific columns in the category template
3. **Add to existing**: Add new child ASINs to an existing parent-child group

## Manage Your Experiments (A/B Testing)

### What It Is
Amazon's built-in A/B testing tool that lets you test different versions of listing content to see which drives more sales.

### What You Can Test
- Product images
- Product titles
- Bullet points
- Product descriptions
- A+ Content
- Brand Story

### How It Works
1. Amazon randomly divides customers viewing your product into groups
2. Each group sees a different content version (A vs. B)
3. The system tracks performance (sales, conversion rate, units sold)
4. Once statistically significant data accumulates, a winner is declared
5. The winning content is automatically published

### Eligibility
- Professional selling account
- Brand Representative role for a Brand Registry enrolled brand
- Sufficient recent product traffic (low-traffic products may not qualify)
- Published A+ Content or Brand Story (for those experiment types)

### Key Features
- Multi-attribute testing (test title AND images simultaneously)
- Machine learning recommendations for titles and A+ Content
- Performance metrics: units sold, sales, conversion rate, projected one-year impact
- Pre-selected settings to optimize speed of results

### Best Practices
- Create a **substantially different** Version B (not minor tweaks)
- Allow experiments to run to completion (do not end early)
- Use pre-selected "to significance" settings for fastest results
- Recommended duration: **8-10 weeks** (can achieve results in 4 weeks with default settings)
- Run multi-attribute experiments for maximum impact
- Test one major hypothesis per experiment
- Use results to inform listing best practices across your catalog

## Listing Optimization Checklist

### Search Visibility (Getting Found)
- [ ] Primary keyword in the first 80 characters of the title
- [ ] 3-5 primary keywords targeted with high search volume
- [ ] 10-15 secondary keywords distributed across title, bullets, and description
- [ ] Backend search terms filled with synonyms and alternate terms (249 bytes max)
- [ ] Keywords mentioned once per listing (repeating does not help)
- [ ] Correct category/subcategory selected (browse node)

### Conversion (Getting the Sale)
- [ ] Main image on white background, product fills 85%+ of frame, 2000+ pixels
- [ ] 7+ high-quality secondary images including lifestyle, infographic, and detail shots
- [ ] Product video uploaded
- [ ] All 5 bullet points used with benefit-led formatting
- [ ] A+ Content published (if Brand Registry eligible)
- [ ] Competitive pricing
- [ ] Strong review count and rating

### Ongoing Optimization
- [ ] Monitor Sessions (traffic) and Unit Session Percentage (conversion rate) in Business Reports
- [ ] Run A/B tests via Manage Your Experiments on title, images, and A+ Content
- [ ] Update keywords based on search term reports and seasonal trends
- [ ] Respond to customer questions promptly (they appear on the listing)
- [ ] Monitor competitor listings for keyword and positioning ideas

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Title is king** for search visibility -- front-load keywords, stay under 200 characters.
2. **Use all 5 bullet points** -- each one is keyword real estate and persuasion space.
3. **Backend keywords** should expand reach with synonyms and alternatives, not repeat existing content.
4. **Main image must be on white background** with product filling 85%+ of the frame.
5. **A+ Content is free** for Brand Registry sellers and can boost sales by 8-20%.
6. **Variations consolidate reviews** -- use them whenever products differ by size, color, etc.
7. **A/B test everything** with Manage Your Experiments to continuously improve conversion.
8. **Keywords only need to appear once** in your listing -- repetition does not help ranking.
