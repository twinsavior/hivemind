# Getting Started with Walmart Marketplace: Complete Seller Guide

## Overview

Walmart Marketplace is an e-commerce platform that allows third-party sellers to list and sell products on Walmart.com alongside Walmart's own inventory. Walmart describes it as "The marketplace built for sellers, by sellers." Unlike Amazon, there are no monthly subscription fees or listing fees -- sellers only pay referral fees on completed sales.

---

## Requirements to Become a Walmart Seller

### Business Entity Requirements

- **Registered business required**: Walmart only allows legally registered businesses. Individual sellers using Social Security Numbers (SSN) are not accepted.
- **Accepted entity types**: LLCs, corporations, partnerships, and similar recognized business structures.
- **Prohibited entity types**: Non-profit organizations, sole proprietorships (excluding India), and Hindu Undivided Family (HUF).
- **U.S. Tax ID (EIN)** or Business License Number required (SSN is not accepted).
- **W-9 tax form** required (or W-8ECI/W-8BEN if incorporated outside the U.S.).
- Business name must match legal registration exactly -- mismatches cause verification delays.

### Product Requirements

- All products must have **GS1-verified GTIN/UPC prefix numbers**.
- Products must comply with Walmart's **Prohibited Products Policy**.
- Even one prohibited item in your catalog can trigger automatic denial.

### Fulfillment Requirements

- Must have a **B2C U.S. warehouse with returns capability**, OR enrollment in **Walmart Fulfillment Services (WFS)**.
- Ability to ship products within **1-2 business days**.
- U.S. return address required.
- Clear customer service plan for inquiries, returns, and refunds.

### Ecommerce Experience

- Walmart prioritizes businesses with a **proven track record** on platforms like Amazon, eBay, Shopify, or other online marketplaces.
- Must demonstrate sales history and estimated monthly revenue.
- High-quality, high-volume product portfolios are preferred.

## Required Documents

Gather these before applying:

1. Legal company name and address
2. Tax ID / EIN or License Number
3. U.S. bank account details
4. W-9 tax form (W-8ECI / W-8BEN for non-U.S.)
5. Product information (GTINs/UPCs, pricing)
6. Sales history from other marketplaces
7. Personal identification document
8. Customer service and return policy details
9. Copy of personal identification for identity verification

## Application Process

### Step 1: Access Signup
- Visit **marketplace.walmart.com** and click "Join Marketplace."
- Select your market and provide your country or region of incorporation.

### Step 2: Company Information
- Enter legal company name, address, and contact details.
- Ensure EIN/TIN matches company name exactly (mismatches cause verification delays).
- Website URL is optional but recommended for legitimacy verification.
- Tip: Using an Outlook email address may reportedly improve approval odds.

### Step 3: Ecommerce Experience
- List platforms you currently sell on.
- Provide sales history and monthly revenue estimates.
- Specify product categories and number of SKUs.
- Note: Selecting "DIY" as a category has been reported to increase acceptance likelihood.

### Step 4: Shipping & Fulfillment Setup
- Identify your shipping carriers (UPS, FedEx, USPS).
- Specify expected delivery times.
- Indicate how you handle returns.
- Choose between self-fulfillment or WFS.

### Step 5: Submit Application
- Review all details thoroughly before submitting.
- You will receive a confirmation email upon submission.
- Walmart runs a verification check confirming company information matches records.

### Approval Timeline
- The approval process typically takes **one to four weeks**.
- During review, Walmart verifies: company name/address against records, performance metrics from other marketplaces, shipping/customer service capability, and Prohibited Products Policy compliance.
- Check email regularly -- delayed responses extend approval timeline.

### Common Rejection Reasons
- Missing or inconsistent company records
- Tax information not matching IRS data
- Insufficient ecommerce experience or sales history
- Prohibited products in catalog
- Poor seller ratings or negative reviews on other platforms
- Inability to meet 1-2 day fulfillment requirement
- Prices significantly higher than competitors
- Incomplete forms or missing details

### Appeal Process
- Create a case with Seller Support via the Help button in Seller Center's menu bar.
- Provide additional records or documentation.
- Address the specific rejection reason directly.
- Note: The resolution Seller Support provides is considered a final decision.

## Onboarding in Seller Center

Once approved, complete the following steps to go live:

### Step 1: Business Verification (Up to 5 Days)

Provide and verify:
- Legal business name
- Entity type
- Business phone number
- State-issued business registration number (U.S.) or business license number (China)
- Personal ID of applicant or legal representative
- Location-specific documentation for non-U.S. businesses

**Important**: Tax registration must precisely match IRS records or government-issued documents.

### Step 2: Payment Setup (Must Complete Within 30 Days)

- Choose a **third-party payout provider** or **Marketplace Wallet**.
- Options vary by country of incorporation.
- If payment setup is not completed within 30 days, your account will be declined and you must reapply.
- You can only receive payouts from one provider at a time.
- Optional billing method can be added now or later.

**New Seller Payment Hold**: New sellers experience a payment hold after beginning to sell, allowing time to build sales history and credibility. Timeframes vary by country of incorporation.

**WFS Billing**: If using Walmart Fulfillment Services, add billing information. Walmart verifies it within 2-3 business days before fulfillment begins.

### Step 3: Store Setup

**Market Details**:
- Provide customer service information.
- International sellers can submit U.S. tax ID and legal business name.

**Fulfillment Configuration**:
- **Option A: Walmart Fulfillment Services (WFS)** -- Select "Ship with WFS" to have Walmart handle picking, packing, shipping, customer support, and returns.
- **Option B: Seller-Fulfilled Shipping** -- Select "Ship yourself" for self-packing and shipping, with protection against carrier delays using Walmart-calculated transit times.
- **Option C: Manual Shipping Settings** -- Set custom transit times; provide return labels, return centers, and fulfillment center details.

### Step 4: Catalog Setup

- Choose your item setup method (see Item Setup guide for full details).
- If importing from external marketplaces, use Import or Spreadsheet upload.
- If you are a Walmart Drop Ship Vendor or Warehouse supplier, import from Supplier One.
- **Private Label**: Request a GTIN exemption for products without product IDs.
- Account setup may take up to 4 hours to finalize after adding inventory.

### Step 5: Go Live

- After listing products and completing all setup, Walmart reviews account before activation.
- Items appear as "Draft" until account setup completes, inventory is added, and pricing is set.
- Bulk upload capability is only available after business verification, market details, payments, and shipping setup are finished.

## Two-Step Verification

- Two-step verification is a security feature available in Seller Center.
- Required for account protection.
- If you experience issues receiving one-time verification codes, troubleshooting resources are available in Seller Help.

---

## Selling Plans

Walmart Marketplace has **no monthly subscription fee and no listing fees**. This is a significant difference from Amazon's $39.99/month Professional plan. Sellers only pay referral fees on completed sales.

---

## Selling and Item Limits

- After your account is active, Walmart assigns selling and item limits based on sales volume, performance metrics, and customer feedback.
- Both the number of listed items and overall selling activity are tracked.
- If you hit your limits, your account pauses temporarily while Walmart evaluates.
- You can request limit increases when your account is eligible and in good standing.
- Walmart evaluates sales history, fulfillment performance, and customer feedback for increases.
