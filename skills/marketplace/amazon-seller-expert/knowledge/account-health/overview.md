# Amazon Account Health & Performance Metrics

## Overview

Amazon Account Health is a compliance measurement system that helps sellers assess their adherence to platform policies. It is accessed through the **Account Health Dashboard** in Seller Central and uses multiple metrics to evaluate seller performance across three key areas: Customer Service Performance, Shipping Performance, and Policy Compliance.

The dashboard provides a centralized view of your account's standing and flags any issues that need attention.

---

## Account Health Rating (AHR)

### What Is AHR?

The Account Health Rating is a numerical score that directly determines whether sellers maintain their selling privileges or face account deactivation. It is calculated based on 180 days of selling activity and policy compliance data.

### Score Ranges and Color Coding

| Score Range | Status | Color | Meaning |
|-------------|--------|-------|---------|
| 200 - 1,000 | Healthy | Green | Account in good standing, not at risk |
| 100 - 199 | At Risk | Yellow | Account needs immediate attention, deactivation possible |
| 0 - 99 | Unhealthy | Red | Account eligible for or already deactivated |

- **New sellers** start with a score of **200**.
- Sellers **lose points** when new policy violations are detected.
- Sellers **gain points** when violations are successfully resolved.
- The score adjusts over 180 days based on violations and selling activity.

### How AHR Is Calculated

The AHR factors in:
- The number and severity of policy violations
- How quickly violations are resolved
- The overall volume of sales activity (higher volume sellers have more "cushion")
- Whether issues are one-time or repeated patterns

## Three Performance Metric Categories

### 1. Customer Service Performance

The primary metric here is the **Order Defect Rate (ODR)**.

- **Target**: Below 1%
- **Measurement period**: Rolling 60 days
- **Components**:
  - Negative feedback (1-2 star seller ratings)
  - A-to-Z Guarantee claims (only those attributed to seller fault)
  - Credit card chargebacks

An ODR above 1% can lead to loss of Buy Box eligibility, listing suppression, or account suspension.

### 2. Shipping Performance (Seller-Fulfilled Orders Only)

These metrics apply to orders you fulfill yourself (not FBA):

| Metric | Target | Measurement Period |
|--------|--------|--------------------|
| Late Shipment Rate (LSR) | Below 4% | 10-day and 30-day windows |
| Pre-fulfillment Cancel Rate | Below 2.5% | Rolling period |
| Valid Tracking Rate (VTR) | 95% or higher | Rolling period |
| On-Time Delivery Rate (OTDR) | 90% or higher | Rolling period |

- **Late Shipment Rate**: Percentage of orders confirmed shipped after the expected ship date.
- **Pre-fulfillment Cancel Rate**: Percentage of orders you cancel before shipping.
- **Valid Tracking Rate**: Percentage of shipments with functional tracking numbers.
- **On-Time Delivery Rate**: Percentage of packages that arrive by the estimated delivery date (introduced September 25, 2024).

### 3. Policy Compliance

Amazon monitors the following types of policy violations:

- Intellectual property violations (suspected and received complaints)
- Product authenticity complaints
- Product condition complaints
- Product safety complaints
- Listing policy violations
- Restricted product violations
- Review manipulation/policy violations
- Food safety issues
- **Target**: 0% violations

---

## Violation Severity Levels

Amazon categorizes policy violations into four severity levels:

| Severity | Impact | Tolerance |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| **Critical** | A single violation can trigger immediate account deactivation | Zero tolerance |
| **High** | Very low tolerance due to serious customer/policy impact | Minimal tolerance |
| **Medium** | Erode trust if persistent; gradual impact | Some tolerance |
| **Low** | Occasional mistakes tolerated but still tracked | Higher tolerance |

### Repeat Violation Limits (180-day period)

- **Infringement-related policies**: Maximum of 5 repeat violations before automatic deactivation (regardless of AHR score)
- **Restricted product policies**: Maximum of 2 repeat violations before automatic deactivation

---

## What Triggers Warnings

- AHR score dropping into the yellow zone (100-199)
- Any single policy violation notification
- Performance metric exceeding its threshold
- Receiving an A-to-Z Guarantee claim
- Negative seller feedback patterns
- Intellectual property complaints

Amazon sends notifications via Seller Central's Performance Notifications and email when issues arise.

---

## What Leads to Deactivation

### Immediate Deactivation Triggers
- AHR score falling to 0-99 (red zone)
- A single critical-severity policy violation
- Exceeding maximum repeat violation thresholds
- Selling counterfeit products
- Review manipulation
- Creating multiple seller accounts
- Severe safety complaints

### Gradual Path to Deactivation
- Persistent ODR above 1%
- Consistently poor shipping metrics
- Accumulation of medium/high severity violations without resolution
- Failure to respond to Amazon's requests for information within deadlines

### Critical Violation Resolution Window
When a critical violation occurs, Amazon typically provides a **3-day resolution window** before deactivating the account. If the violation is addressed successfully within this timeframe, the account remains active.

## How to Monitor Account Health

### Account Health Dashboard
- Located in Seller Central under **Performance > Account Health**
- Shows your current AHR score with color coding
- Displays all active policy violations and their severity
- Lists performance metrics with current values vs. targets
- Provides links to resolve each issue

### Best Practices for Monitoring

1. **Check the dashboard daily** -- Do not wait for notifications
2. **Set up email alerts** for performance notifications
3. **Respond immediately** to any policy violation notifications
4. **Maintain accurate emergency contact information** in Seller Central
5. **Use the Amazon Seller App** for mobile monitoring and quick response
6. **Review metrics by time period** to spot trends before they become problems
7. **Use compliance tools** (restriction checkers, IP alert tools) to identify risks before listing products
8. **Track your ODR components individually** -- monitor negative feedback, A-to-Z claims, and chargebacks separately
9. **For seller-fulfilled orders**: Monitor carrier performance and shipping confirmation timing closely
10. **Document everything** -- Keep records of policy compliance efforts in case you need to appeal

### Proactive Maintenance

- Regularly audit your product listings for accuracy and policy compliance
- Verify that all products meet Amazon's restricted product guidelines before listing
- Respond to customer messages within 24 hours
- Ship orders on time or switch to FBA for more reliable fulfillment
- Address negative feedback promptly and professionally
- Resolve A-to-Z claims before they escalate
- Keep inventory levels accurate to avoid cancellations

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Your AHR score ranges from 0-1,000; stay above 200 (green) to be safe.
2. Three pillars matter: Customer Service (ODR), Shipping Performance, and Policy Compliance.
3. A single critical violation can shut down your account immediately.
4. Repeat violations have hard limits (5 for IP, 2 for restricted products) that override your AHR.
5. New sellers start at 200 -- you have minimal margin for error early on.
6. Resolved violations improve your score; unresolved ones drag it down.
7. Monitor daily, respond immediately, and document your compliance efforts.
