# Pillar 4: Proactive Restock Guide

## Track Recurring Stockouts and Buy Before the Crowd

Proactive restock is the most research-intensive but least competitive A2A pillar. Instead of reacting to today's price drops, you identify products with predictable stockout-and-restock cycles, set tracking alerts, and position yourself to buy the instant prices drop during a restock — before the reactive crowd even notices.

---

## Core Concept

Many Amazon products follow a recurring pattern:

1. **Amazon stocks the product** at a competitive price
2. **Inventory sells through** over days or weeks
3. **Amazon goes out of stock (OOS)** — only third-party sellers remain
4. **Third-party prices spike** during the OOS period (often 50-200% above Amazon's price)
5. **Amazon restocks** at or near the original price
6. **The cycle repeats**

Your edge is that you have already researched these products and set up alerts. When Amazon restocks and the price drops, you buy immediately. While reactive sourcers might spot the price drop hours later through Keepa Deals, you are already prepared with the analysis done, the buy decision made, and the order placed within minutes of restock.

---

## How to Identify Restock Candidates

### Reading Keepa Charts for Restock Patterns

The key indicator is a Keepa price chart that shows a repeating sawtooth pattern:

```
Price
  ^
  |     /\        /\        /\
  |    /  \      /  \      /  \      <-- 3P price spikes when Amazon is OOS
  |   /    \    /    \    /    \
  |--/------\--/------\--/------\--  <-- Amazon's regular price
  |
  +-----------------------------------------> Time
       OOS    Restock   OOS   Restock
```

**What to look for on the Keepa chart:**

| Signal | What It Means | Where to See It |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Orange line (Amazon price) disappearing** | Amazon went out of stock | Gaps in the Amazon price line on Keepa |
| **Blue/pink lines spiking when orange disappears** | Third-party sellers raise prices during Amazon OOS | Price spikes coinciding with Amazon OOS periods |
| **Orange line reappearing at similar level** | Amazon restocked at their usual price | Price returns to baseline after OOS period |
| **Pattern repeats 3+ times in 6 months** | Reliable restock cycle you can track | Look at 6-month and 1-year Keepa views |
| **Sales rank remains strong during OOS** | Product still has demand even at inflated prices | Rank line stays under 50k during OOS periods |

### Finding Candidates: Search Strategies

#### Strategy 1: Keepa Product Finder (Advanced Search)

Use Keepa's Product Finder with these parameters:

| Parameter | Setting | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Amazon out of stock % (90 days) | 10-50% | Product is frequently OOS but not permanently |
| Current Amazon status | In Stock | You want products currently available |
| Sales Rank (current) | Under 50,000 | Sufficient demand |
| Sales Rank (90-day average) | Under 75,000 | Consistent demand even through OOS cycles |
| Price (current Amazon) | $15-$100 | Sweet spot for A2A margins |
| Category | Your target categories | Focus on categories you know |

#### Strategy 2: Mining Your Own Purchase History

Your best restock candidates come from products you have already successfully flipped:

1. Review your tracking spreadsheet for past reactive flips
2. Pull up the Keepa chart for each past flip
3. Identify which ones show the recurring OOS/restock pattern
4. Add those to your proactive restock watchlist
5. You already know the margins work because you flipped them before

#### Strategy 3: Monitoring Known High-Demand, Frequently OOS Products

| Product Type | Why Frequent OOS | Examples |
|-------------|------------------|---------|
| Popular supplements | High demand, supply chain variability | Specific vitamin brands, protein powders |
| Trending kitchen gadgets | Demand surges exceed Amazon forecasting | Viral TikTok products, seasonal appliances |
| Niche electronics accessories | Amazon carries limited depth | Specific cable types, adapters, mounts |
| Popular kids items | Unpredictable demand spikes | Trending toys, educational products |
| Personal care essentials | Loyal customer base creates steady drain | Specific shampoos, skincare, dental products |

#### Strategy 4: Competitor Intelligence

- Browse other A2A seller storefronts (you can see their active listings on Amazon)
- Products they sell repeatedly may indicate restock-based flips
- Cross-reference with Keepa to confirm the OOS pattern

## Setting Up Your Tracking System

### Keepa Price Tracking Alerts

For each restock candidate, set up Keepa alerts with these settings:

| Alert Parameter | Setting | Rationale |
|----------------|---------|-----------|
| **Track** | Amazon price | You want to know when Amazon restocks |
| **Alert type** | Price drops below threshold | Trigger when Amazon's price returns to buy range |
| **Threshold price** | Your maximum buy price (calculated from ROI analysis) | Only alert on profitable buying opportunities |
| **Alert delivery** | Email + browser notification | Multiple channels for fast notification |
| **Tracking duration** | Indefinite (until you remove it) | Restock cycles are ongoing |

### Building Your Restock Watchlist

Create a dedicated tracking document with the following fields for each candidate:

| Field | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| ASIN | Product identifier |
| Product Name | Quick reference |
| Category | For organization |
| Amazon Regular Price | The price when Amazon is in stock |
| 3P Spike Price | Typical price when Amazon is OOS |
| Your Max Buy Price | Highest price at which ROI still meets your threshold |
| Average OOS Duration | How long Amazon typically stays out of stock |
| Average Restock Interval | How often the full cycle repeats |
| Last Restock Date | When Amazon most recently came back in stock |
| Expected Next Restock | Estimated date based on historical pattern |
| Keepa Alert Set? | Confirmation that tracking is active |
| FlipAlert Monitoring? | Confirmation that restock alerts are active |
| Number of Successful Flips | Track record for this specific product |
| Notes | Brand gating status, competition level, special considerations |

### FlipAlert Restock Monitoring

In addition to Keepa, use FlipAlert's restock monitoring feature:

1. Add the ASIN to your FlipAlert restock list
2. Configure the alert to notify you when Amazon comes back as a seller
3. Set price threshold matching your Keepa alert
4. Enable push notifications for immediate alerts on your phone
5. FlipAlert can sometimes detect restocks minutes before Keepa updates

## Decision Criteria for Restock Flips

### Evaluation Framework (When Alert Fires)

When you receive a restock alert, run through this checklist quickly (you should have most answers pre-researched):

| # | Criterion | Threshold | Pre-Researched? |
|---|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Current Amazon price at or below your max buy price** | Must be at or below threshold | No (check live) |
| 2 | **Gating / eligibility confirmed** | You can sell this ASIN | Yes (checked during research) |
| 3 | **Current sales rank** | Under 50,000 in category | Partially (check live rank) |
| 4 | **Number of FBA sellers at target sell price** | Under 8 | No (check live) |
| 5 | **Price recovery potential** | 3P prices were at target level during last OOS | Yes (from Keepa research) |
| 6 | **ROI at current buy price** | Meets your minimum threshold | Yes (calculated during research, reconfirm) |
| 7 | **Quantity available** | Enough to buy, not so much that restock floods the market | No (check live) |

### Speed Advantage: Pre-Research Payoff

The power of proactive restock vs. reactive sourcing:

| Factor | Reactive Sourcing | Proactive Restock |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Discovery time** | Minutes to hours after drop | Seconds after alert fires |
| **Analysis time** | 5-10 minutes per deal (full evaluation) | 1-2 minutes (most analysis pre-done) |
| **Confidence level** | Medium (first time seeing this product) | High (you have tracked it through multiple cycles) |
| **Competition** | High (everyone sees Keepa Deals) | Low (few people maintain restock watchlists) |
| **Purchase decision** | Often hesitant (unfamiliar product) | Fast and confident (you know the margins) |

## Restock Cycle Analysis: How to Estimate Timing

### Step-by-Step Cycle Analysis

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Open the 1-year Keepa chart for the product |
| 2 | Count the number of times Amazon went OOS (orange line disappeared) in the past 12 months |
| 3 | Record the approximate date of each OOS event |
| 4 | Record the approximate date of each restock (orange line reappears) |
| 5 | Calculate the average interval between restocks |
| 6 | Calculate the average duration of each OOS period |
| 7 | Note whether the pattern is regular (predictable) or irregular |

### Cycle Reliability Rating

| Pattern | Reliability | Action |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **5+ OOS/restock cycles in 12 months, regular intervals** | High | Top priority for your watchlist |
| **3-4 cycles in 12 months, somewhat regular** | Medium-High | Good candidate, monitor actively |
| **2-3 cycles in 12 months, irregular intervals** | Medium | Worth tracking but don't count on timing |
| **1-2 cycles in 12 months** | Low | Could be one-off events, not reliable pattern |
| **Product was OOS for months at a time** | Very Low | Possible discontinuation or supply chain issue, skip |

## Advanced Restock Strategies

### Strategy: Staggered Quantity Purchasing

Instead of buying all units at once when Amazon restocks:

| Purchase | Timing | Quantity | Rationale |
|----------|--------|----------|-----------|
| First buy | Immediately on restock alert | 3-5 units | Secure initial inventory at confirmed good price |
| Second buy | 24-48 hours later (if price holds) | 5-10 units | Price confirmed stable, increase position |
| Optional third buy | 3-5 days later (if still available) | Up to your max | Full position if opportunity remains |

This protects you from buying too much if the restock price is temporary or the pattern has changed.

### Strategy: Sell During OOS Period (Existing Inventory)

If you already have FBA inventory from a previous restock purchase and Amazon goes OOS again:

1. Immediately raise your price to match the 3P spike level
2. You become one of the few sellers, commanding a premium
3. This accelerates your profit timeline
4. When Amazon restocks, lower price back to competitive level and buy more inventory

### Strategy: Building Restock Databases Over Time

Your restock watchlist is a compounding asset:

| Month | Watchlist Size | Expected Alerts/Month | Estimated Flips |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Month 1 | 10-15 ASINs | 2-4 | 1-3 |
| Month 3 | 30-50 ASINs | 8-15 | 5-10 |
| Month 6 | 75-120 ASINs | 15-30 | 10-20 |
| Month 12 | 150-250 ASINs | 30-60 | 20-40 |

Over time, your watchlist grows, your alerts increase, and your hit rate improves because you are refining which products actually follow the pattern.

## TOS Compliance for Restock Flips

All standard A2A TOS rules apply. Additional restock-specific considerations:

| Rule | Restock-Specific Notes |
|------|----------------------|
| **Non-Prime purchasing** | Same rule. Do not use Prime even when speed seems critical. |
| **Order volume** | Buying 10-20 units of a just-restocked item is reasonable. Buying 100+ may flag your account. |
| **Pricing during OOS** | When selling during OOS periods, price at market rates (what 3P sellers are charging). Do not price gouge beyond the established 3P market. |
| **Documentation** | Save all purchase invoices. Restock products may be popular targets for inauthentic complaints due to their value. |
| **Returns** | Do not return unsold restock inventory to Amazon as a buyer return. Sell through or liquidate via other channels. |

## Common Restock Mistakes

1. **Not verifying the pattern has enough cycles** — Two OOS events is not a pattern; five is
2. **Setting Keepa alerts at too high a price** — You get alerted on price levels that aren't actually profitable
3. **Ignoring competition changes** — A product that had 3 FBA sellers now has 12; margins are compressed
4. **Buying before confirming current price** — Alert fires but by the time you check, price has already risen
5. **Assuming Amazon will go OOS again** — Past patterns don't guarantee future behavior; Amazon may increase inventory depth
6. **Neglecting watchlist maintenance** — Products get discontinued, gated, or change pricing structure; review your watchlist monthly
7. **Over-relying on a single product** — Even the best restock candidate can stop cycling; diversify your watchlist
8. **Not having funds available when alerts fire** — Capital tied up elsewhere means missed opportunities; maintain a restock cash reserve

## Monthly Watchlist Maintenance Checklist

Perform this review on your entire restock watchlist once per month:

| # | Check | Action if Failed |
|---|-------|-----------------|
| 1 | Is the product still active on Amazon? | Remove from watchlist if discontinued |
| 2 | Has Amazon restocked at least once in the past 60 days? | Investigate; may be permanently OOS |
| 3 | Is the OOS/restock pattern still consistent? | Downgrade reliability rating if pattern is breaking |
| 4 | Has competition at the sell price increased significantly? | Recalculate ROI; remove if margins no longer work |
| 5 | Are your Keepa and FlipAlert alerts still active? | Re-enable any expired alerts |
| 6 | Has the product become gated or restricted? | Remove from watchlist if you can no longer sell it |
| 7 | Are there any IP complaints reported for this brand? | Increase caution or remove; check seller forums |
| 8 | Is your max buy price still accurate? | Recalculate based on current FBA fees and sell price |
| 9 | Have you successfully flipped this product recently? | Confirm with actual data, not assumptions |
| 10 | Are there better alternatives to add? | Replace underperformers with new candidates |
