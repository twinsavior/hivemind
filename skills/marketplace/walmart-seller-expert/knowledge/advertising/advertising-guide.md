# Walmart Marketplace Advertising Guide (Walmart Connect)

## Overview

Walmart Connect is Walmart's advertising platform that enables Marketplace sellers to promote their products through Sponsored Search, Onsite Display, and Search Engine Marketing (SEM) campaigns. The platform operates primarily on pay-per-click and cost-per-impression models, with no setup or monthly fees for Sponsored Search.

---

## Advertising Formats

### 1. Sponsored Products (Sponsored Search)

#### What It Is
Cost-per-click (CPC) ads that appear in high-traffic locations on Walmart.com when customers actively search for products. Ads display in search results, product detail pages, browse pages, and homepage carousels.

#### How It Works
- Sellers bid on relevant keywords
- When a shopper searches for those terms, the product appears alongside organic results
- Selection is based on relevancy and bid amount
- Highest-bidding, most-relevant offering wins the placement
- Uses a second-price auction model: you set a maximum bid but only pay what is needed to win

#### Eligibility Requirements
- Items must be in stock
- Must own the Buy Box for the item
- Item must be published on Walmart.com

#### Campaign Types
- **Automatic Campaigns**: Walmart auto-selects keywords and placements. Ideal for data collection and new advertisers
- **Manual Campaigns**: Seller controls keyword selection and bidding. Best for experienced advertisers seeking precision

#### Bidding
- CPC (cost per click) second-price auction
- You set a maximum bid but only pay what is needed to win
- **Dynamic Bidding**: Automated bid adjustments of up to +100% when conversion is likely
- No setup or monthly fees -- you only pay when a shopper clicks your ad

---

### 2. Sponsored Brands

#### What It Is
Premium ads that appear at the top of relevant search results, featuring your brand logo, custom headline, and selected products.

#### How It Works
- Designed to build brand awareness and drive consideration
- Customizable creative elements including logos and headlines
- Particularly benefits emerging or smaller brands lacking strong organic rankings

#### Eligibility Requirements
- Must be located in US, China, Hong Kong, UK, or India
- Must have an active US Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO) registration
- Must be enrolled in the Walmart Brand Portal

---

### 3. Sponsored Videos

#### What It Is
High-quality video ads designed to enhance visibility through motion, sight, and sound at critical purchase consideration moments.

#### Eligibility Requirements
- Same as Sponsored Brands (location, USPTO registration, Brand Portal enrollment)
- Must have high-quality video creative assets

---

### 4. Onsite Display Advertising

#### What It Is
Self-serve display advertising that enables brand owners to reach customers with visually compelling ads across Walmart.com and the Walmart app.

#### How It Works
- Operates on an eCPM model (cost per thousand impressions)
- Uses dynamic bidding that adjusts bids based on pacing
- First-price auction model: winners pay their winning bid, never exceeding their maximum bid
- Unlike Sponsored Search, Onsite Display targets customers through audience-based targeting rather than active search

#### Eligibility Requirements
- Must be a Marketplace brand owner registered with the Walmart Brand Portal
- No minimum spending requirement, though Walmart recommends at least $4,500 per campaign monthly

#### Targeting Options

| Targeting Tactic | Description |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Contextual | Shows ads on search and item pages in chosen departments |
| Behavioral | Targets based on shopping behavior (brand, category, persona, custom audiences) |
| Keyword | Displays ads on search pages for selected keywords |
| Run-of-site | Appears across Walmart site and app (excluding homepage) |

#### Creative Development
- Build visuals through the Creative Platform using Creative Builder
- Walmart offers the Creative Essentials Ad Certification to help sellers create compelling ad visuals

#### Budget and Bidding Control
- Set base and maximum bids
- Choose daily or lifetime budget strategies
- Complete control over campaign spending and performance

---

### 5. Search Engine Marketing (SEM)

#### What It Is
Walmart's SEM program drives traffic to your Walmart.com listings from external search engines (Google, Bing, etc.).

#### Eligibility and Access
- Only Seller Center users with admin access can create and modify SEM campaigns
- Non-admin users must contact their account administrator
- Pro Sellers receive $50 monthly SEM credit

#### Campaign Types
- **Quick Start**: Automatically recommends and adds high-potential items; only displays if you have sufficient suggested items
- **Custom**: Manual item selection and configuration

#### Campaign Configuration
- Add items individually (search by SKU, item name, or ID) or upload in bulk using a template
- Not all items may be eligible for SEM campaigns
- Partial failures in bulk uploads generate error reports (may take 2-3 days to resolve)

#### Scheduling and Budget
- Start date defaults to the next day
- Daily budget selection is required
- End dates are optional
- If set, end dates must be at least 21 days after the start date

#### Bidding Strategies

**Maximize Clicks**
- Walmart automatically sets bids to get as many clicks as possible within budget
- Best for driving traffic to new products with limited data
- Risk: returns may fall below expectations

**Target Return on Ad Spend (tROAS)**
- Walmart automatically sets bids to maximize sales based on your target ROAS
- Ideal for products with proven SEM performance
- Risk: full budget may not spend if tROAS targets are not achievable

#### Algorithm Performance Phases

**Learning Phase** (7-10 days average)
- System tests different bid levels and analyzes performance metrics
- You may see limited impressions and clicks or inconsistent ROAS
- Do not make major changes during this phase

**Optimizing Phase**
- Once sufficient data exists, the algorithm focuses on achieving optimal ROAS
- Adjusts spend on underperforming items accordingly

#### Campaign Status
- Campaigns display as "Scheduled" on creation day
- Change to "Running" when the start date arrives

## Campaign Management Best Practices

### Getting Started
1. Access Walmart Connect through the Seller Center Advertising section
2. Define clear campaign goals (new product launches, seasonal sales, brand awareness)
3. Select products that are in stock, Buy Box-winning, and category-relevant
4. Start with Automatic campaigns to gather data, then transition to Manual for precision

### Budget Management
- Set daily or campaign-level budgets
- Monitor spend and performance regularly
- Use Dynamic Bidding to automatically optimize bid amounts
- Start with conservative budgets and scale based on performance data

### Keyword Strategy (Manual Campaigns)
- Research relevant keywords using Search Insights tool
- Include a mix of broad, phrase, and exact match keywords
- Add negative keywords to exclude irrelevant searches
- Regularly review search term reports to refine keyword lists

### Performance Optimization
- Monitor key metrics: impressions, clicks, CTR, CPC, ROAS, total sales
- Pause underperforming keywords or increase bids on high-performing ones
- Test different ad creatives for Sponsored Brands
- Adjust bids based on time of day, day of week, and seasonality
- Ensure products remain in stock and Buy Box-eligible during campaigns

## Reporting

### Available Reports
- Campaign performance reports (impressions, clicks, conversions, spend)
- Keyword performance reports (for manual campaigns)
- Search term reports (what customers actually searched)
- ROAS and attribution reporting
- Daily, weekly, and campaign-level reporting available

### Access
- Reports available through the Walmart Connect dashboard in Seller Center
- Walmart Advertising Help Center provides additional support and documentation

## Key Reminders

1. Sponsored Products have no setup or monthly fees -- you only pay per click
2. Sponsored Brands and Videos require Brand Portal enrollment and USPTO trademark
3. Onsite Display uses impression-based (eCPM) pricing, not click-based
4. SEM campaigns need a minimum 21-day run period if an end date is set
5. Allow 7-10 days for the learning phase before judging SEM campaign performance
6. Pro Sellers receive $50 monthly SEM credit
7. Always ensure advertised items are in stock and Buy Box-eligible
8. Use the Search Insights tool to inform your keyword strategy
