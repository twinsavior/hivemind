# Amazon Customer Communication & Messaging Guide

## Overview

Amazon tightly controls how sellers communicate with buyers. All messages must go through Amazon's Buyer-Seller Messaging system, and there are strict rules about what you can and cannot say. Violating these guidelines can result in messaging restrictions or account suspension.

---

## Types of Messages

### 1. Buyer-Initiated Messages
- Messages where the buyer contacts you first (through "Contact Seller" on Amazon)
- You must respond to these messages
- Response time target: **within 24 hours**
- These appear in your Buyer-Seller Messaging inbox in Seller Central

### 2. Proactive Permitted Messages
- Messages you initiate to a buyer who has purchased from you
- Must be sent **within 30 days** of order completion
- Must include the **17-digit order ID**
- Must be in the **buyer's language of preference**
- Subject to strict content rules (see below)

### 3. Critical Messages
- Messages required to complete an order (e.g., customization details, scheduling delivery)
- Given more flexibility in content but still subject to core rules

---

## Permitted Reasons for Proactive Messages

You may proactively contact a buyer only for these specific purposes:

1. **Resolving an order fulfillment issue** (e.g., item out of stock, shipping delay)
2. **Asking a question related to a return** (e.g., clarifying reason, coordinating return)
3. **Sending an invoice**
4. **Requesting critical information needed for the customer to receive their order** (e.g., custom specifications)
5. **Scheduling delivery of a heavy or bulky item**
6. **Requesting a product review or seller feedback**
7. **Verifying a custom design** (for personalized products)
8. **Amazon Home Services appointment scheduling**
9. **Other communications necessary for purchase delivery**

---

## What Sellers CANNOT Do in Messages

### Prohibited Content

| Category | Prohibited Items |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Marketing** | Promotional content, coupons, discount codes, cross-sell offers, upsell attempts |
| **External links** | Links to websites outside Amazon (your website, social media, etc.) |
| **Contact info** | Email addresses, phone numbers, physical addresses, social media handles |
| **Attachments** | Unnecessary file attachments (only invoices and required documents allowed) |
| **Tracking pixels** | Any pixel or tracking technology embedded in messages |
| **Images** | Product images, company logos with website links, unsecure (HTTP) images |
| **Formatting** | Emojis, GIFs, more than 3 font sizes, excessive spacing (>20% margins) |
| **Review manipulation** | Asking for positive reviews, incentivizing reviews, requesting review removal/changes |
| **Opt-out links** | Unsubscribe or opt-out mechanisms |
| **Quality issues** | Major spelling or grammar errors |

### Prohibited Message Types

These types of messages are NOT permitted, even though they seem helpful:

- **Order/shipping confirmations**: Amazon already sends these automatically
- **"Thank you" messages**: Unless combined with a permitted purpose
- **"We're always here to help" messages**: Generic assistance offers with no specific purpose
- **Follow-up messages** that have no specific order-related purpose

### Prohibited Language and Tactics

- **"Please leave a 5-star review"** -- Never ask for a specific star rating
- **"We're a small family business"** -- Emotional appeals are not permitted
- **"Veteran-owned" or "support us"** -- Sympathy-based language is prohibited
- **"If you have any problems, contact us before leaving a review"** -- Attempting to intercept negative reviews
- **Conditional review requests** (e.g., "If you're happy with your purchase, please leave a review")
- **Offering compensation** for positive reviews (gift cards, discounts, free products, refunds)

## Requesting Reviews

### The Request a Review Button
- Available on the **Order Details page** in Seller Central
- Sends a standardized, Amazon-branded review request to the buyer
- Compliant by design -- no risk of policy violation
- Can be used **4 to 30 days** after delivery
- Sends both a product review request and seller feedback request
- Cannot be customized -- Amazon controls the content
- **Recommended approach** for most sellers due to zero risk

### Manual Review Requests
- Permitted through Buyer-Seller Messaging
- Must use neutral language: "We'd appreciate your feedback" (not "Please leave a positive review")
- Only send **one review request per order**
- Do not follow up if the buyer does not respond
- Cannot be conditional on the buyer's experience

### Amazon Vine Program
- Enroll products to have Amazon Vine Voices (trusted reviewers) review them
- Generates high-quality, detailed reviews
- Particularly useful for new product launches
- Enrollment fee applies; products are provided free to reviewers
- Reviews from Vine are marked with a "Vine Customer Review of Free Product" badge

## Dealing with Negative Reviews

### What You Can Do

#### 1. Contact Low-Rating Customers (Brand Registry Required)
- Through the **Customer Reviews** tool in Seller Central
- Available for Brand Representatives enrolled in Brand Registry
- Contact buyers who left ratings below 3 stars
- Offer refunds or request additional information to resolve issues
- Goal: Fix the underlying problem (the review may then be updated by the buyer voluntarily)

#### 2. Post Public Replies to Reviews
- Available through the Amazon Seller App
- Respond professionally and helpfully
- Acknowledge the issue and offer a solution
- Do NOT be argumentative or defensive
- Do NOT ask the buyer to change their review in the public reply

#### 3. Report Abusive Reviews
- Use the **"Report"** option on the product detail page
- Reviews that violate Amazon's Community Guidelines can be removed
- Valid reasons for reporting: review is about the seller (not product), contains profanity, is spam, is from a competitor, customer reviewed the wrong product
- Amazon investigates and decides whether to remove

### What You Cannot Do
- Ask buyers to remove or change negative reviews
- Offer refunds, discounts, or gifts in exchange for review removal
- Contact buyers specifically to get them to change a review
- Use third-party services to remove reviews
- Leave retaliatory negative feedback on customers

## Seller Feedback Management

### What Is Seller Feedback?
- Separate from product reviews
- Rates the seller's performance (shipping speed, packaging, customer service)
- Visible on your seller profile
- Impacts your Order Defect Rate if 1-2 stars

### Responding to Seller Feedback
- You can post a public response to seller feedback
- Be professional and solution-oriented
- Address the buyer's specific concern
- Show future buyers that you take feedback seriously

### Requesting Feedback Removal
You can request removal of seller feedback that:
- Is actually a product review (not about your seller service)
- Contains obscene or abusive language
- Contains personal information
- Is entirely about FBA fulfillment (for FBA orders, Amazon may strike through the feedback)

### How to Request Removal
1. Go to **Seller Central > Performance > Feedback Manager**
2. Find the specific feedback entry
3. Click **"Request removal"**
4. Amazon reviews and decides (typically within 48 hours)

## Response Time Requirements

### Buyer-Initiated Messages
- **Target**: Respond within **24 hours**
- Response time is tracked as part of your customer service metrics
- Late responses negatively impact your Customer Service Dissatisfaction Rate
- If you cannot resolve the issue immediately, acknowledge the message and provide a timeline

### A-to-Z Guarantee Claims
- **Deadline**: Respond within **3 calendar days** of receiving notification
- Failure to respond within the deadline typically results in the claim being granted against you
- Provide all relevant documentation (tracking info, delivery confirmation, refund records)

### Performance Notification Responses
- Respond within the deadline specified in the notification
- Typically 3-7 days depending on severity

## Email Templates for Common Scenarios

### Template: Order Delay Notification
```
Subject: Important information about your order [Order ID]

Hello [Buyer Name],

I'm reaching out regarding your recent order [Order ID] for [Product Name].

Unfortunately, [specific reason for delay - e.g., "a supply chain disruption has affected the availability of this item"].

Your updated estimated delivery date is [date].

If this timeline does not work for you, I can process a full refund. Please let me know how you'd like to proceed.

Thank you for your patience.

[Your Seller Name]
```

### Template: Return Follow-up
```
Subject: Regarding your return request for order [Order ID]

Hello [Buyer Name],

Thank you for reaching out about your return for order [Order ID].

To process this as quickly as possible, could you please let me know:
- [Specific question about the return reason or condition]

Once I have this information, I'll get your return processed right away.

[Your Seller Name]
```

### Template: Requesting Missing Information for Custom Order
```
Subject: Information needed to complete your order [Order ID]

Hello [Buyer Name],

Thank you for your order [Order ID] for [Product Name].

To complete your custom order, I need the following information:
- [Specific detail needed, e.g., engraving text, size specification]

Could you please provide this at your earliest convenience? I'll begin production as soon as I receive your response.

[Your Seller Name]
```

### Template: Damaged Item Resolution
```
Subject: Regarding your order [Order ID]

Hello [Buyer Name],

I'm sorry to hear that your [Product Name] arrived damaged. That is not the experience we want for our customers.

I'd like to offer you the following options:
1. A full replacement shipped immediately at no cost
2. A full refund

Please let me know which option you prefer, and I'll take care of it right away.

[Your Seller Name]
```

## Best Practices for Buyer Communication

### Do:
- Respond to all messages within 24 hours (ideally within a few hours)
- Address the buyer's specific issue directly
- Offer concrete solutions (refund, replacement, tracking information)
- Be professional, courteous, and empathetic
- Include the order ID in all correspondence
- Use the Request a Review button instead of manual review requests
- Keep messages concise and focused on the issue at hand
- Follow up to ensure the issue was resolved

### Do Not:
- Send marketing or promotional content
- Include links to your website or other external sites
- Share your email address or phone number
- Use emojis, GIFs, or excessive formatting
- Ask buyers to contact you outside of Amazon
- Send unnecessary messages (shipping confirmations, thank-you notes)
- Pressure buyers for positive reviews
- Respond emotionally to negative feedback
- Send more than one review request per order

---

## Consequences of Violating Communication Guidelines

1. **Message blocking**: Amazon may block individual messages that violate guidelines
2. **Subject line modification**: Amazon may change your message subject lines
3. **Messaging restrictions**: Loss of ability to send proactive messages; limited to Amazon's pre-built templates only
4. **Account warnings**: Formal warning added to your performance record
5. **Account suspension**: Repeated violations can lead to selling privilege suspension

---

## Key Takeaways

1. All communication must go through Amazon's Buyer-Seller Messaging system -- no external contact.
2. Proactive messages must be within 30 days of order completion and include the order ID.
3. No marketing, external links, contact information, emojis, or product images in messages.
4. Use the **Request a Review button** for review solicitation -- it is the safest method.
5. Never ask for positive reviews or incentivize reviews in any way.
6. Respond to all buyer messages within 24 hours.
7. Respond to A-to-Z claims within 3 calendar days.
8. Report abusive reviews through the product detail page; do not retaliate.
9. Keep messages professional, concise, and focused on resolving the buyer's issue.
