# eBay Getting Started Guide for New Sellers

> Complete reference for account setup, verification, listing limits, payment configuration, first listing creation, and fee overview.

---

## Account Types: Personal vs. Business

Before creating your seller account, decide which account type fits your situation.

| Feature | Personal Account | Business Account |
|---|---|---|
| **Who it's for** | Casual sellers, individuals clearing out items | Registered businesses, full-time sellers |
| **Registration requires** | Name, address, email, phone | Business name, EIN/tax ID, business address |
| **Tax reporting** | 1099-K if you exceed IRS thresholds | 1099-K issued to business entity |
| **Listing limits** | Lower initial limits | Potentially higher initial limits |
| **Buyer perception** | Standard | Displays as business seller |
| **Upgrade path** | Can upgrade to business later | Cannot downgrade to personal |
| **eBay Store** | Can subscribe | Can subscribe |
| **Sales tax collection** | eBay collects automatically (marketplace facilitator) | eBay collects automatically (marketplace facilitator) |

> **Recommendation**: If you plan to sell regularly or already have a business entity, start with a business account. You can always upgrade from personal to business, but you cannot go the other direction.

## Creating Your Seller Account

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Go to** `ebay.com` and click "Register" (top left)
2. **Choose account type**: Personal or Business
3. **For Personal Account, provide**:
   - Full legal name
   - Email address
   - Password
   - Phone number (for verification)
4. **For Business Account, additionally provide**:
   - Business name (as registered)
   - Business type (sole proprietor, LLC, corporation, etc.)
   - EIN or SSN (for tax reporting)
   - Business address
5. **Verify your email**: Click the link sent to your email
6. **Verify your phone**: Enter the code sent via SMS
7. **Set up payment method for seller fees**: Link a bank account or debit card for eBay to charge your seller fees

### Navigation Path to Seller Settings
```
My eBay > Account > Personal Information
My eBay > Account > Business Information (business accounts)
```

## Identity Verification

eBay requires identity verification for all new sellers before you can receive payouts.

### What You'll Need

| Document Type | Accepted Forms |
|---|---|
| **Government-issued photo ID** | Driver's license, passport, state ID card |
| **Proof of address** | Bank statement, utility bill (within 3 months) |
| **SSN or EIN** | Social Security Number (personal) or Employer Identification Number (business) |
| **Bank account** | Checking account for payouts (routing + account number) |

### Verification Process

1. **Navigate to**: Seller Hub > Payments > Payment Settings
2. **Click** "Verify Identity" or follow the prompt in your seller dashboard
3. **Upload documents**: Photo ID (front and back) and proof of address
4. **Enter financial information**: SSN/EIN and bank account details
5. **Wait for review**: Typically 24-48 hours, sometimes instant
6. **Confirmation**: You'll receive an email when verification is complete

> **Common rejection reasons**: Blurry ID photos, address mismatch between ID and proof of address, expired documents, bank account name doesn't match seller name.

## Seller Limits (New Seller Restrictions)

Every new eBay account starts with selling limits. These exist to protect the marketplace from fraud.

### Typical New Seller Limits

| Metric | Typical New Seller Limit |
|---|---|
| **Number of items per month** | 10 items |
| **Total value per month** | $500 |
| **Categories** | May be restricted from high-risk categories (electronics, gift cards, tickets) |

> **Note**: Limits vary by account. Some new accounts get higher limits based on verification quality, linked PayPal history, or other factors eBay doesn't disclose.

### How to Check Your Current Limits
```
Seller Hub > Overview > Monthly Limits (shown in the left sidebar or top banner)
```
Or:
```
My eBay > Selling > Seller Dashboard > Monthly Selling Limits
```

### How to Request a Limit Increase

**Method 1: Automatic Increase**
- Sell consistently and maintain good performance
- eBay automatically reviews and increases limits monthly
- Typically see first automatic increase after 30-90 days of selling

**Method 2: Manual Request**
1. Go to Seller Hub > Overview
2. Click "Request to list more" (or the limits link)
3. Follow the prompts — eBay may ask you to:
   - Verify your identity (if not already done)
   - Provide a phone number for a callback
   - Answer questions about what you plan to sell
4. Call eBay seller support at **1-866-540-3229** and request a limit increase directly

**Tips for Successful Limit Increase Requests:**
- Have a clear answer for what you plan to sell and where you source it
- Demonstrate you have inventory ready
- Show knowledge of the category you want to sell in
- Having a few successful sales with positive feedback helps significantly
- Call during business hours (Monday-Friday, morning) for shorter wait times

### Limit Increase Timeline

| Account Age | Typical Limits After Increase |
|---|---|
| 0-30 days | 10-25 items / $500-$2,500 |
| 30-90 days | 50-100 items / $5,000-$10,000 |
| 90-180 days | 100-500 items / $10,000-$25,000 |
| 180+ days (good standing) | 500-1,000+ items / $25,000-$100,000+ |
| Established seller (1+ year) | Effectively unlimited for most categories |

## Payment Setup (Managed Payments)

All eBay sellers use **eBay Managed Payments**. PayPal is no longer the payment processor — eBay handles all payments directly.

### How Managed Payments Works

1. **Buyer pays** via credit card, debit card, Apple Pay, Google Pay, or PayPal (as a payment method, not processor)
2. **eBay processes** the payment and holds the funds
3. **eBay deducts** seller fees and any other charges
4. **eBay sends payout** to your linked bank account

### Payout Schedule

| Seller Status | Payout Frequency |
|---|---|
| New seller (first 90 days) | Funds held until delivery confirmed + up to 5 business days |
| Established seller (Above Standard) | Daily payouts (1-2 business day processing) |
| Top Rated Seller | Daily payouts (may be faster) |
| Below Standard | Funds may be held longer |

### Setting Up Your Bank Account for Payouts

```
Seller Hub > Payments > Payment Settings > Payout Settings
```

1. Enter your bank routing number (9 digits)
2. Enter your checking account number
3. Confirm account details
4. eBay will make a small test deposit (1-3 business days)
5. Verify the test deposit amount in your eBay settings
6. Payout account is now active

> **Important**: Savings accounts may work but checking accounts are recommended. The bank account name must match your eBay account name (or business name for business accounts).

### Payment Holds for New Sellers

eBay holds funds from new sellers as a fraud prevention measure.

| Situation | Hold Duration |
|---|---|
| Default new seller hold | Until tracking shows delivered + up to 3 days |
| If buyer leaves positive feedback | Hold released early |
| After 90 days of good selling | Holds typically stop |
| Manual release | Can request after tracking shows delivered |

## Your First Listing: Step-by-Step Walkthrough

### Navigation
```
Seller Hub > Listings > Create Listing
```
Or click "Sell" in the top navigation bar on any eBay page.

### Step 1: Identify Your Item
- **Search for your product**: Type the item name, UPC, ISBN, or model number
- **Select a catalog match** if one appears (recommended — pre-fills item specifics)
- **Or choose "Continue without selecting a product"** for unique/used items

### Step 2: Choose Your Category
- eBay will suggest categories based on your search
- Select the most specific category that matches your item
- Some items fit in multiple categories — you can list in a second category for an additional fee

### Step 3: Item Specifics
- **Required fields** (vary by category): Brand, MPN, Color, Size, etc.
- **Recommended fields**: Fill in as many as possible for better search visibility
- **Condition**: New, Open Box, Refurbished, Used, For Parts/Not Working

### Step 4: Title and Description
- **Title**: Up to 80 characters. Include brand, model, key attributes, condition
- **Description**: Describe the item honestly. Include flaws for used items
- **Item condition description**: Appears prominently — be specific about wear/damage

### Step 5: Photos
- **Minimum**: 1 photo (but always use more)
- **Recommended**: 8-12 photos
- **Maximum**: 24 photos (free)
- **Requirements**: At least 500x500 pixels, no watermarks, no borders, no text overlays
- Show all angles, flaws, labels, serial numbers, accessories included

### Step 6: Pricing
- **Fixed Price (Buy It Now)**: Set your price, item stays listed until sold or ended
- **Auction**: Set starting price, duration (1, 3, 5, 7, or 10 days), optional reserve price
- **Best Offer**: Enable on fixed-price listings to allow negotiation

### Step 7: Shipping
- **Free shipping**: Recommended for search boost (build cost into item price)
- **Calculated shipping**: Buyer pays based on their location and package dimensions
- **Flat rate**: You set a fixed shipping cost
- **Handling time**: 1 business day recommended (required for Top Rated Plus)

### Step 8: Returns
- **30-day returns**: Recommended for search boost and TRS Plus eligibility
- **60-day returns**: Even better for visibility
- **No returns**: Allowed but reduces search visibility and buyer confidence

### Step 9: Review and List
- Review all details
- Check the fee summary (insertion fee + estimated final value fee)
- Click "List Item"

## eBay Seller Fees Overview

This is a high-level summary. See the detailed fees guide for complete category-by-category breakdown.

| Fee Type | Amount | When Charged |
|---|---|---|
| **Insertion fee** | Free for first 250 listings/month (no Store), then $0.35/listing | When you create the listing |
| **Final value fee** | 13.25%-15.3% depending on category | When item sells |
| **Per-order fee** | $0.30 per order | When item sells |
| **Promoted Listings** | 2%-30%+ of sale price (seller sets rate) | When item sells via promoted placement |
| **International fee** | 1.65% of sale price | When selling to international buyers |
| **Store subscription** | $4.95-$2,999.95/month | Monthly |

### Free Listing Allowances

| Store Level | Free Listings/Month | Insertion Fee After Allowance |
|---|---|---|
| No Store | 250 | $0.35 |
| Starter Store | 250 | $0.30 |
| Basic Store | 1,000 | $0.25 |
| Premium Store | 10,000 | $0.10 |
| Anchor Store | 25,000 | $0.05 |
| Enterprise Store | 100,000 | $0.05 |

## Essential First Steps After Account Creation

### Checklist for New Sellers

1. **Complete identity verification** — Cannot receive payouts without this
2. **Set up bank account for payouts** — Takes 1-3 days for verification deposit
3. **Configure return policy** — 30-day returns recommended
4. **Set up shipping preferences** — Create at least one shipping profile
5. **Download the eBay Seller app** — Manage sales, respond to messages, print labels on mobile
6. **Set up Seller Hub** — Go to `ebay.com/sellerhub` and opt in (may be automatic)
7. **Familiarize yourself with Seller Hub sections**:
   - Overview: Sales snapshot, to-do list, monthly limits
   - Orders: All orders awaiting shipment, shipped, completed
   - Listings: Active, unsold, drafts
   - Performance: Service metrics, seller level, traffic stats
   - Payments: Payout history, fee invoices

### Common New Seller Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Consequence | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Not shipping on time | Late shipment defect, Below Standard risk | Ship within your stated handling time, always |
| Inaccurate item descriptions | INAD returns, defects, negative feedback | Photograph and describe all flaws honestly |
| Not uploading tracking | Cannot prove delivery, lose disputes | Always upload tracking same day you ship |
| Cancelling orders (out of stock) | Transaction defect | Only list items you have in hand |
| Ignoring buyer messages | Poor experience, cases opened | Respond within 24 hours |
| Pricing without accounting for fees | Selling at a loss | Calculate: item cost + shipping + 13-15% fees + $0.30 |
| Using stock photos for used items | Buyer expectation mismatch, INAD returns | Always use your own photos showing actual item |
