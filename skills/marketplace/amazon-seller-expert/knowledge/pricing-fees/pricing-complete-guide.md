# Amazon Pricing & Fees Complete Guide

## Overview

Understanding Amazon's fee structure is essential for profitability. This guide covers selling plans, referral fees by category, FBA fees, optional costs, pricing tools, promotions, and pricing policies.

---

## Selling Plans

### Individual Plan
- **Cost**: $0.99 per item sold (no monthly subscription)
- **Best for**: Sellers planning to sell fewer than 40 items per month
- **Includes**:
  - Basic listing tools
  - FBA capability
  - Limited advertising access
- **Does not include**:
  - Access to Automate Pricing tool
  - Advanced selling tools
  - Buy Box eligibility for some categories
  - Bulk listing tools

### Professional Plan
- **Cost**: $39.99 per month (plus selling fees)
- **Best for**: Sellers planning to sell more than 40 items per month
- **Includes everything in Individual, plus**:
  - No per-item fee
  - Buy Box eligibility
  - Advanced business reports
  - Automate Pricing tool
  - Bulk listing and upload tools
  - Amazon Ads access
  - Promotions, deals, and coupons tools
  - A+ Content access (with Brand Registry)
  - API access for inventory/order management
  - Customized shipping rates
  - New Seller Guide access

### Which Plan to Choose
- If you sell **40+ items per month**: Professional saves money ($39.99 vs. $0.99 x 40 = $39.60)
- If you want **Buy Box eligibility**: Professional is required for most categories
- If you want **advertising tools**: Professional is required
- **You can switch plans** at any time in Seller Central

## FBA Fees Summary

### Fulfillment Fees
Per-unit fee covering picking, packing, shipping, customer service, and returns:
- **Small standard**: $3.06 - $3.65
- **Large standard**: $3.68 - $6.92+
- **Large bulky**: $9.61+
- **Extra-large**: $26.33 - $194.95+
- **Low-Price FBA**: Approximately 25% less than standard rates

### Storage Fees
- **Standard (Jan-Sep)**: $0.78/cubic foot
- **Standard (Oct-Dec)**: $2.40/cubic foot
- **Oversize (Jan-Sep)**: $0.56/cubic foot
- **Oversize (Oct-Dec)**: $1.40/cubic foot

### Other FBA Fees
- **Aged inventory surcharge**: Applied at 181+ days
- **Long-term storage**: Applied at 365+ days
- **Removal/disposal fees**: Per-unit charges
- **Returns processing fee**: For eligible categories
- **Inbound placement service fee**: For consolidated shipments

(See the FBA Complete Guide for detailed fee tables.)

## Optional Costs and Services

### Amazon Advertising
- **Sponsored Products**: Pay-per-click ads for individual product listings
- **Sponsored Brands**: Banner ads featuring your brand and multiple products
- **Sponsored Display**: Display ads on and off Amazon
- **Cost**: Auction-based (you set bids and daily budgets); average CPC varies by category ($0.50 - $3.00+)
- **Professional plan required**

### Amazon Brand Registry
- **Cost**: Free to enroll (requires a registered trademark)
- **Benefits**: A+ Content, Brand Store, Sponsored Brands, brand protection tools, Vine access

### Amazon Vine
- **Cost**: Enrollment fee per parent ASIN (fee varies; check Seller Central for current pricing)
- **What you get**: Trusted reviewers receive your product for free and leave honest reviews
- **Best for**: New product launches needing initial reviews

### Customer Service by Amazon
- An optional service where Amazon handles customer service for merchant-fulfilled orders
- Fee applies per customer contact

### Strategic Account Services
- Premium support from a dedicated Amazon account manager
- Monthly fee applies
- Tailored advice on advertising, operations, and growth

### Amazon Global Logistics
- International shipping and customs management
- Competitive rates for moving inventory from manufacturing locations to Amazon fulfillment centers

### Amazon Seller Wallet
- Hold, view, and transfer international sales proceeds in multiple currencies
- Currency conversion fees apply when transferring

### Buy with Prime
- Let customers use Prime benefits on your own website
- Fulfillment fees apply (similar to FBA)

### Subscribe & Save
- Program for recurring deliveries
- You offer a discount (5-15%) for subscription orders
- Amazon handles fulfillment and subscription management
- Increases customer lifetime value

## Automate Pricing Tool

### What It Is
A free tool (included with Professional plan) that automatically adjusts your prices in real time based on rules you define. Runs 24/7 to maintain competitiveness.

### Three Rule Types

#### 1. Competitive Price-Based Rules
- Set your price relative to a reference price:
  - **Featured Offer (Buy Box) price**: Match, beat by a fixed amount, or beat by a percentage
  - **Lowest Amazon price**: Stay at or below the lowest competitor
  - **External lowest price**: Match prices from outside Amazon
- Set minimum and maximum price limits to protect margins

#### 2. Sales-Based Rules
- Adjust prices based on sales volume within specified timeframes
- Raise prices when sales velocity is high
- Lower prices when sales are slow
- Useful for demand-based pricing strategies

#### 3. Business Price Rules
- Automatically sync business pricing and quantity discounts with standard price changes
- For sellers using Amazon Business (B2B)

### Setup Process
1. Log into Seller Central (Professional account required)
2. Go to **Pricing > Automate Pricing**
3. Create a new rule or select predefined parameters
4. Select products (individual SKUs or bulk upload)
5. Set minimum and maximum price limits
6. Activate the rule

### Key Details
- **Processing time**: Usually under 15 minutes; up to 1 hour for new rules
- **Cost**: Free with Professional plan
- **Price update limits**: Automated changes do NOT count toward your daily manual price update allotment
- **Featured Offer guarantee**: No -- pricing alone is insufficient; you must also meet inventory, fulfillment, and service requirements
- **30-day history**: Monitor price changes and their impact on Featured Offer percentage

### Best Practices
- Always set a **minimum price** to protect your profit margin
- Use the **Featured Offer** rule to maximize Buy Box wins
- Monitor the 30-day history to verify the rule is working as intended
- Combine with advertising for maximum sales velocity
- Adjust rules seasonally for peak/off-peak periods

## Promotions, Deals, and Coupons

### Promotions

Amazon offers three promotion formats:

#### Percentage-Off Promotions
- Tiered discounts (e.g., 10% off 1 item, 15% off 2 items)
- Applied automatically at checkout
- Can drive multi-unit purchases

#### Buy One Get One (BOGO)
- Customizable (e.g., buy 3, get 1 free)
- Applied automatically at checkout
- Effective for consumable products

#### Social Media Promo Codes
- Custom codes for sharing via social channels and influencer marketing
- One-time use or multi-use codes
- Useful for external traffic campaigns

**How to create**: Seller Central > Advertising > Promotions

### Deals

#### Lightning Deals
- Limited-time promotional offers (4-12 hours, duration set by Amazon)
- Featured on the Amazon Deals page
- Products receive a special deal badge
- **Requirements**: Professional plan, minimum 4-star product rating, eligible products only
- **Cost**: Fee per Lightning Deal (varies by time period; higher during peak events like Prime Day)
- **Application**: Submit through Seller Central > Advertising > Deals; Amazon approves/rejects

#### Best Deals
- Run for a select number of days (typically 1-2 weeks)
- Featured on the Amazon Deals page
- Special deal badge on the listing
- **Requirements**: Professional plan, eligible products, competitive pricing
- **Cost**: Fee may apply depending on the deal type

### Coupons

- Offer percentage off or fixed dollar amount
- Can apply to single or multiple products (up to 200 ASINs)
- Displayed on product pages, Amazon Deals pages, and search results
- Buyers "clip" the coupon to apply the discount
- **Eligibility**: Professional plan, minimum 3.5-star product rating
- **Duration**: Up to 90 days maximum
- **Cost**: Redemption fee charged **per coupon use** (currently $0.60 per redemption)
- **Creation**: Seller Central > Advertising > Coupons

### Discounts (Price Discounts)

- Time-bound campaigns (up to 30 days)
- Limited unit quantities available
- Can target Prime customers only or all shoppers
- Display strike-through pricing in search and on product pages
- **Creation**: Access through the Price Discounts dashboard in Seller Central

### Promotion Best Practices
- Use fixed dollar amounts for expensive items (sounds more impactful)
- Set coupon durations of 2-3 months for sustained visibility
- Avoid discount stacking with existing active promotions
- Plan ahead for peak shopping periods (Prime Day, Black Friday, holiday season)
- Calculate total cost including redemption fees before running
- Monitor promotion performance and adjust strategies based on ROI

## Pricing Policies

### Fair Pricing Policy
- Amazon monitors pricing and may remove the Buy Box or suppress listings for prices deemed unfairly high
- Factors considered: recent pricing history, comparison to other retailers, comparison to other Amazon sellers
- Price gouging during emergencies or high-demand events can result in account suspension
- Prices should reflect fair market value

### Price Parity
- Amazon's current policy does not require price parity with other sales channels (this was changed from earlier policies)
- However, consistently higher prices on Amazon vs. your other channels may affect Buy Box eligibility
- Competitive pricing remains important for Buy Box wins regardless of policy requirements

### MAP (Minimum Advertised Price) and MSRP
- Amazon does not enforce MAP policies set by manufacturers
- Sellers are free to price below MAP on Amazon
- However, some brands restrict Amazon selling to authorized sellers who agree to MAP
- MSRP can be entered for reference pricing (strike-through price) but must be a genuine MSRP

## Fee Optimization Strategies

### Reducing Referral Fees
- Price tiered-fee products strategically (e.g., baby products: 8% under $10 vs. 15% over $10)
- Consider bundling to change the effective fee rate
- Check if your product fits a lower-fee category without misclassifying

### Reducing FBA Fees
- Optimize packaging to reduce dimensional weight (smaller = cheaper)
- Use Low-Price FBA rates for qualifying products
- Enroll new ASINs in the FBA New Selection program for fee rebates
- Avoid aged inventory surcharges by managing stock levels
- Remove slow-moving inventory before long-term storage fees hit

### Reducing Storage Fees
- Use just-in-time inventory replenishment
- Clear excess inventory before peak season (Oct-Dec) when rates triple
- Create removal orders for products approaching 181 days
- Consider liquidation for products approaching 365 days
- Monitor Inventory Performance Index (IPI) to maintain storage limits

### Maximizing Profitability
- Use the Revenue Calculator before sourcing any product
- Factor in ALL fees: referral + FBA fulfillment + storage + advertising + returns
- Target products with healthy margins after all fees (30%+ margin recommended)
- Review fee changes annually (Amazon typically updates fees in January/February)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Professional plan** ($39.99/month) is essential if you sell 40+ items or want Buy Box/advertising access.
2. **Referral fees** range from 5-45% depending on category; most categories are 15%.
3. **Media items** incur an additional $1.80 closing fee per sale.
4. **FBA fees** are separate from referral fees and based on product size/weight.
5. **Automate Pricing** is free with Professional plan and runs 24/7.
6. **Coupons** cost $0.60 per redemption on top of the discount amount.
7. **Lightning Deals** have a fee per deal and require product eligibility (4-star minimum).
8. Use the **Revenue Calculator** to estimate profitability before committing to any product.
9. Review fee schedules annually -- Amazon updates them regularly.
10. Optimize packaging dimensions to reduce FBA fees.
