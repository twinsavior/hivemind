# TOS Compliance and Documentation Guide

## Protecting Your Amazon Accounts and Business

Compliance is not optional in A2A arbitrage. A single violation can result in account suspension, loss of inventory, and forfeiture of pending payments. This guide covers every compliance rule, documentation requirement, and account protection strategy you need.

---

## The Prime Rule: Never Use Prime for Sourcing

### Why This Is Rule Number One

Amazon Prime membership is a consumer benefit program. Using Prime shipping discounts to source inventory for resale violates Amazon's Terms of Service. Amazon can detect this and it is the single most common reason A2A sellers get both their buying and selling accounts suspended.

### How Amazon Detects Prime Sourcing

| Detection Method | How It Works |
|-----------------|--------------|
| **Receipt analysis** | When you provide invoices for inauthentic claims, Prime orders show "Prime" shipping on the receipt |
| **Account linking** | Amazon can link your buyer and seller accounts through shared payment methods, addresses, IP addresses, device fingerprints, and browser cookies |
| **Order pattern analysis** | Prime accounts placing bulk orders for items that then appear as FBA inventory raise algorithmic flags |
| **Return address matching** | Your shipping address on Prime orders may match your FBA seller return address |

### The Correct Account Structure

| Account | Purpose | Prime Status | Payment |
|---------|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Personal buying account** | Regular personal Amazon shopping | Can have Prime (for personal use only) |  Personal credit card |
| **Sourcing buying account** | All A2A inventory purchases | **NO PRIME — ever** | Separate credit card or business card |
| **Seller account (Seller Central)** | Listing and selling inventory | N/A (Seller Central) | Business bank account |

### Account Separation Best Practices

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Email addresses** | Different email for each account |
| **Payment methods** | Different credit/debit cards for buying account vs. personal account |
| **Physical addresses** | Ideally different (use business address for sourcing account if available) |
| **Browsers** | Use different browsers or browser profiles for each account |
| **IP addresses** | Using the same home IP is generally fine, but avoid logging into both accounts in the same browser session |
| **Phone numbers** | Different phone numbers if possible; same is acceptable if other factors differ |
| **Device** | Log in to sourcing account from a consistent device; do not switch between devices frequently |

## Amazon Business Account for Sourcing

### Benefits

| Benefit | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Business-only pricing** | Some products have exclusive business pricing that increases margins |
| **Quantity discounts** | Tiered pricing on bulk orders |
| **Tax-exempt purchasing** | With proper tax exemption documentation |
| **Business analytics** | Better order tracking and reporting |
| **No Prime temptation** | Business accounts do not include Prime by default |

### Risks

| Risk | Details |
|------|---------|
| **High-dollar purchase scrutiny** | Amazon monitors Business accounts for unusual purchasing patterns |
| **Potential suspension** | Rapid high-volume purchases can trigger review |
| **Linkage concerns** | Amazon may link Business account to your Seller account |
| **Spending velocity flags** | Sudden spending increases (e.g., $500/month jumping to $5,000/month) draw attention |

### Risk Mitigation for Business Account

1. Ramp up spending gradually (increase 20-30% per month maximum)
2. Maintain a consistent ordering cadence (do not go from 2 orders/week to 20)
3. Order from varied categories (do not bulk-buy a single ASIN repeatedly)
4. Keep order quantities reasonable (5-15 units per order, not 50+)
5. Maintain a clean return rate (under 5% of orders)

## Record Keeping Requirements

### Why Documentation Is Critical

When Amazon receives an inauthentic complaint on a product you are selling, they will ask you to provide documentation proving the authenticity and source of your inventory. For A2A, your proof is the original Amazon purchase invoice. Without it, you cannot defend your listing and may face suspension.

### What to Save for Every Purchase

| Document | How to Obtain | Where to Store |
|----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Order confirmation email** | Sent to your buying account email after purchase | Email folder + local backup |
| **Order invoice/receipt** | Download from Amazon > Your Orders > Invoice | Dedicated folder per month |
| **Packing slip** | Included in the shipped package | Scan and save digitally |
| **Screenshot of product listing at purchase** | Take screenshot showing price, ASIN, condition | Save with order ID in filename |
| **Screenshot of Keepa chart at purchase** | Shows the price drop that motivated the purchase | Save for post-mortem reference |
| **Credit card statement** | Monthly statement from your sourcing card | Auto-saved by bank |
| **FBA shipment records** | Seller Central > Inventory > Manage FBA Shipments | Accessible in Seller Central |

### Invoice Requirements for Inauthentic Claims

When Amazon requests invoices, they must meet these criteria:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Supplier name** | The invoice must show who sold you the product (in A2A, this is Amazon.com or Amazon Retail) |
| **Your name or business name** | The buyer name must match your seller account name or business entity |
| **Invoice date** | Must be within the last 365 days (ideally within 180 days) |
| **Product description** | Must match the ASIN or product you are selling |
| **Quantities** | Must match or exceed the quantity you listed for sale |
| **Purchase price** | Must be visible (shows you paid for the items) |
| **Supplier contact information** | Amazon.com's address and contact info are on their invoices |

### How to Download Amazon Invoices

1. Log in to your sourcing buying account
2. Go to Your Orders
3. Find the relevant order
4. Click "Invoice" in the order details
5. Download as PDF
6. Save with filename format: `ASIN_OrderID_Date.pdf`

## Fair Pricing Policy

### Amazon's Fair Pricing Policy

Amazon monitors seller pricing and may suppress or remove listings that are deemed unfairly priced. This is separate from price gouging laws and applies to all sellers.

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| **No excessive pricing** | Do not price significantly above the recent historical average |
| **No misleading reference prices** | List price and sale price must reflect actual market value |
| **No price gouging during emergencies** | Special scrutiny during disasters, pandemics, or shortages |
| **Market-rate pricing** | Your price should be within the range of other current sellers |

### Safe Pricing Guidelines for A2A

| Scenario | Pricing Strategy |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Reactive flip (price recovering)** | Price at or slightly below the 90-day average price |
| **Selling during OOS period (restock pillar)** | Price at the current 3P market rate — match existing sellers |
| **Seasonal peak** | Price at seasonal peak levels shown in Keepa history |
| **Debundled units** | Price at or near the current Buy Box for the individual listing |

### What to Avoid

- Pricing 2x or more above the product's 90-day average (can trigger fair pricing review)
- Being the only seller at a drastically higher price (attracts scrutiny)
- Rapid price increases on essential/health products (Amazon is especially vigilant here)

## Drop Shipping Prohibition

### The Rule

Amazon requires that you take physical possession of the product before reselling it. You must:

1. Receive the product at your location
2. Inspect it for condition and accuracy
3. Prep and label it for FBA
4. Ship it to an Amazon fulfillment center

### What Constitutes Drop Shipping (Prohibited)

| Activity | Allowed? |
|----------|----------|
| Buying on Amazon and having it shipped to an FBA warehouse directly | **No** — this is drop shipping |
| Buying on Amazon and having it shipped to you, then sending to FBA | **Yes** — you took possession |
| Buying on Amazon and having it shipped to a prep center, then to FBA | **Yes** — prep center counts as taking possession (you or your agent) |
| Using Amazon "ship to multiple addresses" to send to FBA | **No** — this is drop shipping |

## Return Policy Compliance

### Buyer Account Returns

Amazon monitors your buyer account return rate. Excessive returns on your sourcing account can lead to:

| Consequence | Trigger |
|-------------|---------|
| **Return warnings** | Return rate exceeds 10% of orders |
| **Account review** | Repeated high-value returns or suspicious patterns |
| **Account suspension** | Persistent excessive returns |
| **Refund restrictions** | Amazon may issue gift card refunds instead of credit card refunds |

### Guidelines for Returns on Your Buying Account

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Keep returns rare** | Only return genuinely defective or incorrect items |
| **Never return unsold inventory** | Do not buy A2A inventory and then return it to Amazon as a buyer if it doesn't sell |
| **Legitimate returns only** | Returns for actual defects or wrong items received are fine |
| **Target return rate** | Keep under 5% of total orders on your buying account |
| **Space out returns** | Do not return multiple items in the same week |

## IP and Brand Gating

### Understanding Gating

Some brands and categories require Amazon approval before you can list products. Selling gated products without approval results in listing removal and potential account suspension.

### How to Check for Gating

1. Go to Seller Central > Catalog > Add a Product
2. Search for the ASIN
3. If you see "Apply to sell" or "Approval required," the product is gated
4. If you see "List this product," you are approved

### Maintaining Gating Approvals

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Save approval confirmation** | Screenshot or save the email confirming your approval |
| **Monitor for changes** | Brands can add gating at any time; check before purchasing |
| **Keep invoices for gated brands** | If questioned, you need to prove authorized sourcing |
| **Do not share approval status** | Your approvals are account-specific |

### Common Gated Categories in A2A

| Category/Brand Type | Gating Likelihood | Notes |
|--------------------|-------------------|-------|
| Major electronics brands | High | Apple, Samsung, Sony, etc. |
| Premium beauty brands | High | Prestige skincare, cosmetics |
| Grocery (consumables) | Medium-High | Many brands now gated |
| Health supplements | Medium | Especially popular brands |
| Toys (major brands) | Medium | Seasonal gating increases in Q4 |
| Generic household items | Low | Usually open |

## Handling Inauthentic Complaints

### What Is an Inauthentic Complaint?

An inauthentic complaint is when a brand owner, rights holder, or Amazon's automated systems flag your listing as potentially selling counterfeit or unauthorized products. This is the most common compliance issue in A2A.

### Step-by-Step Response Process

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | **Read the complaint carefully** | Immediately upon notification |
| 2 | **Identify the ASIN and the complaint source** | Same day |
| 3 | **Locate your purchase invoice for the product** | Same day (this is why record-keeping matters) |
| 4 | **Prepare your Plan of Action (POA)** | Within 24 hours |
| 5 | **Submit the POA with supporting documentation** | Within 48 hours of complaint |
| 6 | **Follow up if no response within 72 hours** | Day 5 |

### Plan of Action Template for A2A Inauthentic Claims

Your POA should include:

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Root cause** | Explain that the product was purchased directly from Amazon.com as a retail customer. The item is genuine, sourced from Amazon's own marketplace. |
| **Corrective action** | You have reviewed the complaint, verified the product's authenticity via your Amazon purchase records, and are providing the invoice as proof. |
| **Preventive measures** | You maintain detailed records of all purchases including invoices, order confirmations, and receipts. You source only from authorized retailers (Amazon.com). |
| **Supporting documents** | Attach: Amazon purchase invoice, order confirmation, packing slip (if available) |

### Key Points When Responding

- **Be professional and concise** — Amazon reviewers handle thousands of appeals
- **Provide the invoice prominently** — This is your strongest evidence
- **Do not admit wrongdoing** — State facts; the product is genuine and purchased from Amazon
- **Do not mention "arbitrage" or "reselling"** — Simply state you are a retail purchaser
- **Respond quickly** — Faster responses generally get faster resolutions

## Order Volume and Velocity Limits

### Buying Account Volume Guidelines

| Factor | Safe Range | Caution Zone | Danger Zone |
|--------|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Orders per day** | 1-5 | 6-10 | 10+ |
| **Units per order** | 1-10 | 11-20 | 20+ of same item |
| **Spending per week** | Under $500 | $500-$2,000 | $2,000+ |
| **Spending ramp rate** | 10-20% increase per month | 30-50% increase | Doubling or more |
| **Same ASIN purchases per week** | 1-2 orders | 3-4 orders | 5+ orders of same item |

### Strategies to Stay Within Safe Limits

1. **Spread purchases across days** — Do not place all orders on Monday
2. **Vary the products** — Do not buy 20 units of the same product in one order
3. **Use multiple payment methods** — Rotating 2-3 credit cards looks more natural
4. **Keep order sizes reasonable** — 5-10 units is a normal consumer range
5. **Ramp gradually** — Start small and increase volume over weeks and months

## Account Suspension: Prevention and Recovery

### Suspension Warning Signs

| Warning Sign | What It Means | Action |
|-------------|---------------|--------|
| **Performance notification in Seller Central** | Amazon flagged an issue | Address immediately |
| **Listing deactivated** | Specific ASIN has a complaint | Provide documentation for that ASIN |
| **Request for invoices** | Amazon wants proof of authenticity | Submit within 48 hours |
| **Buyer account warning** | Unusual activity on sourcing account | Reduce volume, review recent orders |
| **Sudden payment hold** | Amazon is reviewing your account | Do not make major changes; wait for communication |

### If Suspended: Immediate Steps

1. **Do not panic or make impulsive changes**
2. **Read the suspension notice carefully** — understand exactly what is alleged
3. **Gather all relevant documentation** — invoices, order confirmations, tracking numbers
4. **Draft a Plan of Action** — address root cause, corrective actions, prevention
5. **Submit through the proper channel** (Seller Central > Account Health > Appeal)
6. **Do not submit multiple appeals** — one well-crafted appeal is better than five rushed ones
7. **Consider professional help** — if significant revenue is at stake, suspension appeal services exist
