# Walmart Marketplace Brand Management Guide

## Overview

The Walmart Brand Portal is a centralized platform for verified brand owners to protect their intellectual property, manage brand privileges, report policy violations, and control how their products appear on Walmart.com. Registration is required for accessing premium advertising features (Sponsored Brands, Sponsored Videos, Onsite Display) and advanced brand content tools.

---

## Brand Portal Overview

### Purpose
- Streamline intellectual property infringement reporting
- Enable trademark owners to manage brand rights on Walmart.com and Marketplace
- Provide tools for brand content management
- Grant access to premium advertising formats
- Protect brands from counterfeit and unauthorized sellers

### Access
- Available at the Brand Portal section of Walmart Marketplace
- Requires active trademark registration and verification
- Dashboard provides centralized management of all brand activities

---

## Brand Registration Process

### Eligibility Requirements

**Trademark Requirements**
- Must be a trademark or rights owner registered with the United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO)
- Trademark registration must have a 7-digit registration number
- Trademark must be in active status
- Third-party sellers and brand protection agencies cannot apply

**Geographic Requirements**
Applicants must be based in one of the following countries:
- United States
- United Kingdom
- Canada
- China
- Hong Kong
- India
- South Korea
- Vietnam

### Registration Steps
1. Navigate to the Brand Portal section of Walmart Marketplace
2. Submit trademark documentation (7-digit USPTO registration number)
3. Walmart verifies trademark ownership and active status
4. Upon approval, brand owner gains access to Brand Portal features
5. Application status is trackable within the Brand Portal dashboard

### Multiple Brands
- Sellers can register multiple brands under the same Brand Portal account
- Each brand requires valid and current trademark documentation
- Separate trademark verification is required for each brand

## Managing Brand Privileges

### Role Types

**Acting Brand Owner**
- Can update item content across all sellers listing the brand's products
- Can establish branded shops (Brand Shop and Shelf)
- Only one acting brand owner allowed per brand
- Has the highest level of content control

**Authorized Resellers**
- Can modify product content except where acting brand owners have set restrictions
- Multiple authorized resellers are permitted per brand
- Content updates are subject to acting brand owner authority

### Team Management
Brand Portal accounts support tiered permissions:
- **Super Admin**: Full access to all Brand Portal features and team management
- **Admin**: Access to most features with some restrictions
- **Reporter**: Limited access, primarily for viewing and reporting

### Content Management
- Upload and manage product content across all listings
- Control product titles, descriptions, images, and attributes
- Override content submitted by individual sellers (as acting brand owner)
- Access analytics showing unpublished listings and compliance metrics

### Trademark Portfolio Management
- Upload additional trademarks that you own through the trademark registration section
- Manage multiple registered trademarks from a single dashboard
- Track registration status for each trademark

## IP Protection

### Claim Types
Brand Portal users can submit claims for violations related to:
- **Trademark infringement**: Unauthorized use of registered trademarks
- **Copyright infringement**: Unauthorized reproduction of copyrighted materials
- **Patent infringement**: Products violating registered patents
- **Counterfeit goods**: Fake or imitation products sold under the brand name

### Submitting Claims
1. Log into the Brand Portal
2. Navigate to the IP Claims section
3. Submit a claim identifying the specific listing(s) and violation type
4. Provide supporting documentation
5. Track claim status through the claims dashboard

### Claims Dashboard
- View all submitted IP claims in one place
- Monitor claim status: resolved, pending, or rejected
- Track how Walmart is enforcing protections for your brand
- Access historical claim data

### Alternative Reporting
- **Public IP Webform**: Available for non-Brand Portal account holders to report IP violations
- **IP Claim Form**: Can be used if you believe a counterfeit product is being sold on Walmart.com
- Email-based reporting available through Walmart's IP team

---

## Counterfeit Reporting

### How to Report
If you believe a counterfeit of your product is being sold on Walmart.com:
1. Use the Brand Portal claims submission process (preferred)
2. Use the IP Claim Form on Walmart's website
3. Provide evidence of authenticity and the infringing listing

### Walmart's Response
- Walmart reserves the right to remove any content it believes to be counterfeit
- Additional measures may be taken as deemed appropriate
- Repeat offenders may face account suspension or termination
- Walmart handles counterfeit claims with high priority

### Prevention
- Register your brand in the Brand Portal as early as possible
- Monitor listings regularly for unauthorized sellers
- Report violations promptly to minimize customer impact
- Work with Walmart support for recurring infringement issues

## Brand Content Tools

### Brand Shop and Shelf
- Available to acting brand owners
- Create a branded shopping experience within Walmart.com
- Showcase your product line in a curated storefront
- Available through Onsite Display advertising program

### Product Content Control
- As acting brand owner, override content across all sellers' listings
- Ensure consistent brand messaging and accurate product information
- Control product images, titles, descriptions, and key attributes
- Prevent unauthorized modifications to brand content

### Analytics and Insights
- View compliance metrics across your brand's listings
- Identify unpublished listings and resolve issues
- Track brand performance across the Marketplace
- Access data on how your brand content is performing

## Connection to Advertising

### Advertising Access Through Brand Portal
Brand Portal registration unlocks premium advertising formats:

| Ad Format | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Sponsored Brands | Brand Portal enrollment + USPTO registration |
| Sponsored Videos | Brand Portal enrollment + USPTO registration |
| Onsite Display | Brand Portal enrollment |

### Brand Advertising Benefits
- Sponsored Brands appear at the top of search results with your logo
- Sponsored Videos showcase products with motion and sound
- Onsite Display enables audience-targeted visual advertising
- Brand Shop provides a dedicated storefront for your brand

## Support and Resources

### Brand Help Portal
- Dedicated support portal for Brand Portal account holders
- Assistance with registration, claims, and content management issues
- Available through the Brand Portal dashboard

### Support Contacts
- For IP infringement: Use the Brand Portal claims process
- For registration issues: Contact Brand Help through the portal
- For non-account holders: Use the public IP webform
- For identity verification consent withdrawal: Email globalprivacy@wal-mart.com

---

## Key Reminders

1. Only USPTO-registered trademark owners can register (no third-party agents)
2. Trademark must have a 7-digit registration number and be in active status
3. Only one acting brand owner is allowed per brand; multiple authorized resellers are permitted
4. Brand Portal enrollment is required for Sponsored Brands, Sponsored Videos, and Onsite Display ads
5. IP claims cover trademark, copyright, patent, and counterfeit violations
6. Monitor listings regularly and report violations promptly
7. Team permissions are tiered: Super Admin, Admin, and Reporter
8. Multiple brands can be registered under a single Brand Portal account
