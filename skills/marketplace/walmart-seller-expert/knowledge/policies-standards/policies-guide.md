# Walmart Marketplace Policies & Standards Guide

## Overview

Walmart Marketplace sellers must comply with a comprehensive set of policies covering product eligibility, listing quality, pricing, performance standards, and seller conduct. Non-compliance can result in listing suppression, account suspension, or termination. This guide covers all major policy areas sellers need to understand.

## Prohibited Products Policy

### Core Requirements
- All products must comply with applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations
- All product labels, information, and images in a Walmart.com listing must be in English
- Product information cannot contain conflicting details
- Sellers must provide safety documentation for children's and regulated consumer products

### Prohibited Activities
- Selling stolen goods or property with violated rights
- Purchasing from competitors and using their delivery services for fulfillment
- Reselling items from other online marketplaces without proper licensing
- Offering products incompatible with Walmart's brand

### Territorial Restrictions
Products cannot originate from or contain materials sourced through:
- Iran, Cuba, North Korea
- Sanctioned Ukrainian regions (Luhansk, Donetsk, Crimea)
- Forced labor operations
- Entities prohibited under U.S. import regulations

### Prohibited Product Categories
Major restricted categories include:
- Alcohol
- Animals and animal products
- Auto parts (certain types)
- Cosmetics (certain formulations)
- Dietary supplements (require pre-approval)
- Drugs and drug paraphernalia
- Electronics (certain types)
- Food products (certain categories)
- Hazardous items and materials
- Jewelry and precious metals (certain types)
- Medical devices
- Offensive content
- Pet products (certain types)
- Plants and seeds
- Recalled items
- Firearms, weapons, and ammunition
- Tobacco and tobacco products

### Pre-Approval Required Categories
The following require Walmart approval before listing:
- Books
- Ingestible and topical products
- Over-the-counter (OTC) drugs
- Fragrances
- Luxury brands
- Software
- Covered goods subject to FinCEN regulations

### Enforcement
- Walmart will remove any product listing violating these policies
- Account suspension or termination may result from non-compliance
- Prohibited inventory in Walmart's custody may be returned, destroyed, or disposed of at seller's expense

## Listing Quality Standards

### Item Condition Requirements
- **New items**: Must be in pristine condition; packaging must meet manufacturer requirements
- **Restored/Pre-Owned/Open Box/Remanufactured**: Only permitted if you have been invited to participate in the Resold program
- Items must meet all condition, category, quality, and disclosure requirements
- Misrepresentation of condition is prohibited

### Content Standards
- Product titles, descriptions, and attributes must be accurate and complete
- Images must be high-quality and meet Walmart's image guidelines
- Required attributes must be filled in for each category
- Content must not contain offensive terms, competitor references, or misleading information

### Product Identifiers
- Valid GTIN, UPC, ISBN, or EAN required for most products
- Incorrect or missing product IDs will result in listing errors
- GTIN exemptions available for private label or handmade items (must be requested)

### Duplicate Listings Policy
- Creating duplicate listings for the same product is prohibited
- Walmart may merge or remove duplicate listings
- Sellers should search for existing listings before creating new ones

## Pricing Rules

### General Pricing Standards
- Prices must be competitive and reasonable
- Raising retail prices after order completion is prohibited
- Creating highly uncompetitive or unfairly priced shipping is prohibited

### Price Protection Mechanisms
- Walmart may automatically unpublish offers priced erroneously too low to protect sellers from unintended losses
- Offers priced significantly above competitor prices or historical rates may also be unpublished
- When pricing adheres to the rules, Walmart automatically republishes the offer (typically within 48 hours)

### Shipping Price Standards
- Egregious shipping costs relative to item price will result in unpublishing
- Sellers must update shipping template fees to align with market conditions
- When shipping fees fall within an acceptable range, Walmart automatically republishes

### Comparison Pricing
- Comparison pricing (showing a "was" price vs. current price) must be accurate
- Inflated comparison prices are prohibited

## Seller Performance Standards

### Key Metrics and Thresholds

| Metric | Threshold | Evaluation Period |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Cancellation Rate | Less than or equal to 2% | 30-60 days |
| On-Time Delivery Rate | Greater than or equal to 90% | 30-60 days |
| Valid Tracking Rate | Greater than or equal to 99% | 30-60 days |
| Refund Rate | Less than or equal to 6% | 30-60 days |
| Seller Response Rate | Greater than or equal to 95% | 30-60 days |
| Negative Feedback Rate | Less than or equal to 2% | 60 days (effective spring 2026) |
| Return Rate | Less than or equal to 6% | 60 days (effective spring 2026) |
| Item Not Received Rate | Less than or equal to 2% | (effective spring 2026) |

### Metric Details

**Cancellation Rate**
- Seller-accountable factors: out-of-stock, pricing errors, missed ship dates
- Not accountable: customer-requested cancellations, fraud

**On-Time Delivery Rate**
- Measures orders delivered by Expected Delivery Date
- Seller controls: carrier selection, shipment timing, lag time accuracy
- Uncontrollable: weather delays, carrier exceptions

**Valid Tracking Rate**
- Requires accurate tracking info shared after carrier pickup
- Violations include invalid IDs, misleading tracking, non-integrated carriers

**Refund Rate**
- Seller-responsible refunds for damaged or incorrect items
- Accountable causes: defects, wrong items, damage, late arrival

**Seller Response Rate**
- Must respond to inquiries within 48 hours
- Covers order tracking, cancellations, feedback, returns

**Negative Feedback Rate**
- Measures one-star or two-star ratings from the past 60 days
- Drivers: defects, quality concerns, incorrect descriptions

### WFS Impact on Performance
- If using Walmart Fulfillment Services, most performance metrics are handled by WFS
- Exception: Negative Feedback Rate remains the seller's responsibility even with WFS

### Grace Period
- New sellers will not face potential suspension until their marketplace account has been live for 120 days

## Consequences for Non-Compliance

### Progressive Enforcement

**Step 1: Warning**
- Email or Seller Center notification identifying which metrics are below standards
- Recommended corrective actions provided

**Step 2: Listing Suppression**
- If metrics do not improve after warnings, product listings may be suppressed
- Seller-fulfilled listings are removed from Walmart.com
- WFS listings remain active during suppression

**Step 3: Suspension**
- Both seller-fulfilled and WFS listings are removed
- Account privileges are restricted
- Appealable with a business improvement plan

**Step 4: Termination**
- Complete removal from the Marketplace program
- Appeals of termination are not accepted
- Walmart is not required to provide a reason for termination

### Notifications
- Performance notifications arrive via email and Seller Center
- Sellers cannot unsubscribe from these communications

## Account Suspension Appeals

### Eligible for Appeal
- Suppressions and suspensions are eligible for appeal
- Terminations are NOT eligible for appeal

### Appeal Process

**Step 1: Review Performance**
- Access the Performance page in Seller Center
- Analyze which metrics failed and why

**Step 2: Develop a Business Plan**
Create a written plan addressing:
- Description of the violation
- Corrective steps you will take
- Prevention measures for future policy violations

Documentation may be required:
- Current warehouse images
- Distributor or supplier invoices (under 2 months old)
- Intellectual property documents

**Step 3: Submit Appeal**
- For suppression: Select the "Start appeal" banner on the Performance page
- For suspension: Use the Help button in Seller Center to contact support

### Important Notes
- Appeals are handled in the order received
- Failure to provide required documentation may result in denial
- Reinstatement does not guarantee future compliance -- continued poor performance may result in additional suspension or termination

## Seller Code of Conduct

### Fair Competition and Integrity
- No agreements that restrict competition
- No sharing of competitively sensitive information (pricing, promotions, inventory plans) with other sellers

### Respectful Treatment
- Treat customers, other sellers, and Walmart associates with respect
- No sharing of customer information with third parties
- No initiating unsolicited contact with customers
- No misusing personal data for unauthorized communications
- No providing inaccurate item information or inflating customer reviews

### Accurate Information Requirements
- Display names: 3-30 characters
- Company descriptions: 20-1,000 characters
- Cannot include offensive terms, duplicates, PII, competitor references, emojis, special characters, or variations of "Walmart"
- Must match marketplace language (English for U.S.)
- Display name changes allowed once every 90 days

### Customer Diversion Prohibition
- Any attempt to direct customers to external websites, platforms, or storefronts is prohibited
- This includes hyperlinks and social media references in communications or fulfillment materials

### Multiple Seller Accounts
- Operating multiple seller accounts is prohibited without Walmart approval
- Each business entity should have one Marketplace account

## Seller Privacy Policy

### Data Walmart Collects from Sellers
- Core identifiers: name, phone, address, email, government-issued identifiers
- Digital information: device identifiers, IP addresses, cookies, browsing activity
- Financial data: bank account details, credit scores, transaction histories
- Biometric data: fingerprints, facial geometry (for identity verification only, with written consent)
- Employment and background information

### How Data Is Used
- Seller onboarding and risk assessment (including sanctions list screening)
- Account creation and transaction management
- Fraud prevention and identity verification
- Payment processing and settlement
- Tax calculations and regulatory reporting
- Marketing and targeted advertising
- Credit eligibility for merchant cash advances

### Data Sharing
- Internal sharing across Walmart's corporate family
- External vendors for onboarding, payments, hosting, analytics, fraud detection
- Advertising partners, research consultants, auditors, banks, credit agencies
- Law enforcement when legally required

### Data Retention
- Information retained for as long as reasonably necessary
- Biometric data retained until initial purpose is satisfied or within 3 years of last interaction, whichever comes first

### Seller Rights (California)
- Right to request data access, correction, deletion
- Right to opt out of targeted advertising sales
- Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal is honored

## Buy Box Eligibility

### Factors Affecting Buy Box
- Price competitiveness (item price plus shipping)
- Seller performance metrics (all metrics above)
- Fulfillment method (WFS items may have an advantage)
- Shipping speed
- Inventory availability
- Listing quality

### Impact of Poor Performance
- Below-standard metrics can result in Buy Box removal
- Maintaining an Order Defect Rate (ODR) below 2% is critical for Buy Box eligibility

---

## Performance Dashboard

### Monitoring Tools
- **Performance Dashboard**: Available in Seller Center for real-time metrics review
- **Walmart Seller App**: Mobile access to performance data
- **Seller Performance APIs**: Automated metric tracking
- **Seller Center Inbox**: Policy notifications and warnings

### Recommended Practices
- Review metrics regularly (at least weekly)
- Set up alerts for metrics approaching thresholds
- Investigate and resolve issues promptly
- Document improvement actions for potential appeals

---

## Key Reminders

1. New sellers have a 120-day grace period before suspension risk applies
2. Terminations cannot be appealed -- suspensions and suppressions can
3. WFS handles most performance metrics except Negative Feedback Rate
4. Display name changes are limited to once every 90 days
5. Never direct customers to external websites or platforms
6. Keep all performance metrics within thresholds to maintain Buy Box eligibility
7. Monitor the Performance Dashboard regularly and address warnings promptly
